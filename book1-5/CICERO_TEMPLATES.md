# Cicero Templates (Author Form)

Use these when drafting or revising reader-facing prose. Attackers do not write in this form; they only force you to improve it.

## Default chapter form (8 steps)

1. **Scene** (2–8 sentences) — concrete situation.
2. **Principle** — one clear sentence.
3. **Partitio** — “Three points…” (map).
4. **Nature** — why reality / human limits force this.
5. **Exemplum** — one fully told case beats three named.
6. **False twin** — one sharp opposite (not a catalogue).
7. **Hard case** — where the rule hurts or conflicts.
8. **Charge** — what to do or refuse.

If a revision only lengthens step 6’s catalogue, cut it.

## Thin exterior (one thesis-family)

For constructivism, pure outcome-equity slogans, etc.

1. Scene where truth must decide action  
2. Strongest short form of rival  
3. Competing principles named  
4. Institutional wreck  
5. Positive joint restated  
6. Hard case + fence (what they get partly right)  
7. Charge  
8. **Stop**

Target: ~800–1500 words.

## Thick exterior (multi-package tradition)

For `muslim_package` and any large living tradition:

1. Institutional scene  
2. **Disaggregate** 2–4 principle packages  
3. Score each on the five principles  
4. One primary-source spine per inverted package  
5. Mechanism of conflict  
6. False twin *inside* the author’s camp  
7. Positive reconstruction (what a people does Monday)  
8. Persons ≠ packages fence  
9. **Stop** if heat rises without new mechanism  

Scripturalism (`scripturalist`) is usually **thin exterior epistemology**, not thick civilizational package—use the thin template unless the chapter deliberately compares churches.

## Positive ratio heuristic

In a diff for an attack cycle:

- New **scene / definition / exemplum / office / hard case** lines  
  should meet or exceed  
- New **rebuttal scaffolding** lines  

If not, the forge is overheating.
