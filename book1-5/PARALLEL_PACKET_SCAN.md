## The construct

**Packet stack (bad default):** each item carries its own job clause before the next item starts.

```text
the trade that would keep the city fed,
the skills the schools would have to form,
the order that could hold a multi-racial city together,
and the neighbors who would not wait for better feelings.
```

Reads as (A→A′, B→B′, C→C′, D→D′). The ear restarts four times.

**Preferred regroup (when stakes or consequences are the point):** name the set, then cash the stakes as a second list or as binary outcomes.

```text
He had to name trade, talent, order, and enemies.
The port would eat or go hungry; the schools would form real skills or empty credentials;
the city would hold or fracture; the neighbors would wait for no one.
```

Reads as ((A, B, C, D) → (A′, B′, C′, D′)).

### When *not* to force the transform

- **Two-item contrasts** that need tight pairing (*a people that persists and a people that files complaints*).
- **Deliberate compact principles** (*Health over habit, maintenance over neglect…*) where A→A′ *is* the mnemonic.
- **Single relative clause** on a long sentence (ordinary English, not a stack).
- **Legal or definitional “that”** (*the claim that unequal results…*).

## Scan method (for later passes)

1. Flag sequences of **2+** adjacent phrases of the form  
   `(a|an|the)? NOUN-PHRASE (that|who|which) CLAUSE` joined by commas or *and*.
2. Flag **3+** *X over Y* pairs only if the line also feels hard to parse (default: keep).
3. Prefer transform when the first half is **inventory / naming / cast of roles** and the second half is **stakes, jobs, or binary outcomes**.
4. After any meta-cut that produces bare telegrams, re-check the same loci for packet stacks (they often appear when “smoothing” lists).

## House preference (summary)

| Prefer | Avoid |
|--------|--------|
| ((A, B, C) → (A′, B′, C′)) when listing inventory then stakes | (A→A′, B→B′, C→C′) as the default long list |
| Name the cast, then what each must do or suffer | Four mini-essays in one sentence |
| Binary outcomes for hard worlds (*eat or go hungry*) | Nested “the X that would Y” chains |
