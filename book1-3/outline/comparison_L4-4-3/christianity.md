# Provenance regarding Christianity
Primary sources include the Old and New Testaments of the Bible (NASB). The conceptual framework emphasizes a Created Reality, a Fallen Human Nature, and a Redeemer who establishes moral and procedural symmetry.

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential
"In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth." (Genesis 1:1).
"For since the creation of the world His invisible attributes, His eternal power and divine nature, have been clearly seen, being understood through what has been made, so that they are without excuse." (Romans 1:20).
"The heavens are telling of the glory of God; And their expanse is declaring the work of His hands." (Psalm 19:1).
"Pilate said to Him, 'What is truth?'" (John 18:38) - posing the central question which Christianity answers with a Person and a Reality ("I am the way, and the truth, and the life." John 14:6).
"The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge." (Proverbs 1:7).
"Test all things; hold fast what is good." (1 Thessalonians 5:21).
"For we know in part and we prophesy in part, but when the perfect comes, the partial will pass away." (1 Corinthians 13:9-10).

## Humans are Limited and Selfish
"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." (Romans 3:23).
"The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately sick; who can understand it?" (Jeremiah 17:9).
"For I know that nothing good dwells in me, that is, in my flesh; for the willing is present in me, but the doing of the good is not." (Romans 7:18).
"But Jesus, on His part, was not entrusting Himself to them, for He knew all men, and because He did not need anyone to testify concerning man, for He Himself knew what was in man." (John 2:24-25).
"There is none righteous, not even one." (Romans 3:10).
"If we say that we have no sin, we are deceiving ourselves and the truth is not in us." (1 John 1:8).

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility
"But if anyone does not provide for his own, and especially for those of his household, he has denied the faith and is worse than an unbeliever." (1 Timothy 5:8).
"Each one must examine his own work, and then he will have reason for boasting in regard to himself alone, and not in regard to another. For each one will bear his own load." (Galatians 6:4-5).
"So then each one of us will give an account of himself to God." (Romans 14:12).
"Fathers, do not provoke your children to anger, but bring them up in the discipline and instruction of the Lord." (Ephesians 6:4).
"Honor your father and your mother, that your days may be prolonged in the land which the Lord your God gives you." (Exodus 20:12).
"Let him who stole steal no longer; but rather let him labor, performing with his own hands what is good, in order that he may have something to share with him who has need." (Ephesians 4:28).

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well
"God blessed them; and God said to them, 'Be fruitful and multiply, and fill the earth, and subdue it; and rule over the fish of the sea and over the birds of the sky and over every living thing that moves on the earth.'" (Genesis 1:28).
"The thief comes only to steal and kill and destroy; I came that they may have life, and have it abundantly." (John 10:10).
"Instruct those who are rich in this present world not to be conceited or to fix their hope on the uncertainty of riches, but on God, who richly supplies us with all things to enjoy." (1 Timothy 6:17).
"He causes the grass to grow for the cattle, And vegetation for the labor of man, So that he may bring forth food from the earth, And wine which makes man's heart glad, So that he may make his face glisten with oil, And food which sustains man's heart." (Psalm 104:14-15).
"Every good thing given and every perfect gift is from above." (James 1:17).

## Procedural Symmetry
"You shall do no injustice in judgment; you shall not be partial to the poor nor defer to the great, but you are to judge your neighbor fairly." (Leviticus 19:15).
"One law shall be for the native-born and for the stranger who sojourns among you." (Exodus 12:49).
"Treat people the same way you want them to treat you." (Matthew 7:12).
"There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free man, there is neither male nor female; for you are all one in Christ Jesus." (Galatians 3:28).
"Open your mouth, judge righteously, and defend the rights of the afflicted and needy." (Proverbs 31:9).
"God shows no partiality." (Acts 10:34).
"Do not show partiality in judgment; you shall hear the small and the great alike. You shall not fear man, for the judgment is God’s." (Deuteronomy 1:17).
"Wait for the Lord, and He will save you. Differing weights are an abomination to the Lord, And a false scale is not good." (Proverbs 20:22-23).

## Humility (The Criticism Test)
"Render to Caesar the things that are Caesar’s; and to God the things that are God’s." (Matthew 22:21).
"My kingdom is not of this world. If My kingdom were of this world, then My servants would be fighting." (John 18:36).
"Come now, and let us reason together." (Isaiah 1:18).
"But sanctify Christ as Lord in your hearts, always being ready to make a defense to everyone who asks you to give an account for the hope that is in you, yet with gentleness and reverence." (1 Peter 3:15).
"For the weapons of our warfare are not of the flesh." (2 Corinthians 10:4).
"If possible, so far as it depends on you, be at peace with all men." (Romans 12:18).
