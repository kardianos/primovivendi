# Provenance regarding Shinto
Primary sources include *The Kojiki* (Record of Ancient Matters), *The Nihon Shoki* (Chronicles of Japan), and Motoori Norinaga. Shinto emphasizes the divinity of nature (Kami) and purity of the community.

## 5-Principle Compatibility Summary

| Principle | Rating | Reason |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| P1 (Reality) | **Compatible** | Nature is manifestation of Kami; world is sacred territory, not illusion; creative power (Musubi) is real |
| P2 (Human Nature) | **Mixed** | "Man is child of Kami"; no original sin; impurity (Kegare) is external, not internal; innate purity doctrine |
| P3 (Tiered Responsibility) | **Compatible** | Family transmits tradition; ancestors are Kami; communal and vertical responsibility |
| P4 (Goodness) | **Compatible** | "Shinto desires life"; Musubi = spirit of birth and becoming; pollution is death, purity is life; fertility celebrated |
| P5 (Procedural Symmetry) | **Incompatible** | "Japan is the divine country"; ethno-centric, not universal law; Shinto is Way of Japanese People specifically |
| Humility Test | **Fails** | (Historically) Emperor sacred and inviolable; lese-majeste was crime; doubt of divine descent was treason |

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential
"Nature itself is the manifestation of Kami." (Sokyo Ono, *Shinto: The Kami Way*).
"This great land is the land of the Gods." (*Nihon Shoki*).
"The world is not an illusion; it is a sacred territory." (General conceptual summary).
"Musubi... the creative and harmonizing power of the Kami." (Norinaga).

## Humans are Limited and Selfish (Mixed: Innate Purity)
"Man is the child of Kami." (Central dogma).
"There is no concept of original sin. Impurity (*Kegare*) is external." (Sokyo Ono).
"When the heart is pure, it will naturally do what is right." (Motoori Norinaga).
"To sweep away the dust of the mind." (Ritual purification concept).
*(Note: Shinto tends to view evil as external pollution rather than internal corruption, differentiating it from the 'Constrained Vision' of the West).*

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility
"The family is the main mechanism for transmitting tradition." (Sokyo Ono).
"Ancestors are Kami." (Ancestor worship practice).
"The nation is a family with the Emperor as the father." (Kokutai theory - Meiji era).
*(Note: Responsibility is communal and vertical, often bypassing the individual).*

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well
"Shinto desires life... to have life and have it more abundantly." (Sokyo Ono).
"Musubi is the spirit of birth and becoming." (Norinaga).
"To be fully alive is to be filled with Kami-power." (General concept).
"Pollution is death; Purity is life." (Ritual concept).
"Be fruitful and multiply." (Implicit in fertility festivals).

## Procedural Symmetry (Incompatible: Particularism)
"Japan is the divine country." (*Jinnō Shōtōki*).
"The Emperor is the descendant of the Sun Goddess Amaterasu." (*Kojiki*).
"Shinto is the Way of the Japanese People." (Sokyo Ono).
*(Note: Shinto is ethno-centric. It does not posit a universal law for all mankind, but a specific way for the Japanese).*

## Humility (The Criticism Test)
*(Historically Negative)*
"The Emperor is sacred and inviolable." (Meiji Constitution).
"Lese-majeste is a crime against the Kokutai." (Pre-WWII Law).
"To doubt the divine descent is treason." (Pre-WWII attitude).

## 5-Principle Analysis

**P1 (Reality)**: Compatible. "Nature itself is the manifestation of Kami." The world is not illusion—it is sacred territory. "Musubi is the creative and harmonizing power." Reality is real and ordered by divine creative power. Shinto is a this-worldly religion that affirms material existence.

**P2 (Human Nature)**: **Mixed.** "Man is the child of Kami." There is no concept of original sin. Impurity (Kegare) is external contamination, not internal corruption—it can be washed away through ritual purification. "When the heart is pure, it will naturally do what is right." This contradicts P2: if the purified heart is naturally good, then humans are not naturally selfish. The constrained vision of Western thought is absent here.

**P3 (Tiered Responsibility)**: Compatible. "The family is the main mechanism for transmitting tradition." "Ancestors are Kami." The family structure is the medium of religious practice. Responsibility flows through family and community, with the nation conceived as a family ("the Emperor as the father"). This is P3, though with a more communal than individual emphasis.

**P4 (Goodness)**: Compatible. "Shinto desires life... to have life and have it more abundantly." "Musubi is the spirit of birth and becoming." "Pollution is death; Purity is life." Fertility festivals celebrate the continuation of life. This aligns strongly with P4: life should continue and continue well. Shinto is life-affirming.

**P5 (Procedural Symmetry)**: **Incompatible.** "Japan is the divine country." "Shinto is the Way of the Japanese People." This is explicitly ethno-centric. Shinto does not posit universal law for all mankind—it is a particular way for a particular people. The Emperor descends from Amaterasu; this claim cannot apply to non-Japanese. The procedure is identity-based, not universal. This violates P5.

**Humility Test**: **Fails (historically).** "The Emperor is sacred and inviolable." Lese-majeste was criminal. "To doubt the divine descent is treason." State Shinto made critique of the central institution punishable. Folk Shinto may be more relaxed, but the structural capacity for State Shinto shows the system can fuse religion and politics in ways that suppress critique.
