# Provenance regarding Shinto
Primary sources include *The Kojiki* (Record of Ancient Matters), *The Nihon Shoki* (Chronicles of Japan), and the works of revivalist scholars like Motoori Norinaga. Shinto emphasizes the divinity of nature (Kami) and the purity of the community.

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential
"Nature itself is the manifestation of Kami." (Sokyo Ono, *Shinto: The Kami Way*).
"This great land is the land of the Gods." (*Nihon Shoki*).
"The world is not an illusion; it is a sacred territory." (General conceptual summary).
"Musubi... the creative and harmonizing power of the Kami." (Norinaga).

## Humans are Limited and Selfish (Friction: Innate Purity)
"Man is the child of Kami." (Central dogma).
"There is no concept of original sin. Impurity (*Kegare*) is external." (Sokyo Ono).
"When the heart is pure, it will naturally do what is right." (Motoori Norinaga).
"To sweep away the dust of the mind." (Ritual purification concept).
*(Note: Shinto tends to view evil as external pollution rather than internal corruption, differentiating it from the 'Constrained Vision' of the West).*

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility
"The family is the main mechanism for transmitting tradition." (Sokyo Ono).
"Ancestors are Kami." (Ancestor worship practice).
"The nation is a family with the Emperor as the father." (Kokutai theory - Meiji era).
*(Note: Responsibility is communal and vertical, often bypassing the individual).*

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well
"Shinto desires life... to have life and have it more abundantly." (Sokyo Ono).
"Musubi is the spirit of birth and becoming." (Norinaga).
"To be fully alive is to be filled with Kami-power." (General concept).
"Pollution is death; Purity is life." (Ritual concept).
"Be fruitful and multiply." (Implicit in fertility festivals).

## Procedural Symmetry (Incompatible: Particularism)
"Japan is the divine country." (*Jinnō Shōtōki*).
"The Emperor is the descendant of the Sun Goddess Amaterasu." (*Kojiki*).
"Shinto is the Way of the Japanese People." (Sokyo Ono).
*(Note: Shinto is ethno-centric. It does not posit a universal law for all mankind, but a specific way for the Japanese).*

## Humility (The Criticism Test)
*(Historically Negative)*
"The Emperor is sacred and inviolable." (Meiji Constitution).
"Lese-majeste is a crime against the Kokutai." (Pre-WWII Law).
"To doubt the divine descent is treason." (Pre-WWII attitude).
*(Note: While folk Shinto is tolerant, State Shinto was historically intolerant of critique).*
