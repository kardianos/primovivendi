# Provenance regarding Immanuel Kant
Primary sources include *Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals* (1785), *Critique of Practical Reason* (1788), *The Metaphysics of Morals* (1797), *Idea for a Universal History* (1784), and *Religion within the Bounds of Bare Reason* (1793). Kant provides a deontological (duty-based) framework for procedural symmetry.

## 5-Principle Compatibility Summary

| Principle | Rating | Reason |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| P1 (Reality) | **Mixed** | Noumenal/Phenomenal split doubts "things in themselves"; BUT treats phenomenal world as objective for all practical purposes |
| P2 (Human Nature) | **Compatible** | "Crooked timber" quote; "radical evil"; humans need masters because they abuse each other; system works for "race of devils" |
| P3 (Tiered Responsibility) | **Compatible** | Imperfect duties allow latitude for who we help; no compulsion to be happy by another's conception; autonomy of will |
| P4 (Goodness) | **Mixed** | "Fiat justitia, pereat mundus"—let justice be done though world perish; duty over happiness; moral worth over flourishing |
| P5 (Procedural Symmetry) | **Compatible** | Categorical Imperative = universalizability; equality of subjects; no hereditary privilege; strongest match |
| Humility Test | **Passes** | "Sapere Aude"; public use of reason must always be free; freedom of the pen is safeguard |

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential (Mixed: Noumenal/Phenomenal)
"We can know nothing of these [noumena] as they are in themselves, but strictly only as they appear to us [phenomena]." (*Prolegomena to Any Future Metaphysics*).
"Thoughts without content are empty, intuitions without concepts are blind." (*Critique of Pure Reason*).
"Two things fill the mind with ever new and increasing admiration and awe... the starry heavens above me and the moral law within me." (*Critique of Practical Reason*).
*(Note: While he doubts metaphysical knowledge of the 'Thing-in-Itself', he affirms the objective universality of scientific laws within the shared human experience).*

## Humans are Limited and Selfish
"Out of the crooked timber of humanity, no straight thing was ever made." (*Idea for a Universal History*, Proposition 6).
"Man is an animal who needs a master... for he certainly abuses his freedom with respect to other men." (*Idea for a Universal History*, Proposition 6).
"Radical evil... constitutes a corrupt propensity of the human heart... to subordinate the incentives of the moral law to the incentives of self-love." (*Religion within the Bounds of Bare Reason*).
"The problem of organizing a state, however hard it may seem, can be solved even for a race of devils, if only they are intelligent." (*Perpetual Peace*).

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility
"I can vary the degree greatly in accordance with the different objects of my love (one of whom concerns me more than another)." (*The Metaphysics of Morals*).
"The duty of beneficence is imperfect... it leaves a playroom (latitudo) for free choice in following the law." (*The Metaphysics of Morals*).
"Every man has a legitimate claim to respect from his fellow men and is in turn bound to respect every other." (*The Metaphysics of Morals*).
"Autonomy of the will is the property that the will has of being a law to itself." (*Groundwork*).
"No one can compel me to be happy in accordance with his conception of the welfare of others." (*Theory and Practice*).

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well (Mixed: Duty vs Happiness)
"The principle of private happiness... is the direct opposite of the principle of morality." (*Critique of Practical Reason*).
"Morality is not the doctrine of how we may make ourselves happy, but how we may make ourselves worthy of happiness." (*Critique of Practical Reason*).
"Fiat justitia, pereat mundus" (Let justice be done, though the world perish). (*Perpetual Peace*).
*(Note: Kant prioritizes the Right over the Good. However, his political goal is Peace and Freedom).*

## Procedural Symmetry
"Act only according to that maxim whereby you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law." (*Groundwork*).
"Act in such a way that you treat humanity, whether in your own person or in the person of any other, never merely as a means to an end, but always at the same time as an end." (*Groundwork*).
"The civil state... based on... the equality of each with every other as a subject." (*Theory and Practice*, 8:290).
"No member of the commonwealth can have a hereditary privilege against his fellow subjects." (*Theory and Practice*).
"Every member of the commonwealth must be able to reach any level of rank... to which his talent, industry and luck can take him." (*Theory and Practice*).

## Humility (The Criticism Test)
"Sapere Aude! (Dare to know!) Have courage to use your own understanding." (*What is Enlightenment?*).
"The public use of one's reason must always be free, and it alone can bring about enlightenment among men." (*What is Enlightenment?*).
"To deny the freedom of the pen... is to take away the only safeguard of the people's rights." (*Theory and Practice*).
"Reason depends on this freedom for its very existence. For reason has no dictatorial authority; its verdict is always simply the agreement of free citizens." (*Critique of Pure Reason*).

## 5-Principle Analysis

**P1 (Reality)**: **Mixed.** Kant's noumenal/phenomenal distinction means we cannot know "things in themselves." This creates philosophical friction with P1's claim that reality is fully knowable. HOWEVER, Kant treats the phenomenal world (the world of science, law, and experience) as governed by objective, universal laws. In practice, he is an empirical realist. The friction is metaphysical, not practical.

**P2 (Human Nature)**: Compatible. "Out of the crooked timber of humanity, no straight thing was ever made." Humans have "radical evil"—a propensity to subordinate moral law to self-love. "Man is an animal who needs a master" because humans abuse each other. The system can work "even for a race of devils." This is P2: humans are limited, selfish, not naturally good.

**P3 (Tiered Responsibility)**: Compatible. Kant distinguishes "perfect duties" (universal respect, apply to all) from "imperfect duties" (beneficence, allow latitude). "I can vary the degree greatly in accordance with the different objects of my love." This allows tiered responsibility: you owe RESPECT to all (P5), but you owe CARE to your sphere (P3). "No one can compel me to be happy by his conception."

**P4 (Goodness)**: **Mixed.** "Fiat justitia, pereat mundus"—let justice be done though the world perish. "The principle of happiness is the opposite of the principle of morality." Kant explicitly prioritizes DUTY over HAPPINESS. P4 says life should continue well; Kant says moral worth matters more than flourishing. This is real friction. However, his political goal IS peace and stability, so practice aligns better than theory.

**P5 (Procedural Symmetry)**: **Strongest match.** The Categorical Imperative—"act only according to that maxim you can will as universal law"—is procedural symmetry as ethical principle. "Equality of each with every other as a subject." "No hereditary privilege against fellow subjects." Kant provides the philosophical engine for P5.

**Humility Test**: Passes. "Sapere Aude"—dare to know. "The public use of reason must always be free." "Freedom of the pen is the only safeguard." "Reason has no dictatorial authority; its verdict is the agreement of free citizens." Kant explicitly requires open debate and critique. His system structurally allows correction.
