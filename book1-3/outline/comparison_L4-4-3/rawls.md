# Provenance regarding John Rawls
Primary sources include *A Theory of Justice* (1971) and *Political Liberalism* (1993). Rawls builds a system using the "Original Position" and "Veil of Ignorance" to generate "Justice as Fairness."

## 5-Principle Compatibility Summary

| Principle | Rating | Reason |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| P1 (Reality) | **Mixed** | "Method of avoidance"—explicitly avoids metaphysical truth; freestanding view not grounded in reality |
| P2 (Human Nature) | **Compatible** | Parties are mutually disinterested; immediate interests will conflict; accepts selfish premise |
| P3 (Tiered Responsibility) | **Incompatible** | "Natural talents as common asset"—collapses tiers; productive must benefit least advantaged by default |
| P4 (Goodness) | **Compatible** | Primary goods serve human flourishing; rational life plan as goal; reasonable compatibility |
| P5 (Procedural Symmetry) | **Incompatible** | Applies DIFFERENT procedures to talented vs. untalented; talented encumbered by claims untalented don't face |
| Humility Test | **Mixed** | Critique allowed, but "Public Reason" filters out religious/metaphysical arguments as "unreasonable" |

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential (Mixed: Method of Avoidance)
"Justice as fairness allows... that there may be no such [moral] order, or that if there is, it is one to which problems of political philosophy are not related." (*Political Liberalism*).
"We should apply the principle of toleration to philosophy itself." (*Political Liberalism*).
"Political liberalism is not a comprehensive doctrine... it is a freestanding view." (*Political Liberalism*).
*(Note: Rawls explicitly avoids grounding his system in metaphysical truth, relying instead on 'Overlapping Consensus').*

## Humans are Limited and Selfish
"The parties [in the Original Position] are conceived as not taking an interest in one another's interests." (*A Theory of Justice*).
"They know that in the society they are to enter, their immediate interests will often conflict." (*A Theory of Justice*).
"Men are not indifferent to how the greater benefits produced by their collaboration are distributed." (*A Theory of Justice*).
*(Note: Rawls accepts the 'selfish' premise of the social contract tradition).*

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility (Incompatible: Difference Principle)
"The difference principle represents, in effect, an agreement to regard the distribution of natural talents as a common asset." (*A Theory of Justice*).
"Social and economic inequalities are to be arranged so that they are... to the greatest benefit of the least advantaged." (*A Theory of Justice*).
"No one deserves his greater natural capacity nor merits a more favorable starting place in society." (*A Theory of Justice*).
"We are led to the difference principle if we wish to set up the social system so that no one gains or loses from his arbitrary place in the distribution of natural assets." (*A Theory of Justice*).

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well
"Primary goods... are things that every rational man is presumed to want." (*A Theory of Justice*).
"Rights, liberties, and opportunities, and income and wealth, and the social bases of self-respect." (*A Theory of Justice* - list of Primary Goods).
"A person's good is determined by what is for him the most rational long-term plan of life." (*A Theory of Justice*).

## Procedural Symmetry (Incompatible: Asymmetry of Talent)
"The natural distribution is neither just nor unjust... what is just and unjust is the way that institutions deal with these facts." (*A Theory of Justice*).
"Those who have been favored by nature... may gain from their good fortune only on terms that improve the situation of those who have lost out." (*A Theory of Justice*).
*(Note: This creates a legal asymmetry: the productive are encumbered by a claim from the non-productive).*

## Humility (The Criticism Test)
"Citizens are to conduct their fundamental discussions within the framework of what each regards as a political conception of justice... this is the duty of civility." (*Political Liberalism*).
"Comprehensive doctrines... are not to be introduced into public political discussion." (*Political Liberalism*).
"Unreasonable doctrines... are a threat to democratic institutions." (*Political Liberalism*).

## 5-Principle Analysis

**P1 (Reality)**: **Mixed.** Rawls explicitly avoids grounding his system in metaphysical truth. "Justice as fairness allows that there may be no such moral order." His system relies on "overlapping consensus" rather than correspondence to reality. This creates friction: if P1 says reality is knowable and consequential, Rawls says we can build politics without knowing what reality IS. Practical, but philosophically rootless.

**P2 (Human Nature)**: Compatible. "The parties in the Original Position are conceived as not taking an interest in one another's interests." "Their immediate interests will often conflict." Rawls accepts the selfish premise of social contract theory. He designs institutions for humans as they are, not as we might wish them to be.

**P3 (Tiered Responsibility)**: **Incompatible.** This is the critical failure. "The distribution of natural talents [is treated] as a common asset." The Difference Principle makes the talented RESPONSIBLE for the least advantaged BY DEFAULT, not by choice. This collapses P3's gradient: you cannot put self/family first if your talents are already communal property. The productive individual's responsibility to strangers is made EQUAL to or GREATER than responsibility to self.

**P4 (Goodness)**: Compatible. "Primary goods are things every rational man is presumed to want." "A person's good is determined by what is for him the most rational long-term plan of life." Rawls aims at human flourishing through access to resources. This aligns with P4, though subordinated to "justice."

**P5 (Procedural Symmetry)**: **Incompatible.** This is the structural failure, analyzed by principle:
- The Difference Principle applies DIFFERENT procedures based on IDENTITY (talented vs. untalented).
- The talented face claims on their productivity that the untalented DO NOT face.
- This is procedural asymmetry by definition: the rule varies by WHO you are.
- Structurally identical to taxing by race: the PROCEDURE varies by identity characteristic.
- P5 requires the same procedure for all subjects. Rawls violates this by design.

**Humility Test**: **Mixed.** "Citizens conduct their fundamental discussions within the framework of political conception." "Comprehensive doctrines are not to be introduced into public discussion." This filters out religious and metaphysical arguments as inadmissible. While not criminalizing critique, it defines certain critiques as outside the bounds of "reasonable" discourse. This is soft exclusion.
