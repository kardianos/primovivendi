# Provenance regarding Stoicism
Primary sources include Epictetus (*Discourses*, *Enchiridion*), Marcus Aurelius (*Meditations*), and Seneca (*Letters from a Stoic*). Stoicism emphasizes the objective Logocentric nature of reality, the need for individual self-mastery, and duties to the Cosmopolis (World City).

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential
"If you are going to be a philosopher, you must adjust your will to nature." (Epictetus, *Discourses*).
"Everything that happens happens as it should, and if you observe carefully, you will find this to be so." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 4.10).
"The universe is transformation; life is opinion." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 4.3). *(Note: This refers to our judgment of events, not the denial of events themselves).*
"Constantly regard the universe as one living being, having one substance and one soul." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 4.40).
"Nature is volitional and acts towards an end." (Seneca, *Letters*).
"Live in accordance with nature." (Zeno of Citium).

## Humans are Limited and Selfish
"It is not things that disturb us, but our judgment of things." (Epictetus, *Enchiridion* 5).
"When you wake up in the morning, tell yourself: The people I deal with today will be meddling, ungrateful, arrogant, dishonest, jealous, and surly. They are like this because they can't tell good from evil." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 2.1).
"No man is free who is not master of himself." (Epictetus, *Enchiridion*).
"We are sick with evils that we have loved." (Seneca, *Letters*).
"The mind is the ruler of the soul. It should remain unstirred by agitations of the flesh." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 5.26).

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility
"As for your parents, you have a duty to take care of them, even if they are bad parents." (Epictetus, *Discourses*).
"My nature is rational and social; and my city and country, so far as I am Antoninus, is Rome, but so far as I am a man, it is the world." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 6.44).
"Circles of concern include the body, family, neighbors, fellow citizens, and humanity." (Hierocles).
"It is the duty of a man... to treat his own family with affection." (Hierocles, *On Duties*).
"We must draw the circles inward." (Hierocles). 
"He who lives only for himself is dead to others." (Publilius Syrus - Stoic influenced).

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well
"Virtue is the only good." (Seneca, *Letters* 71). *(Note: High bar for Goodness, can friction with 'Goodness is Survival').*
"The objective of life is to live in agreement with nature." (Zeno).
"It is a characteristic of the rational soul... that it loves its neighbors, and truth and modesty." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 11.1).
"Joy comes to us from those whom we love even when they are absent." (Seneca, *Letters*).
"Choose not to be harmed—and you won't feel harmed. Don't feel harmed—and you haven't been." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 4.7).

## Procedural Symmetry
"There is but one law for all, namely that common reason which affirms what should be done and forbids the contrary." (Cicero, *De Legibus* - Stoic influenced).
"If our intellectual part is common, the reason also, in respect of which we are rational beings, is common... if this is so, we are fellow-citizens." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 4.4).
"Slavery is an external condition; the mind remains free." (Seneca, *De Beneficiis*).
"Treat your superior as you would wish your superior to treat you." (Seneca, *Letters* 47).
"We are members of one great body. Nature produced us related to one another." (Seneca, *Letters* 95).

## Humility (The Criticism Test)
"If anyone can refute me, show me I’m making a mistake or looking at things from the wrong perspective, I’ll gladly change. It’s the truth I’m after, and the truth never harmed anyone." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 6.21).
"Begin the morning by saying to thyself... I have met with the busy-body, the ungrateful, the arrogant, the deceitful, the envious, the unsocial." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 2.1) - *Acknowledging the imperfection of the social world implies tolerance of imperfection.*
"Does another do me wrong? Let him look to it. He has his own disposition, his own activity." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 9.42).
"The best revenge is to be unlike him who performed the injury." (Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations* 6.6).
