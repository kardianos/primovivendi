# Provenance regarding The Jesuit Order (Modern: 1920s-1980s)
Primary sources include Michel de Certeau (*The Practice of Everyday Life*, *The Writing of History*) and Xavier Tilliette (*Christologie philosophique*). This period is marked by syncretism between Catholic theology and Continental Critical Theory, leading to "Liberation Theology."

## 5-Principle Compatibility Summary

| Principle | Rating | Reason |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| P1 (Reality) | **Split** | Tilliette: "Facticity is criterion of truth"—realist. Certeau: "The real talks constantly but is simulacrum"—constructivist. Internal contradiction. |
| P2 (Human Nature) | **Mixed** | Oppressor/Oppressed binary; "weak must poach on property of others"; structures matter more than nature |
| P3 (Tiered Responsibility) | **Mixed** | Agency attributed to "Structures" and "Strategies"; responsibility shifted from individual to System |
| P4 (Goodness) | **Mixed** | "Good" framed as resistance to status quo; revolutionary rather than conservative flourishing |
| P5 (Procedural Symmetry) | **Incompatible** | Critical stance attacks "dominant culture" while treating "marginalized" as morally superior—asymmetric by identity |
| Humility Test | **Mixed** | Intellectually humble (kenosis of reason), BUT "Critical Vanguard" claims to know hidden structures better than common man |

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential (The Metaphysical Divergence)
*Xavier Tilliette (The Theological Realist):*
"Facticity is the ultimate criterion of truth... The Christ-event is a fact." (Xavier Tilliette).
"Philosophy must account for the relationship between eternity and time." (Xavier Tilliette).
*Michel de Certeau (The Critical Constructivist):*
"The media transforms the great silence of things into its opposite... the real now talks constantly [but is a simulacrum]." (Michel de Certeau).
"History is a staging of the past." (Michel de Certeau).
*(Note: While Tilliette defends a 'Real' Christ, Certeau adopts the language of Postmodernism where social reality is a construct of power, creating deep friction with the 'Shared Reality' of empiricism).*

## Humans are Limited and Selfish (Tactics vs Class Struggle)
"Everyday life invents itself by poaching in countless ways on the property of others." (Michel de Certeau).
"The weak must continually turn to their own ends forces alien to them." (Michel de Certeau).
"A tactic is determined by the absence of power." (Michel de Certeau).
*(Note: Unlike classic Marxism which empowers the 'Proletariat' as a new Class, Certeau focuses on the individual 'Tactician'. However, the 'Oppressor/Oppressed' binary remains the primary lens).*

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility (Mixed: Structuralism)
"Social law divests the individual of his competence to establish the capital of a collective competence." (Michel de Certeau).
"We must analyze the 'strategies' of institutions." (Michel de Certeau).
*(Note: By attributing agency to 'Structures' and 'Strategies', responsibility is often shifted away from the individual moral actor to the 'System', a hallmark of Marxist thought).*

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well (The Preferential Option)
"Through matter and its substances, Christ is revealed." (Xavier Tilliette).
"To make do (faire avec)... is to live." (Michel de Certeau).
*(Note: The 'Good' is often framed as resistance to the status quo, which dovetails with revolutionary rather than conservative flourishing).*

## Procedural Symmetry (Incompatible: The Critical Vanguard)
"The Scriptural Economy... separates the writers (those with power) from the readers (those without)." (Michel de Certeau).
"The only freedom... is that of grazing on the ration of simulacra." (Michel de Certeau).
*(Note: The Modern Jesuit often adopts a 'Critical' stance that attacks the 'Hegemony' of the West/Capitalism. This creates an asymmetry: they critique the dominant culture (The Right/West) while treating the 'Marginalized' as morally superior, violating the neutrality of Procedural Symmetry).*

## Humility (The Criticism Test): Mixed
"Belief is... the subject's investment in a proposition." (Michel de Certeau).
"Philosophy implies a kenosis (emptying) of reason before the Fact." (Xavier Tilliette).

## 5-Principle Analysis

**P1 (Reality)**: **Split—internal contradiction.** Tilliette: "Facticity is the ultimate criterion of truth. The Christ-event is a fact." This is realist. Certeau: "The media transforms the great silence of things into its opposite—the real now talks constantly but is a simulacrum." "History is a staging of the past." This is constructivist. The Modern Jesuit contains BOTH positions. The tension is unresolved.

**P2 (Human Nature)**: **Mixed.** Certeau focuses on "tactics" of the weak who "must poach on property of others." The frame is Oppressor/Oppressed, not the constrained vision of universal human limitation. While Certeau's "tactician" is more individual than Marxist "class," the binary remains. Structures explain behavior more than innate nature.

**P3 (Tiered Responsibility)**: **Mixed.** "Social law divests the individual of his competence." "We must analyze the strategies of institutions." By attributing agency to Structures and Strategies, responsibility shifts from individual moral actor to System. This is the Marxist frame: if the System made me, the System is responsible. P3's individual-first responsibility is diluted.

**P4 (Goodness)**: **Mixed.** "To make do (faire avec) is to live"—survival as resistance. The "Good" is framed as resistance to the status quo, which dovetails with revolutionary rather than conservative flourishing. P4's "continue well" typically means stable improvement within order. Liberation Theology's "Good" often means disruption of order.

**P5 (Procedural Symmetry)**: **Incompatible.** "The Scriptural Economy separates the writers (those with power) from the readers (those without)." The Modern Jesuit critical stance attacks the "dominant culture" (The West/Capitalism/The Right) while treating the "Marginalized" as morally superior. This creates asymmetry: critique flows one direction. The powerful are guilty by identity; the weak are innocent by identity. P5 requires the same evaluation procedure regardless of identity.

**Humility Test**: **Mixed.** Tilliette: "Philosophy implies a kenosis (emptying) of reason before the Fact"—intellectual humility before truth. BUT the "Critical Vanguard" stance claims to know "Hidden Structures" that the common man cannot perceive. This is gnostic certainty: "We see the oppression you cannot see." The academic can critique the worker's false consciousness, but the worker cannot critique the academic's theory. Asymmetric humility.
