# Provenance regarding Postmodernism
Primary sources include Michel Foucault (*Discipline and Punish*, *Power/Knowledge*), Jacques Derrida (*Of Grammatology*), Jean-François Lyotard (*The Postmodern Condition*), and Herbert Marcuse (*Repressive Tolerance*). This worldview rejects metanarratives and views truth as a function of power.

## 5-Principle Compatibility Summary

| Principle | Rating | Reason |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| P1 (Reality) | **Incompatible** | "No outside-text"; truth is produced by power; "incredulity toward metanarratives"; reality is constructed |
| P2 (Human Nature) | **Incompatible** | Subject is a "function" of power; human nature is socially constructed; blank slate enables utopianism |
| P3 (Tiered Responsibility) | **Incompatible** | "Structure speaks, not subject"; responsibility shifted to systems; individual agency denied |
| P4 (Goodness) | **Incompatible** | Critique without construction; goal is overthrow, not flourishing; "deconstruction is justice" ignores goodness |
| P5 (Procedural Symmetry) | **Incompatible** | Marcuse: "intolerance against the Right, tolerance for the Left"—explicit asymmetry by identity |
| Humility Test | **Fails** | Critique from "dominant" group classified as violence; universality is "hegemony"; system cannot be questioned by wrong identities |

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential (Incompatible)
"There is no outside-text (Il n'y a pas de hors-texte)." (Derrida, *Of Grammatology*).
"Simplifying to the extreme, I define postmodern as incredulity toward metanarratives." (Lyotard, *The Postmodern Condition*).
"Truth isn't outside power... it is produced only by virtue of multiple forms of constraint. And it induces regular effects of power." (Foucault, *Power/Knowledge*).
"The 'truth' is centered on the form of scientific discourse and the institutions which produce it." (Foucault, *Power/Knowledge*).
"Objectivity is a subject's delusion that observing can be done without him." (General Critical Theory concept).

## Humans are Limited and Selfish (Incompatible: Social Construction)
"The subject is not the constitutive factor; it is rather a variable... a function." (Foucault, *What is an Author?*).
"Man is an invention of recent date. And one perhaps nearing its end." (Foucault, *The Order of Things*).
"Gender is a kind of imitation for which there is no original." (Judith Butler, *Inside/Out* - derived from Postmodern thought).
*(Note: The denial of a fixed human nature ('Blank Slate') allows for utopian engineering).*

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility (Incompatible: Structuralism)
"Power is everywhere, not because it embraces everything, but because it comes from everywhere." (Foucault, *The History of Sexuality*).
"It is the structure that speaks, not the subject." (Structuralist maxim).
*(Note: If the subject is a result of power structures, responsibility is shifted to the structure).*

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well (Incompatible: Critique without Construction)
"Deconstruction is justice." (Derrida). *(But ignores 'Goodness').*
"The ultimate goal of the revolution is not to achieve 'happiness' or 'liberty' in the bourgeois sense, but to overthrow the system of domination." (Frankfurt School influence).
*(Note: Postmodernism is an engine of critique, not a theory of the Good Life).*

## Procedural Symmetry (Incompatible: Equity)
"Liberating tolerance, then, would mean intolerance against movements from the Right and toleration of movements from the Left." (Marcuse, *Repressive Tolerance*).
"The withdrawal of toleration of speech and assembly from groups and movements which promote sort of aggressive policies... [is required]." (Marcuse, *Repressive Tolerance*).
"Justice is infinite... it is the experience of the impossible." (Derrida).
*(Note: The explicit call for 'intolerance' against the 'Right' violates Procedural Symmetry).*

## Humility (The Criticism Test)
"To treat the oppressed and the oppressor equally is to aid the oppressor." (General Critical Theory maxim).
"Universality is a mask for white, male, European hegemony." (Critical Race Theory concept).

## 5-Principle Analysis

**P1 (Reality)**: **Incompatible at root.** "There is no outside-text"—reality is not accessible independent of discourse. "Truth is produced by virtue of multiple forms of constraint"—truth is a function of power, not correspondence to reality. This directly negates P1. If reality is not knowable independent of power relations, then there is no neutral ground for evidence, law, or science.

**P2 (Human Nature)**: **Incompatible.** "The subject is not constitutive; it is a function." "Man is an invention of recent date." Human nature is socially constructed, not fixed. This is the blank slate that enables utopian engineering: if there is no human nature, humans can be remade. P2 says humans are LIMITED and SELFISH by nature. Postmodernism denies there is a nature to be limited.

**P3 (Tiered Responsibility)**: **Incompatible.** "It is the structure that speaks, not the subject." "Power comes from everywhere." If the individual is a product of structures, then responsibility shifts from individual to system. You are not responsible for your choices; the oppressive system is responsible for your outcomes. This is the inversion of P3's individual-first responsibility.

**P4 (Goodness)**: **Incompatible.** "Deconstruction is justice"—but where is the GOOD? Postmodernism is an engine of critique, not a theory of the good life. The "ultimate goal is overthrow of the system of domination," not flourishing. P4 requires a positive vision of life continuing well. Postmodernism offers only negation.

**P5 (Procedural Symmetry)**: **Incompatible—explicitly so.** Marcuse: "Liberating tolerance would mean intolerance against movements from the Right and toleration of movements from the Left." This is asymmetric procedure by design. "To treat oppressed and oppressor equally is to aid the oppressor." P5 requires the same procedure for all. Postmodernism explicitly rejects this: different rules for different identity categories.

**Humility Test**: **Fails.** "Universality is a mask for hegemony." If someone from the "dominant" group questions the system, their critique is classified as violence or oppression. The system is immune to critique from wrong identities. This is the opposite of humility—it is epistemic closure based on identity.
