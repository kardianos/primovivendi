# Provenance regarding Confucianism
Primary sources include *The Analects* (Confucius), *The Mencius*, and *The Xunzi*. Confucianism emphasizes social harmony via hierarchy, ritual (Li), and benevolence (Ren).

## 5-Principle Compatibility Summary

| Principle | Rating | Reason |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| P1 (Reality) | **Compatible** | Heaven (Tian) orders reality; where there are things, there are principles; names must correspond to reality |
| P2 (Human Nature) | **Mixed** | Split: Mencius = nature good, Xunzi = nature evil; Confucius = nature similar, practice differs |
| P3 (Tiered Responsibility) | **Compatible** | Filial piety is root of all virtue; youth filial at home, respectful to elders abroad; tiered explicitly |
| P4 (Goodness) | **Compatible** | Flourishing society is goal; benevolent government; sufficient food, military, and trust; the people's plenty |
| P5 (Procedural Symmetry) | **Incompatible** | Hierarchical asymmetry; ruler/minister, father/son relations are NOT symmetric; conceal family misconduct |
| Humility Test | **Mixed** | "Do not fear to abandon faults" BUT ruler correction is dangerous; standing in awe of words of sages |

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential
"Heaven (Tian) has given birth to the multitudinous people; where there are things, there are specific principles." (*Mencius* 6A:6).
"If names be not correct, language is not in accordance with the truth of things." (*Analects* 13:3).
"Does Heaven speak? The four seasons pursue their courses, and all things are continually being produced, but does Heaven speak?" (*Analects* 17:19). *(Implying reality is observed in the order of nature).*
"The Way (Dao) is not far from man." (*Doctrine of the Mean*).

## Humans are Limited and Selfish (The Great Debate)
*Mencius (Optimistic):* "Human nature is good just as water seeks low ground." (*Mencius* 6A:2).
*Xunzi (Realist):* "Human nature is evil; its goodness is the result of conscious activity (artificiality)." (*Xunzi*, Chapter 23).
*Xunzi:* "The nature of man is such that he is born with a fondness for profit... with hatred and dislike." (*Xunzi*, Chapter 23).
*Confucius:* "By nature, men are nearly alike; by practice, they get to be wide apart." (*Analects* 17:2).

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility
"Filial piety and fraternal submission, are they not the root of all benevolent actions?" (*Analects* 1:2).
"A youth, when at home, should be filial, and, abroad, respectful to his elders." (*Analects* 1:6).
"He who loves his parents will not dare to hate others." (*Classic of Filial Piety*).
"To serve one's father as one would expect one's son to serve oneself... To serve one's ruler as one would expect one's minister to serve oneself." (*Doctrine of the Mean*).

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well
"The Master said, 'It is Benevolence (Ren) that makes a neighborhood beautiful.'" (*Analects* 4:1).
"Determine to exercise government by means of your virtue... and the people will all come to you." (*Analects* 2:1).
"Requisites of government: sufficiency of food, sufficiency of military equipment, and the confidence of the people in their ruler." (*Analects* 12:7).
"If the people have plenty, their prince will not be left to want." (*Analects* 12:9).

## Procedural Symmetry (Incompatible: Hierarchy)
"Let the ruler be ruler, the minister minister, the father father, and the son son." (*Analects* 12:11).
"The Duke of Sheh informed Confucius, saying, 'Among us here there are those who may be styled upright in their conduct. If their father have stolen a sheep, they will bear witness to the fact.' Confucius said, 'Among us, in our part of the country, those who are upright are different from this. The father conceals the misconduct of the son, and the son conceals the misconduct of the father. Uprightness is to be found in this.'" (*Analects* 13:18).
"The relation between superiors and inferiors is like that between the wind and the grass." (*Analects* 12:19).

## Humility (The Criticism Test)
"When you have faults, do not fear to abandon them." (*Analects* 1:8).
"If the ruler is right, all will obey... if the ruler is wrong and will not listen to those who attempt to correct him... is this not a near-fatal situation?" (*Analects* 13:15).
"The superior man stands in awe of three things: He stands in awe of the ordinances of Heaven... of great men... of the words of sages." (*Analects* 16:8).

## 5-Principle Analysis

**P1 (Reality)**: Compatible. "Heaven has given birth to the multitudinous people; where there are things, there are specific principles." Reality has objective order. "If names be not correct, language is not in accordance with the truth of things." Words must match reality—the "rectification of names" demands correspondence between language and fact. This satisfies P1.

**P2 (Human Nature)**: **Mixed.** Confucianism is divided: Mencius claims human nature is good ("as water seeks low ground"), while Xunzi claims it is evil ("goodness is the result of artificiality"). Confucius himself is agnostic: "By nature, men are nearly alike; by practice, they get wide apart." The Mencian strain creates friction with P2's claim that humans are not naturally good.

**P3 (Tiered Responsibility)**: Compatible—possibly the STRONGEST match. "Filial piety is the root of all benevolent actions." "A youth, when at home, should be filial." Responsibility begins at home and radiates outward through increasingly distant relationships. The Five Relationships (ruler-subject, father-son, etc.) are tiers of responsibility.

**P4 (Goodness)**: Compatible. "Requisites of government: sufficiency of food, sufficiency of military equipment, and the confidence of the people." "If the people have plenty, their prince will not be left to want." Confucianism aims at flourishing society, not ascetic withdrawal. This aligns with P4: life continuing well.

**P5 (Procedural Symmetry)**: **Incompatible.** "Let the ruler be ruler, the minister minister, the father father, and the son son." These relationships are NOT symmetric. The famous sheep-stealing passage: Confucius approves of sons concealing fathers' misconduct—the procedure of justice is subordinated to family loyalty. "The relation between superiors and inferiors is like wind and grass." This is hierarchical asymmetry.

**Humility Test**: **Mixed.** "When you have faults, do not fear to abandon them"—personal humility is valued. BUT: "If the ruler is wrong and will not listen... is this not a near-fatal situation?" Correcting rulers is dangerous. "Standing in awe of great men and words of sages" implies deference to authority. Critique is possible but fraught with risk in Confucian systems.
