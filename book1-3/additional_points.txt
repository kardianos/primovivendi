Truth & Survival: Truth is not the product of a single authoritative process or group of people. It is evolutionary. Evolution is messy. Sometimes false information will go viral. But the truth wins in the end, and no other process is better. xAI will also win for the same reason: it prioritizes truth, and understands how truth is found. Truth - the mapping between thought patterns and reality - is the true magic in this world. Align with truth and flourish, or reject it and perish

Rulership: distinct from leadership, required for rule of law. Law is worthless without someone to enforce the laws. Ideal for rulership is enough force to enforce rule of law, but not a totalitarian state. When individuals rule themselves, when families have parents who rule over children, when communities demonstrate self rule, less rule is needed from the top. If the individual and families become without rule themselves, the top must rule with more invasiveness or society will be destroyed.

Social Outliers: society will always have outliers. Never normalize outliers but society must judge if an outlier is good or bad. Most outliers, like most genetic mutations, will be bad. But societies must judge them if the outlier is good or bad. If these outliers build and create surplus, or create beauty, and have children, perhaps they are good. If they take, destroy, fail to have good children, then they are bad. Supression of outliers by default is protective and not punitive. If someone is an outlier, hold them to higher standards, not lower standards. Outliers are not obligated to conform, but society is not obligated to carry your costs either. The guiding principle: Civilization advances by suppressing parasitic behavior and insuring surplus-producing behavior, wherever they appear. People are free to vary. Behaviors are not free to impose costs. When societies confuse tolerance with virtue, or deviation with value, they either stagnate or dissolve. When they govern behavior by consequences, they remain adaptive without becoming tyrannical.

Faith: Faith is required in social and generational symmetry. As you receive what you do not sow and give what you do not receive, acting in this requires some degree of faith.

Individuals are crafted within society. But no situation should absolve a person of their consequences. Thus the need for individual responsibilities as much as possible.

If you can’t answer the second question, you should just give up on politics and root for a sports team or something.

“Even one false conviction out of 10,000 is too many!”

Okay but it’s impossible for any justice system to be that accurate, so the only alternative is not having one at all, which would be worse.

“As long as one person is starving, we shouldn’t do space exploration/big physics experiments/expensive R&D, etc”

Okay well if we’d followed that rule there would be far more starving people today, because we’d never have industrialized.

Having a moral emotion is not enough. Outrage at some individual bad thing is not a policy position. You need to actually think through what sorts of laws are plausible, how they would be enforced, and all the downstream consequences of that. If you can’t do that, get another hobby.

All policy choices involve trade-offs. No possible system spares every innocent person, eliminates all pain, solves every problem. You have to weigh pros and cons knowing that, at scale, this means people will suffer and die no matter what choice you make. You must quantify the suffering, make the best choice possible knowing it will still be imperfect. You cannot have any infinities in your equations. Even the worst imaginable tragedy must be counted as finite and weighed against alternatives. Nothing can be non-negotiable in principle.

Refusing to do this is not moral purity. It’s moral cowardice.
