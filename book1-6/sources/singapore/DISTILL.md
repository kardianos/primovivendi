# Singapore distill — relevant quotes for the book

**Book use:** Part 2 historical positive case; Part 1 thrival under constraints; excellence/merit; survival not owed.  
**Full transcripts:** same folder (`lky_*.txt`). This file is enough for drafting.  
**Provenance:** NAS-style English transcripts from `book1-4/ref/singapore/`. Spot-check against National Archives of Singapore if publishing long quotes.

---

## What this case is for (logic)

1. Small place, no hinterland fantasy → constraints are real.  
2. Survival is not a tribunal right; work and order earn the next year.  
3. Quality/talent under scarcity; character not identical to test scores.  
4. Multi-racial civic order as survival equipment, not slogan-only diversity.  
5. Tough/rugged society = capacity to stay free, not sadism.  
6. Rule of law = certainty of peaceable life under known rules.

## Core quotes (use these)

### 1. World does not owe a living  
**Source:** Speech, Serangoon Gardens Circus, 11 Dec 1965 (`lky_1965_survival_small_country_19651211c.txt`)

> Look, the world does not owe us a living. We start off with that. At the same time, we owe the world nothing.

**Nearby logic in same speech:** survival interests of outsiders if Singapore fails; disciplined workers; stakes are “your life and mine.”

### 2. Survival and multi-racial order  
**Source:** Queenstown Community Centre, 10 Aug 1966 (`lky_1966_quality_merit_tough_society_19660810.txt`)

> And the best way to ensure our survival and to ensure an enduring future is through a multi-racial society. By that I mean a society which is tolerant to all groups, which gives a chance to everybody. If you start a society on the basis of “I am the majority…”

### 3. Tough / rugged society  
**Source:** same 10 Aug 1966

> …whether you should have a rugged, robust, disciplined effective society, a hard society, a tough, rugged society … You want to live your lives as free men in an open society, not as sycophants or worse, as serfs doing other people’s bidding.

### 4. Quality over size  
**Source:** same 10 Aug 1966

> what we lack in numbers and size, we must make up for in quality. This is most important: quality. It is the young that will determine what happens to this society.

### 5. Yeast / catalyst talent under scarce resources  
**Source:** 19 Jun 1967 talent/education speech (`lky_1967_trier_stayer_gifted_talent_19670619.txt`)

> …expend our limited and slender resources in order that they will provide that yeast, that ferment, that catalyst in our society which alone will ensure that Singapore shall maintain its pre-eminent place…

**Same speech (gifted vs cultivated):**  
> How much of a man is innate, born, gifted, how much of it is cultivated, nurtured and disciplined — educationalists, physiologists and psychologists will argue about this to the end of time.

**Writer use:** excellence under scarcity; do not pretend talent is uniform; character/formation still load-bearing.

### 6. Steel / quality of people after turbulence  
**Source:** interview 9 Sep 1965 (`lky_1965_separation_survival_19650909.txt`)

> the survival of a people is not in the numbers of population and the breadth of territory alone. It is the quality of the men. And I think Singapore, having gone through two decades of turbulence, has got steel in it.

### 7. Rule of law (certainty)  
**Source:** 18 Mar 1967 rule of law speech (`lky_1967_rule_of_law_19670318.txt`)

Context: answering critics of rule of law in new states; stress on certainty of a man’s future, capacity to live in peace unmolested under known order.  
**Writer:** quote only after reopening full paragraph in that file (search “rule of law” / “certainty”).
