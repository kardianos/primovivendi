## SEQ-1 Reality and truth (Part 1 B1–B2, M)

1. Bodies need food, shelter, heat.  
2. Whether those needs are met depends on what happens, not on slogans.  
3. Therefore treat the world as independent of wish (B1).  
4. Cooperation needs shared claims (plant, build, heal, defend).  
5. Shared claims need a shared test: does the claim track the world?  
6. That test is correspondence (B2).  
7. When model and world disagree, prefer the world.  
8. **Branch:** If a claim cannot be tested in this reality → do not pretend this book decided it (M1, H9).  
9. **Branch:** If it can be tested → go to SEQ-2.

## SEQ-2 Models: wrong, incomplete, progressive (Part 1 M)

1. Suppose procedure P outputs useful predictions or successful practice.  
2. That shows P is useful in a domain.  
3. It does **not** alone prove P’s ontology is the full true account (M2).  
4. **Claim as made (M2b):** Grade the proposition actually advanced (precision, reasons available at the time), not a wider story rewritten after the outcome.  
5. Later luck does not repair bad grounds; vague claims that almost any outcome can “confirm” earn little credit.  
6. Confirmability tracks how far a claim could have failed; award future trust only to processes that earn hits under pinned claims.  
7. **Wrong:** fails systematically under proper tests; no progressive domain (alchemy-class).  
8. **Incomplete:** succeeds in a domain; fails outside; successor keeps limit-case success (M3).  
9. **Example wrong-story / good-enough numbers:** historical astronomical schemes with epicycles could be relatively accurate for positions while the physical story was not the true arrangement; Kepler improved correspondence (M4A; `philosophy/models_astronomy_chain.md`).  
10. **Example incomplete progressive chain:** Kepler → Newton → special relativity (M4B).  
11. Apply **same critical standard** to favored and disfavored models, including the same rule for credit and failure (B5, M5, M2b).  
12. Therefore: keep useful tools inside their envelope; revise stories under harder tests; do not hermetically protect preferred models; do not take victory laps on unpinned claims.

## SEQ-3 Life is good (Part 1 B3)

1. Without living people, no values, justice, or kindness have holders.  
2. Pure person-affecting / consent-only ethics: empty world wrongs no subject → dead earth ranks with living earth.  
3. That ranking is coherent only if existence of life is not itself a good.  
4. Reject that ranking; **affirm** axiom: human life should continue and continue well.  
5. This is an axiom (chosen standard), motivated by (1)–(3) and ancestral chain — not a pure deduction from “exists.”  
6. **Corollaries:** persistent institutions; do not celebrate extinction; honor chain; transmit platform.  
7. **Participation:** have children or raise up; calibrate full life not headcount; not draft into every profession.  
8. Rival axioms (harm-reduction-max, equity-as-good, sacred unity-as-good, anti-natal) are named as rivals, not mere arithmetic errors.

## SEQ-4 Tiers and procedure (Part 1 B4–B5)

1. Time, knowledge, strength finite (B7).  
2. Unlimited declared duty becomes undone duty.  
3. Therefore rank duties: near stronger; outer real but weaker (B4).  
4. Cooperation among limited self-interested agents needs stable expectations.  
5. Stable expectations need same rules within a defined scope (B5).  
6. Outcome equality as justice requires unequal procedure → corruption and equalizing class.  
7. Value-for-value revenge is backward feud, not forward cooperation.  
8. Therefore justice = procedural symmetry in honest scopes, not outcome or value symmetry (Part 2 C expands).

## SEQ-5 Particular person, not marble (Part 1 B8)

1. If the self were a featureless token, particular duties would be arbitrary prejudice.  
2. But the existing self is produced by ancestors, language, formation, place.  
3. Erase the chain in imagination and there is no subject left to theorize.  
4. Therefore particular gratitude and transmission are not “bias” by default.  
5. Customs still tested by truth and thrival — not everything inherited is sacred.  
6. Not every comprehensive vision fits one space; different spaces under a shared testable public floor (E12).  
7. Allow real failure in formation; do not engineer failure into self or children (D15).

## SEQ-7 Fall: harm entitlement (Part 1 F)

1. Good: short job loss / hunger should not mean death when a bridge exists (F0).  
2. Society gets good at survival and bridges.  
3. People treat bridges as weather.  
4. Slide: bridge → lifestyle entitlement → “I must never be harmed/refused for any reason.”  
5. But survival required no’s that harm someone’s plan (thief, free rider, invader).  
6. If never-harm-aims is master rule, predation cannot be stopped → survival erodes → bridges die.  
7. Therefore refuse the slide; keep F0.  
8. Reaction “let them starve; cruelty is clarity” fails B3 and F0.  
9. Positive: time-bound reciprocal bridges; fair no’s; allow failure without courting it.
