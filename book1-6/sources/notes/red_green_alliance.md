# Working note: Red–green alliance commonalities

**Status:** Not integrated into chapters. Do not concat into `book.md`.  
**Purpose:** Hold mechanisms and commonalities that create a natural *tendency* for progressive left (“red”) and political-Islamist / hard Islamic packages (“green”) to coordinate—without claiming full principle identity, permanent friendship, or a census of persons.  
**Related notes:** `notes/purity_virtue_reality.md` (dual codes, purity vs reality procedure).  
**Frame anchors:** persons ≠ packages; premises cut labels; P1–P5; alliance-then-purge (see chapters on Iran / mirror).

**Register:** Tendencies and mechanisms, not a law that every progressive and every Muslim form a bloc.

---

## Already on the table (baseline)

These were established in conversation before the longer list:

1. **Common enemy** — West / liberal order / “Zionist” / existing civic procedure.  
2. **Shared language** — oppression, resistance, illegitimacy of the current order; speak the same political tongue without shared metaphysics.  
3. **Structural anti-correspondence habits** — truth as instrument of the cause; reality may not freely veto the package.  
4. **Instrumental use** — power packages use others for power; nearest useful partner is often easiest to coordinate with.  
5. **Complementary roles** — left as shield (narrative cover, two-tier scrutiny, institutional access); hard package as sword (organization, demography, willingness to dominate when able).  
6. **Formal dual codes** — membership-relative morals (in-group purity / out-group license; or sex-class purity loading). Overlap of *form*, not full content of sexual or legal programs.  
7. **Not full principle compatibility** — alliance is not identity of the five answers; residual package conflict remains.  
8. **Alliance, then purge** — temporary joint action against a shared target; when monopoly of force is available, the harder or better-positioned package sorts the partner (Iran “Red and Black” as structural warning).

## Other commonalities that push cooperation

### 1. Shared enemy is thicker than one slogan

Not only “the West,” but often the same **concrete blockers**:

- Neutral rule of law and equal citizenship  
- Borders and immigration control as legitimate  
- Israel as durable liberal-adjacent success under fire  
- Free inquiry that embarrasses sacred or identity claims  
- Bourgeois family / sexual norms that neither revolutionary socialism nor political Islam fully owns  

When the *same institutions* obstruct both packages, coordination is cheap.

### 2. Both are civilizational / totalizing, not hobby politics

Both tend to treat politics as **moral totality**: who may speak, who may rule, how children are formed, what counts as justice. That makes them rivals of a **limited procedural republic** more than of each other—until victory. Two totalizers facing a non-totalizer often ally first.

### 3. History-as-guilt vs history-as-humiliation

- Progressive dialect: West as permanent debtor (colonialism, racism, patriarchy).  
- Islamist dialect: ummah as humiliated by West / Crusades / Israel / secular rulers.  

Different stories, **same usable emotion**: resentment + moral permission to break ordinary restraints against the designated oppressor. Coalitions love shared permission structures.

**Lens note:** Much Middle Eastern politics over recent decades reads more clearly through **victim / humiliation** (status, narrative, redress of shame) than through a simple win–loss ledger of material interests alone. Humiliation politics can dominate even when “winning” in conventional terms is unclear or secondary.

### 4. Preference for group subject over limited individual under law

- Left: class / race / gender coalition as political subject.  
- Political Islam: ummah / creed community as political subject.  

The frame’s unit (limited agent under procedural symmetry) is a shared obstacle. “Collective subject first” is a real commonality even when the collectives differ.

### 5. Hierarchy of victims (and of contempt)

Both run **sorted moral worth**:

- Progressive: punching up/down; favored identity gets narrative protection.  
- Islamist hard package: Muslim vs kafir; sometimes free vs slave; in-group honor vs out-group license.  

The *content* of the ladder differs; the habit of **unequal moral protection** makes two-tier enforcement feel normal to both—and makes progressive two-tier policing legible as “solidarity” rather than injustice.

### 6. Anti-merit / anti-thrival strains (partial, not universal)

Not all leftists, not all Muslims—but strong subcurrents:

- Outcome equity, anti-“excellence as oppression,” degrowth / anti-natal warmth on parts of the left.  
- Fate-sharing, anti-success, envy of high-performing out-groups in some Islamist and post-colonial rhetorics; also simple preference for sacral order over competence order.  

Where **thriving under open procedure** is suspect, high-performing open societies and high-performing minorities become shared irritants. That aligns hostility even without a joint five-year plan.

### 7. Control of speech and sacred offense (explicit or implicit blasphemy codes)

Both run **blasphemy codes**—named as such in Islamic law and politics, often unnamed but enforced on the progressive left.

Both often want:

- blasphemy-like protection for their sacred (identity or God / revelation),  
- punishment or stigma for description that harms the cause,  
- “harm” defined as challenge to the package.  

Classical liberal free speech is a shared solvent. That produces natural cooperation on campus codes, hate-speech expansion, and policing of cartoons, lectures, or crime statistics.

**Green (explicit sacred):** Insult Muhammad or burn a Quran in many Muslim-majority states—or, in practice, in parts of Europe under intimidation and selective enforcement—and the response is not a seminar. Life, limb, or permanent exile from normal civic safety can be on the line. The sacred text and the Prophet are not up for ordinary criticism the way a policy paper is.

**Red (implicit sacred):** Biological sex and related claims about trans ideology function as protected dogma in large progressive institutions. Speak ordinary empirical points and the machinery of cancellation, HR, and reputational destruction activates. James Damore’s Google memo stated comparatively boring, literature-adjacent claims about group averages and interests; the institutional response was figurative burning at the stake—termination plus moral contamination—not rebuttal under open inquiry.

**Asymmetric “book banning” rhetoric:** Progressive dialect often treats restrictions on pornographic or sexually explicit material in elementary schools as outrageous “book banning,” while works that violate progressive sacred narratives (e.g. trying to purchase or platform *The Camp of the Saints* and similar forbidden-adjacent titles) meet social and commercial taboo. The pattern is not neutral free-speech maximalism. It is **protection of one sacred set and exposure of another**.

**Alliance implication:** Each side already believes some speech is *unspeakable* and some speakers *deserve ruin*. That makes joint campaigns against “hate speech,” blasphemy-adjacent laws, and institutional punishment of dissent feel natural—even when the *content* of the sacred differs (God/Prophet vs identity/biology taboos). Shared method: **sacralize, then punish desecration.**

### 8. Entryism and institutional capture as method

Neither needs a majority of private belief first. Both understand:

- schools, HR, parties, NGOs, mosques, student societies as **power bases**,  
- demographic and organizational patience,  
- using the host’s rules until the host can be rewritten.  

Shared *method* (capture, not only debate) creates operational sympathy even when end-states diverge.

### 9. Revolutionary time horizon

Both can justify present cruelty or double standards by a **future rectification** (equity end-state / divine or caliphal order). Procedural liberals look pedantic; fellow eschatologists look serious. Temporary alliance feels strategic, not hypocritical, inside end-state ethics.

### 10. Demographic and street politics

In Western cities:

- Left parties need blocs, moral energy, and “diversity” credentials.  
- Islamist and communalist entrepreneurs need cover, funding channels, and protection from equal scrutiny.  

That is **interest alignment** more than theology—machine politics. Red–green is often a **vote + narrative + street** deal before it is a seminar on Marx and fiqh.

### 11. Anti-national particularism (ironic twin)

- Progressive: nation as xenophobia; open solidarity with global oppressed.  
- Political Islam: nation secondary to ummah; local patriotism suspect if it blocks creed politics.  

Both weaken **citizen-tier** loyalty that the frame treats as real. The nation-state as peer set becomes a common target (until an Islamist state wants its own borders hard).

### 12. Feminism as optional rhetoric, women as terrain

Not “both love women.” Both often treat **women’s status as political terrain**:

- Left: export “our” gender story or excuse theirs under cultural relativism.  
- Islamist: in-group control; out-group women differently valued.  

Progressive reluctance to police Islamic gender regimes (fear of racism) is a **one-way bridge**: green gains space; red keeps its self-image as defender of the marginalized. That asymmetry *produces* alliance behavior even without mutual affection.

### 13. Epistemic closure under sacred texts of different kinds

- Left: theory can be unfalsifiable (disparity = oppression; dissent = fragility).  
- Hard religious package: revelation / unbound will outranks observation.  

Shared habit: **reality may not veto the cause.** That makes joint action against “fact people” natural.

### 14. Aesthetic of resistance

Keepsakes of struggle: keffiyeh, fist, martyr posters, “by any means.” Youth culture and activist culture reward **oppositional identity**. Red and green both sell belonging-through-enmity more easily than “maintain the bridge and the books.”

## Weaker or false “commonalities” (do not lean on these)

| Claim | Problem |
|-------|---------|
| Same economics | Islamists often market-tolerant or clientelist; left often redistributive |
| Same sexual program | Dual-code *form* ≠ same bedroom rules |
| Same God or same metaphysics | Category error (frame already warns) |
| Same view of women as policy | Surface “protect women” can mean opposite procedures |
| Permanent friendship | Alliance-then-purge; Iran is the warning |

## Causal stack (one model)

```text
Shared obstruction (liberal procedural order)
    + shared permission (resentment / victim hierarchy / end-state ethics)
    + shared methods (capture, speech control, street + institutions)
    + complementary assets (votes, morality police of racism, organizational discipline, demography)
    → red–green coordination
    → residual package conflict → purge or domination when monopoly is available
```

More predictive than “they believe the same things.”

## Frame-altitude summary (if ever used in book1-5)

Highest-value commonalities at **framework** level:

1. Hostility to knowable reality as veto  
2. Hostility to limited individual under symmetric procedure  
3. Membership- or narrative-relative justice  
4. End-state permission to break present rules  
5. Common enemy = the frame’s public order  
6. Blasphemy codes (explicit or implicit): some claims may not be tested in public without ruin  

Sex dual codes, Palestine symbolism, entryism, humiliation politics, Damore / Quran / school-library double standards: **illustrations**, not the definition of the alliance.

## Break points (why the tendency is not fusion)

Useful when testing the model; alliance is real *and* limited:

- Apostasy and exit from Islam  
- Gay rights and pride politics  
- Blasphemy vs absolute speech  
- Jewish success / Israel as non-negotiable for large progressive factions vs eliminationist strands  
- Women’s exit, forced hijab, honor violence when description is forced into the open  
- Who holds the monopoly after the shared enemy falls (purge logic)
