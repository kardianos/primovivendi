## Latest formulation

**Purity systems** sort people into clean and unclean by membership or supposed nature, then load moral procedure from that sort. In hard Islamic packages, Muslim standing can mark full protection and kafir standing reduced reciprocity. In a major progressive-feminist dialect, female sexual agency is coded clean and male desire, gaze, and initiation unclean—even when the observable pattern is ordinary human sexuality under different stories. Soft traditional storytelling can err from another direction: the story-tale charming princess as pure by default, with vice outsourced to witches, stepmothers, or “bad men.” Reality is not the standard; the caste or the tale is.

**Virtue is not purity-as-default, and it is not a technical inventory of acts avoided.** A woman can be chaste or unchaste, modest or immodest, and still fail or succeed at what thrival requires: truthfulness, self-command, reliability in family and civic trust, orientation to life continuing well. Technical abstinence from this or that act does not make someone good. The reverse error is just as important: women *can* be virtuous in body and heart. That is achievement and habit, not ontology and not a halo awarded at birth. The same holds for men. Virtue is cultivated in both sexes. It may take sex-typical forms. It is not factory settings in either.

**Formation is always happening.** In a strong, loving family much of it can be almost implicit: the child absorbs limits, loyalty, work, and care without a seminar. That does not mean nature handed her a finished moral character. It means good institutions did formative work quietly. The mirror case proves the point from the other side. The woman who enforces in-group honor and helps maintain her husband’s sexual domination of out-group captives was formed too—by doctrine, household, fear, status, and habit. She is not a blank page and not a pure essence corrupted only by outsiders. She is a developed agent inside a package. “Naturally virtuous woman” fails both cases: it flatters the princess and cannot explain the collaborator.

**Human nature is neither blank slate nor fixed script.** People are not empty boards waiting for slogans. They arrive with expressed genes; every genome differs slightly; minds are plastic and moldable; around puberty the brain prunes connections so adult character is shaped by both endowment and formation. Twin studies keep reminding us how stubborn the biological side can be without licensing fatalism. The practical upshot for morals is simple enough for public life: design for limited, self-interested humans who can still be taught; do not design for angels, pure maidens, or infinitely rewriteable clay.

**Sex-typical patterns are real without becoming a purity caste.** On average men more readily run toward physical danger; many women experience that impulse as recklessness even while mammalian life often shows related divisions of risk and care. Those tendencies can inform roles, training, and mutual understanding. They do not make men unclean for desiring, or women clean by default, or either sex exempt from the same basic bans on force, fraud, and predation.

**Reality procedure** does not run on pure and impure sexes or creeds. It starts from what is: average differences in drive and initiation; attraction as normal rather than crime; violation and exploitation as wrong for everyone; sex as a consequential act that can bind lives and create children across years. In scopes that require trust (work, professional roles), tone down sexualization for both sexes. Do not treat ordinary male attraction as guilt, or ordinary female desirability as proof of moral status. Hold adults accountable for actions under known rules.

Where bonds are already real—marriage, long partnership—the governing facts are fidelity, care, non-violation, and shared responsibility. A careful ethic of willingness still matters: force, drugs, and predation are wrong. What does not follow is a single campus script of perpetual first-date verbal theater applied to every stage of every relationship as if strangers and spouses were the same moral situation.

**Reason and reality dominate, without naive rationalism.** First-order facts matter: bodies, incentives, limits, the possibility of children. Second- and third-order effects matter too: norms that shape trust between the sexes, family formation, and what children inherit a generation later. Faith is not being reduced to a social club with useful hobbies. For people who hold a religion as true, its demands bind as truth. For public argument under a shared world, the overlapping test is still open to believer and unbeliever alike: does this discipline train real self-command, protect the vulnerable, and support durable cooperation under human limits—or does it mainly enforce a purity story while reality pays later? Affirm what passes that test. Be cautious where costs compound over time. That is taking both truth and consequences seriously—not swapping revelation for vibes, and not swapping formation for fairytales.

That is not segregation of the impure. It is **one procedure under facts**, with virtue as formation rather than halo.

### 2. Fuller thinking

#### 2.1 What the formulation is really about

On the surface it talks about sex, gaze, hijab, princess stories. Underneath it is about **how public morals get their warrant**:

| Warrant | Pattern | Failure mode |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| Purity / caste | Clean vs unclean by membership or “nature” | Procedure changes by who you are; reality secondary |
| Fairytale essence | One sex (or age/role) born already good | Formation denied; collaborator unintelligible |
| Reality procedure | Same basic bans and duties under facts, scoped honestly | Harder to sloganize; must face limits and time |

The sexual material is a **high-resolution exemplum** of a general disease: justice and virtue redefined as sorting the pure from the impure instead of holding limited humans to knowable rules in a consequential world.

#### 2.2 Dual code (same agent, two procedures)

Not: half the left / half of Islam.  
Yes: one coherent membership ethic can demand strict control of “ours” and reduced protection (or predation) toward “theirs.” Progressive hybrids can code her sexual display as clean and his looking as unclean while the human pattern is continuous. Soft tradition can code the girl pure by default. These are different contents, **same formal sin against procedural symmetry and against “not naturally good.”**

#### 2.3 Virtue vs purity

- Purity-as-default: ontology or untouched status.  
- Purity-as-checklist: technical avoidance of named acts.  
- Virtue (frame sense): cultivated capacity to thrive and to support others’ thriving—truth, limits, duty, fair procedure, generational transmission.

A person can “pass” a purity checklist and fail thrival. A person can be sexually disciplined and still cruel, idle, or anti-truth. Conversely, virtue can include bodily self-command without reducing goodness to virginity mythology.

#### 2.4 Formation cuts both ways

Good families form virtue partly implicitly. Bad packages form vice and collaboration explicitly or by habit. That blocks:

- blank slate (“only society wrote them”),  
- natural angel (“she would have been pure but for X”),  
- pure victim essentialism that cannot assign agency where agency is real.

#### 2.5 Nature without fatalism

Genes differ; minds are plastic; development prunes and trains. Twin evidence supports non-blank-slate anthropology without erasing formation. Public order designs for **limited teachable humans**, not rewriteable software and not born saints.

#### 2.6 Sex-typical patterns without purity caste

Averages (e.g. male readiness for physical danger; related mammalian splits of risk and care) may inform understanding and voluntary roles. They must not become:

- man unclean for desire,  
- woman clean by essence,  
- exemption from force/fraud rules for either sex.

#### 2.7 Religion without social-club reduction

Believers may hold religious sexual ethics as *true*. Public reason under the frame does not convert churches into utility clubs. The overlapping civic test is narrower: knowable world, human limits, thrival, tiers, procedure—and willingness to let second- and third-order effects over time count as evidence. Affirm disciplines that pass; caution where generational costs compound. That is not “religion is useful vibes.”

#### 2.8 Consent brand vs non-violation

Willingness matters (force, drugs, predation are out). A single campus first-date verbal script applied to all stages of all relationships is a bad universal. Prefer: **non-violation + accountability + scope** (stranger, courtship, marriage are not one situation).

### 3. The bar this note must eventually meet (and does not yet)

The largest challenge is **altitude**.

**Wrong altitude (current risk):**  
A semi-specific positive sexual culture—how to date, how to talk in marriage, what to tone down at work, how to feel about attraction. Useful as illustration; **not** the book’s job as a five-principle frame.

**Right altitude (target):**  
Framework claims only:

1. **Public order and public reason should align with consequential reality** (P1 + public square).  
2. **Humans are limited**; first-order reason is necessary; **second- and third-order effects are hard** and still real—naive rationalism fails (P1 + P2 + time).  
3. **What goodness, for this conservatism, must include** at minimum: life continuing and going well; formation of virtue in limited people; refusal of purity castes that break equal basic procedure (P2 + P3 + P5).  
4. **Minimum social stance:** same fundamental bans and protections do not flip by creed or sex-class story; membership-relative justice is not civic justice; virtue is cultivated in both sexes; attraction and sex-typical averages are facts to design around, not guilt or halo.

The latest formulation **approaches** that bar in the closing paragraphs and then **slides** into exemplum-level positives (workplace tone, marriage governance, campus consent critique). Those slides are fine in a working note. They are **not yet** a chapter-ready frame insert.

**Hopeful path:** keep exempla short and labeled as illustrations; state the four framework claims in frame vocabulary; let sexual dual codes be *one* illustration beside non-sexual purity systems if needed (outcome purity, sacred political purity, etc.).
