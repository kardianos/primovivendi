## 1. Durnovo memorandum (1914) — war → social revolution

**File:** `durnovo_memorandum_1914.txt`  
**Role:** Pre-1917 warning from former Interior Minister P. N. Durnovo.

### Social revolution in the defeated country

> …there must inevitably break out in the defeated country a social revolution which, by the very nature of things, will spread to the country of the victor… That these troubles will be of a social, and not a political, nature cannot be doubted…

### Masses and political rights (elite isolation)

> The opponents of the government have no popular support. The people see no difference between a government official and an intellectual. The Russian masses, whether workmen or peasants, are not looking for political rights, which they neither want nor comprehend. The peasant dreams of obtaining a gratuitous share of somebody else’s land; the workman, of getting hold of the entire capital and profits of the manufacturer.

**Writer use:** Brittle order + redistribution dreams + war stress. Not a full sociology; a primary warning of *direction*.

## 2. Lenin, April Theses (1917) — rupture blueprint

**File:** `lenin_april_theses_1917.txt`  
**Source note in file:** marxists.org / Pravda 7 Apr 1917.  
**Full text is short — prefer full file for complete theses.** Essential points:

### Second stage of revolution (thesis 2)

> The specific feature of the present situation in Russia is that the country is passing from the first stage of the revolution—which… placed power in the hands of the bourgeoisie—to its second stage, which must place power in the hands of the proletariat and the poorest sections of the peasants.

### No support for Provisional Government (thesis 3)

> No support for the Provisional Government; the utter falsity of all its promises should be made clear… Exposure in place of the impermissible, illusion-breeding “demand” that this government, a government of capitalists, should cease to be an imperialist government.

### Soviets as only form of revolutionary government (thesis 4, opening)

> The masses must be made to see that the Soviets of Workers’ Deputies are the only possible form of revolutionary government…

**Writer use:** Explicit **break / second stage / no support** for the provisional channel — contrast reform that works inside constraints. Read remaining theses in full file for land, banks, commune-state themes.

## 4. Economic grade — Cheremukhin et al. (2017)

**File:** `cheremukhin_yale_russian_economy_1913_1928_or_later.txt`  
**Paper:** Anton Cheremukhin, Mikhail Golosov, Sergei Guriev, Aleh Tsyvinski, “The Industrialization and Economic Development of Russia through the Lens of a Neoclassical Growth Model,” *Review of Economic Studies* 84 (2017): 613–649.

### Data hole 1914–27 (primary gap acknowledged)

> Very little data exist for 1914–27 and we omit this period in our analysis. The structure of the Russian economy in 1928 looked very similar to the one in 1913.

### Collectivization response (livestock, production, famine)

> By the early 1930s, the Soviet government had attempted to socialize all agricultural livestock and ban private agricultural markets. Peasants were forced to join newly formed collectives. Peasants responded with widespread slaughtering of livestock; agricultural production plummeted, and the severe famine of 1932–33 followed.

### Productivity cost of Soviet transformation

Abstract/findings language in paper:

> Soviet industrial transformation after 1928 was achieved primarily by reducing such frictions, albeit coinciding with a significantly lower performance of productivity in both agricultural and non-agricultural sectors.

And:

> while Soviet industrialization and collectivization policies resulted in a significant structural change, they were also disastrous in terms of productivity in either sector; on balance, the Soviet economy did not outperform the counterfactual.

**Writer use:** Peer-reviewed secondary, not eyewitness. Pair with Lenin program as intent; this as thrival/productivity grade of forced socialization path. Do not invent 1917–21 death tolls without a named secondary.
