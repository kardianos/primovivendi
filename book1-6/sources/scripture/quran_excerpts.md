# Qur’an excerpts (standard verse numbering)

**Use:** Part 1 H8 / Part 2 packages — political-theological structure, jizya, concealment under fear, persons ≠ packages.  
**Translation note:** English below follows common **Sahih International**-style wording widely used in academic citation. For publication, check against a printed Sahih International or another named translation (Yusuf Ali, Pickthall, etc.) and cite the edition.  
**Arabic:** Not reproduced here; verse numbers are standard Cairo/Kufic numbering.  
**Rule for the book:** Quote packages and classical *receptions*; do not treat every Muslim person as the package. Compatible secondary-cause traditions exist (falsafa, some Muʿtazilī positions, Ibn Rushd) — see `philosophy/islam_secondary_causes_brief.md`.

## Jizya / fighting People of the Book (political subordination structure)

### Qur’an 9:29 (at-Tawbah)

**Sahih International (typical English):**

> Fight those who do not believe in Allah or in the Last Day and who do not consider unlawful what Allah and His Messenger have made unlawful and who do not adopt the religion of truth from those who were given the Scripture — [fight] until they give the jizyah willingly while they are humbled.

**Writer use:**
- Classical political packages read this as a structured subordination of non-Muslim scriptuaries under Muslim rule, with jizya as a distinctive tax/status mark — not identical to ordinary citizen tax under equal citizenship.
- Ottoman and other historical practice treated jizya as regular revenue from non-Muslim subjects (secondary histories; not in this verse alone).
- **Package test:** tiers grounded in creed + coercion of exit/status differ from parent/child tiers under shared civic procedure.
- **Not:** “every Muslim today enforces 9:29.” Officials aim at texts, institutions, demonstrated political programs.

## Taking protectors from disbelievers / concealment under fear

### Qur’an 3:28 (Āl ʿImrān)

**Sahih International (typical English):**

> Let not believers take disbelievers as allies [or protectors] rather than believers. And whoever [of you] does that has nothing with Allah, except when taking precaution against them in prudence. And Allah warns you of Himself, and to Allah is the [final] destination.

**Writer use:**
- Classical discussion of *taqiyya* / precautionary concealment in some schools and periods draws on this and related verses (also 16:106).
- Epistemic worry for civic trust: if doctrine permits managing appearance under fear, the outsider needs conduct and institutional tests, not only verbal profession.
- **Symmetric caution:** Other traditions have mental reservation casuistry; apply procedure to acts and institutional rules, not blood.

### Qur’an 16:106 (an-Nahl)

**Sahih International (typical English):**

> Whoever disbelieves in Allah after his belief… except for one who is forced [to renounce his religion] while his heart is secure in faith. But those who [willingly] open their breasts to disbelief, upon them is wrath from Allah, and for them is a great punishment.

**Writer use:** Forced utterance under compulsion vs willing apostasy — relevant to duress, not a free pass for political fraud in peacetime civic dealing. Keep distinctions honest.

## No compulsion in religion (often cited against coercion packages)

### Qur’an 2:256 (al-Baqarah)

**Sahih International (typical English):**

> There shall be no compulsion in [acceptance of] the religion. The right course has become clear from the wrong…

**Writer use:**
- Used by many Muslims for liberty of conscience.
- Hard political packages still coexist with other verses (e.g. 9:29) and with historical dhimmi structures — **internal diversity is real**.
- Book method: do not pretend one verse erases another; ask which package is institutionalized.

## Relation to non-Muslims who do not fight you

### Qur’an 60:8–9 (al-Mumtaḥanah)

**Sahih International (typical English):**

> Allah does not forbid you from those who do not fight you because of religion and do not expel you from your homes — from being righteous toward them and acting justly toward them. Indeed, Allah loves those who act justly.  
> Allah only forbids you from those who fight you because of religion and expel you from your homes and aid in your expulsion — [forbids] that you make allies of them…

**Writer use:** Supports scoped justice and non-aggression toward peaceful non-Muslims in this reading. Pair with 9:29: traditions and polities weight these differently. Package = which verses + which institutions dominate.

## Fighting / fitnah language (context-sensitive)

### Qur’an 2:190–193 (al-Baqarah) — abbreviated pointer

Classical theme: fight those who fight you; fitnah worse than killing; stop if they cease — highly contested in modern interpretation (defensive vs expansionist readings).

**Writer use:** Do not invent a single “Islam says.” Show that **political packages** pick interpretive paths; grade the path by thrival, procedure, and reality-tests when claims are empirical.

## Citation hygiene

1. Give surah:ayah (e.g. 9:29).  
2. Name translation.  
3. Separate: (a) text; (b) classical fiqh reception; (c) modern political use; (d) what this book’s axioms require for shared public order.  
4. Never equate “verse exists” with “every believer is an enemy.”
