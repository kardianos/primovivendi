# Bible excerpts (public domain / standard English)

**Use:** Part 1 H9 / Part 2 packages — two-books, creation mandate, procedure, judgment, household duty.  
**Translation note:** Unless marked otherwise, wording follows common public-domain **King James Version (KJV)** or closely equivalent traditional English. Verify against a printed Bible if quoting in publication.  
**Rule for the book:** These passages are **data for argument**, not a claim that this book settles divine authority. Testable claims still go to reality (dictionary M / H9).

---

## Creation mandate and life continuing

### Genesis 1:28 (KJV)

> And God blessed them, and God said unto them, Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth, and subdue it: and have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over every living thing that moveth upon the earth.

**Writer use:** Life continuing and dominion/stewardship as a classical theistic parallel to “continue and continue well.” Not identical to the book’s axiom, but compatible in orientation.

### Genesis 9:1 (KJV)

> And God blessed Noah and his sons, and said unto them, Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth.

## Creation as witness (two-books epistemology)

### Romans 1:20 (KJV)

> For the invisible things of him from the creation of the world are clearly seen, being understood by the things that are made, even his eternal power and Godhead; so that they are without excuse.

**Writer use:** Nature teaches; correspondence-compatible theism. Contrast scripturalism that severs creation as witness.

### Psalm 19:1 (KJV)

> The heavens declare the glory of God; and the firmament sheweth his handywork.

## Procedural symmetry (conduct)

### Matthew 7:12 (KJV)

> Therefore all things whatsoever ye would that men should do to you, do ye even so to them: for this is the law and the prophets.

**Writer use:** Reciprocal procedure test — same rule applied to self and other.

## Judgment vs hypocrisy (not non-judgment as foundation)

### Matthew 7:1–5 (KJV)

> Judge not, that ye be not judged.  
> For with what judgment ye judge, ye shall be judged: and with what measure ye mete, it shall be measured to you again.  
> And why beholdest thou the mote that is in thy brother’s eye, but considerest not the beam that is in thine own eye?  
> Or how wilt thou say to thy brother, Let me pull out the mote out of thine eye; and, behold, a beam is in thine own eye?  
> Thou hypocrite, first cast out the beam out of thine own eye; and then shalt thou see clearly to cast out the mote out of thy brother’s eye.

**Writer use:** Condemns *hypocritical* judgment and demands self-application first — not a ban on all judgment. Ties to procedural symmetry in scrutiny.

## Mercy after wrong named (not permissiveness)

### John 8:10–11 (KJV)

> When Jesus had lifted up himself, and saw none but the woman, he said unto her, Woman, where are those thine accusers? hath no man condemned thee?  
> She said, No man, Lord. And Jesus said unto her, Neither do I condemn thee: go, and sin no more.

**Writer use:** Protection + command to stop the wrong. Mercy is not “no standard.”

## Household tier (explicit)

### 1 Timothy 5:8 (KJV)

> But if any provide not for his own, and specially for those of his own house, he hath denied the faith, and is worse than an unbeliever.

**Writer use:** Tiered duty; household first. Hard line against universal action-duty that starves the near.

---

## Reactive duty / neighbor by deed (not pure universalism)

### Luke 10:29–37 (summary for writer; full parable in any Bible)

Expert asks “Who is my neighbor?” Jesus answers with the Samaritan who aids a half-dead man on the road — **immediate life-threat**, costly action, then Jesus reframes: who *proved* neighbor.

**Writer use:** Reactive duty under crisis ≠ permanent identical claim of every distant person on your full budget. Pair with Bethany hierarchy (Matthew 26:11: “ye have the poor always with you; but me ye have not always”) for tiered proactive priority.

### Matthew 26:11 (KJV)

> For ye have the poor always with you; but me ye have not always.

## Enemy disposition (not identical household provision)

### Matthew 5:44 (KJV)

> But I say unto you, Love your enemies, bless them that curse you, do good to them that hate you, and pray for them which despitefully use you, and persecute you.

**Writer use:** Often misused as open-border or self-destruction politics. In context of personal disposition and prayer; not a command to fund those who destroy your household (pair with 1 Tim 5:8 and enemy recognition). Greek *agapate* is will-love, not identical action-budget to spouse/child.
