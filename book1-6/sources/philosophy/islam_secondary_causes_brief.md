# Islamic intellectual diversity on causality — brief (fairness pack)

**Use:** When discussing occasionalism / Ashʿarī packages vs correspondence.  
**Rule:** Persons ≠ packages; traditions contain multiple schools.

---

## 1. Ashʿarī occasionalism (simplified)

- Classical Ashʿarī kalām (associated with al-Ashʿarī; developed in later manuals; al-Ghazālī’s *Tahāfut al-Falāsifa* is a major polemic against falāsifa causal necessity) holds that created “causes” do not necessitate effects by intrinsic power; God creates the event.  
- Regularities are often described as divine habit (*ʿāda*) — so **prediction can still work** if habit is stable.  
- **Book tension:** If public epistemology treats will as unbound by stable order in practice, experiment and shared veto of false claims weaken. If habit is rock-steady, operational science can proceed while metaphysics differs.  
- **Writer honesty:** Do not claim Ashʿarī thought made all prediction impossible; claim risk to **secondary-cause realism** and to packages that subordinate empirical correction to unbound sacral politics.

**Primary to fetch for publication:** al-Ghazālī, *The Incoherence of the Philosophers* (English: Marmura translation common).

---

## 2. Falsafa / Ibn Rushd (Averroes)

- Ibn Rushd (*Tahāfut al-Tahāfut*) defends causal connection and Aristotelian natural philosophy against al-Ghazālī’s critique.  
- **Writer use:** Correspondence-compatible strands exist inside Islamic civilization’s high intellectual history.  
- Package test still applies to **political** programs that deny exit, equal civic procedure, or reality’s veto.

**Primary to fetch:** Ibn Rushd, *Incoherence of the Incoherence* (Van Den Bergh translation classic).

---

## 3. Muʿtazilī rationalism (pointer)

- Early theological school emphasizing divine justice and human reason/responsibility (internal diversity over centuries).  
- **Writer use:** Another reminder that “Islam” is not one epistemology.

## 5. Minimal fairness paragraph (template for manuscript)

> The Islamic intellectual tradition is not one mind. Strands that defend real secondary causes and a stable created order can sit with public correspondence the way two-books Christianity can. The quarrel is with packages that make unbound sacral or clerical will the constitution of public truth, block exit, and install two-tier standing — graded by texts, institutions, and conduct, not by surname.
