# Standards in making — mechanisms note (not a proof dossier)

**Use:** Part 1 section G.  
**Job:** Remind the writer what G claims and does not claim. Optional pointers for later reading—not required quotes for draft.

---

## Claim level

- **In manuscript:** time effects, perverse incentives, second- and third-order effects on **skill transmission** and **what gets paid/credentialed**.  
- **Not in manuscript as proven:** a complete verdict on modernism, every school, or every decade.

---

## Mechanism checklist (must be able to state without a museum tour)

1. **Time:** Stop drilling a skill → next cohort cannot do what the last could.  
2. **Incentives:** If shock + theory essay outpays mastery, careers follow.  
3. **2nd order:** Curriculum drops hard fundamentals.  
4. **3rd order:** Fewer people can build, finish, or judge competent work; words replace hands.

---

## Domains to flag (look later if expanding)

- Apprenticeship and shop classes cut.  
- Architecture graduates weak on construction detailing.  
- Conservatory/art-school hours: drill vs theory-only.  
- Grant and tenure criteria: novelty language vs demonstrated skill.  
- Public realm: maintenance and finish quality of housing and streets.

---

## Optional secondary reading (polemical; mark as such)

- Institutional/status critiques of art-world theory capture (e.g. Tom Wolfe, *The Painted Word*—polemic, not gospel).  
- Standard craft/formation literature and trade pedagogy.  
- Do not treat one polemic as closed proof in the book’s voice.

---

## Falsifier (from plan G5)

Widespread competent making without serious prior-standard training, or clear recovery of hard training and public reward for mastery after a negation-heavy phase → narrow or drop the risk claim.
