# Hermetic argument — analysis

**Use:** Part 2 B; Part 1 M5.

---

## Definition

A **hermetic argument** protects a system so that:

- Every **success** is credited to the ideal or package.  
- Every **failure** is declared not a true instance (“not real X,” wrong implementers, external sabotage only).  
- Therefore the ideal never loses.

---

## Why it violates procedural symmetry

1. Cause is assigned by a **one-way valve**.  
2. The same rule of inference is not applied to good and bad outcomes.  
3. Under Part 1 B5 / M5, success and failure must be graded by the same procedure.

**Symmetric repair:**  
If no failure can count against X, no success may be credited to X either — *or* (better) drop the valve and keep a real ledger of implementations under known conditions.

---

## Connection to models

A calculation scheme that “works” can still rest on a wrong story (astronomy chain). Hermetic politics treats every good quarter as proof of the ontology and every famine as “not real socialism / not real religion / not real reform.”

---

## Writer test

- List three claimed successes and three failures of the package.  
- Ask whether the theory of cause is the same in all six.  
- If not, the argument is hermetic.
