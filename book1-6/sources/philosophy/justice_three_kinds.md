# Three kinds of “justice” — analysis

**Use:** Part 1 B5; Part 2 C.

---

## 1. Procedural symmetry (this book’s default)

**Definition:** Within an honest scope or role, the same rules and methods of judgment apply to each person in the same position.

**Examples:** same criminal procedure for rich and poor defendants; same professional standard for licensees; same household rule for each child of the same age class; same critical standard for favored and disfavored scientific claims.

**Not:** Parent and child as identical roles. Citizen and foreign invader as identical process when the invader rejects the shared order by force.

**Why it fits limited humans:**
1. People need predictable expectations to cooperate.  
2. Predictability dies when rules are personal.  
3. Outcome equality requires continuous unequal intervention.  
4. Feud equality of harm regenerates conflict.

**Primary cross-ref:** Madison Federalist 51 — ambition counteracting ambition; if men were angels no government needed (`usa/federalist_51_excerpts.txt`). Design for non-angels, not for outcome sameness.

---

## 2. Outcome symmetry (equity as identical end-states)

**Definition:** Justice = same results across persons or groups. Disparity is treated as automatic proof of unjust process.

**Chain of failure:**
1. Human talent, effort, luck, and choice differ (B7, B8).  
2. Forcing identical ends requires unequal rules (quotas, suppressed excellence, punished success).  
3. A class of equalizers gains power.  
4. Truth about causes of disparity becomes taboo (speech + hermetic).  
5. Thrival declines when competence is punished.

**Not a ban on all remedy:** Proven theft, fraud, coercion can be remedied under same rules. The error is **results as the definition of justice**.

---

## 3. Value symmetry (equal return in kind)

**Definition:** Justice = balance the ledger of harms/benefits in kind (eye for eye as social OS).

**Failure:**
1. Backward-looking.  
2. Measurement of “equal harm” is contested → endless feud.  
3. Does not build forward cooperation or formation.  
4. May have limited place in proportional criminal sentencing debates; fails as the master theory of social order.

---

## 4. Decision table for writers

| Situation | Prefer |
|-----------|--------|
| Court verdict | Procedure |
| Wage gaps without fraud | Do not assume injustice from gap alone |
| Proven embezzlement | Remedy under law (procedure + restoration) |
| Blood feud | Reject value symmetry as OS |
| Scientific dispute | Same critical standard (procedure in inquiry) |
