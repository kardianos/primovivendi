# Life is good — long argument (for writers)

**Use:** Part 1 B3, F.  
**Status:** Spelled-out reasoning. Not a claim of pure deduction from is to ought without a choice.

---

## 1. The question

What should public ethics and personal orientation treat as the good for human life and society?

This book’s answer: **human life should continue and continue well** (survive and thrive).

---

## 2. Why some answer is required

1. Description of the world (is) does not alone pick a goal (ought).  
2. Every society still acts on *some* standard: thrival, purity, equity, sacred empire, harm-min, extinction-as-kindness, etc.  
3. Hidden standards are exploited. Name yours.

---

## 3. Motivations (not fake pure proof)

### 3.1 Holders of value
Without living persons, justice, beauty, knowledge, and kindness have no holders.  
A standard that destroys its holders as its ideal end is self-eliminating as a public ethic.

### 3.2 Dead earth vs consent-only
1. Suppose value is exhausted by effects on existing consenting subjects.  
2. Empty world: no subjects wronged.  
3. Full flourishing world: no prior subject made better who would have existed as the same subject in the empty world.  
4. Ranking dead = living is then “coherent” only if existence of life is not itself a good.  
5. This book rejects that indifference.  
6. **Affirm:** flourishing human life is good as standard — not merely good-for-someone already on a ledger.

### 3.3 Ancestral chain
Every living person is the end of a chain of parents who continued life under difficulty.  
Celebrating the end of the chain while consuming its gifts is civilizational free-riding (broken link).

### 3.4 Selection fact (not itself a duty)
Practices and peoples that never replace themselves tend to disappear.  
That explains why surviving cultures are often pro-natal in practice; it does not alone create moral duty. Duty comes from the affirmed axiom + participation layer.

---

## 4. Layers (do not collapse)

| Layer | Content | Status |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Axiom | Continue and continue well | Affirmed |
| Corollaries | Persistent institutions; honor chain; transmit platform; do not celebrate extinction | Strong given axiom + reality |
| Participation | Have children or raise up; make continuation honorable | Affirmed under the vision; not “everyone drafted into every vital job” |

**Calibration:** full life, not headcount maximising. Biology forbids some paths; raising-up remains.

---

## 5. Rival axioms (name them)

- Harm reduction as highest good → can terminate in anti-natal “cleanest world is empty of sufferers.”  
- Outcome equity as the good → flattens thrival signals.  
- Sacred political unity as the good → procedure and truth become servants.  
- Life-neutral or life-negative fashion → footprint and extinction-as-virtue.

---

## 6. Cushions vs entitlement (F0/F1)

1. Under life-as-good, short shocks should not equal death when bridging is possible.  
2. That is thrival machinery.  
3. Treating insulation from all scarcity and all “no” as a permanent adult lifestyle free-rides on others’ work and breaks formation.  
4. Keep (1); refuse (3).

---

## 7. What not to write

- “Logic forces natalism without any axiom.”  
- “Anyone without biological children is evil” (false to raise-up layer and hard cases).  
- “Consent ethics is ‘valuing nothing’” — it values subjects; its cost is dead-earth indifference.
