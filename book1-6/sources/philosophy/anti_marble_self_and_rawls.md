# Particular persons, not marble-selves — analysis (anti-modular identity)

**Use:** Part 1 B8, D15; Part 2 C.  
**Fairness rule:** Distinguish (a) Rawls’s *Theory of Justice* as a technical device for choosing principles of basic structure from (b) the **popular political use** of veil/lottery talk that dissolves particular duty.

---

## 1. The marble-self claim (target)

**Popular form:**
- You did not choose parents, country, talents, sex, or era.  
- Therefore these are morally arbitrary.  
- Therefore advantages are unearned lottery prizes.  
- Therefore justice is redistribution until outcomes no longer reflect the lottery.  
- Family preference and national preference are biases to overcome.

**Metaphysical image:** A generic token (marble) that could have been anyone; only the costume differs.

---

## 2. What is true in the target

1. No one chooses their birth.  
2. Luck is real.  
3. Fraud, caste law, and fake “merit” that is only inheritance of corruption are real injustices under procedure.  
4. A pure egoist who denies all outer duty is not the book’s position.

---

## 3. Where the target fails (long chain)

1. **Existence of the valuer depends on the chain.**  
   The person who theorizes is the product of specific ancestors, language, and care.  
   There is no actual marble that “could have been” a different organism with the same identity.

2. **Non-identity (related point):**  
   Policies that change who is born do not make “the same person worse off” in a simple comparative sense. Popular lottery talk ignores this when it treats all inheritance as transferable chips.

3. **Duties attach to actual relations.**  
   Parent–child, debtor–creditor, citizen–polity are not arbitrary costumes; they are how limited beings survive (B4, B7).

4. **Gratitude is not theft.**  
   Receiving English, law, and vaccines without inventing them does not make inheritance “stolen” by default.  
   Broken-link consumption without transmission is the vice (D7).

5. **Outcome flattening destroys information and formation.**  
   If every failure is injustice, children cannot be allowed real tests (D15).  
   If every success is suspect, excellence dies (D5, E10).

6. **Global marble pool is not operable.**  
   Unlimited equal claim of all humans on every household budget is not performed by anyone; systems that claim it hide tiers or free-ride (B4).

7. **Different visions need different spaces.**  
   Forcing one jurisdiction to fully embody incompatible comprehensive doctrines produces coercion or a lying thin shell (E12).  
   Shared public floor: testable reality, life, limits, ranked duty, procedure — not identical private theologies.

---

## 4. Rawls — fair steelman and popular misuse

### Steelman (brief)
- Veil of ignorance: choose principles for basic structure without knowing your place, to block special pleading.  
- Difference principle and priority rules are about **institutions** of a society, not a claim that souls pre-exist as seabirds.  
- Rawls’s later *Law of Peoples* is not simple cosmopolitan outcome equality of all persons globally.

### Popular misuse this book answers
- “I could have been a seagull / anyone” as metaphysics of self.  
- “Any unequal result is injustice.”  
- Dissolution of family and national duty as moral progress.

**Writer:** Attack the misuse hard. If engaging academic Rawls, do not pretend the seagull is his careful view.

---

## 5. Allow failure; do not court failure

1. Competence requires real loss and retry.  
2. Cushioning every sting produces fragility.  
3. **Seeking** failure (neglect, ideological sabotage of children’s formation, envy-equality that breaks the strong) is vice under life-as-good.  
4. Picture: lose the race and train; do not break legs for equal finish lines; do not refuse coaching because winning would be privilege.
