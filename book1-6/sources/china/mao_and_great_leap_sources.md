# China / Mao — source pack for thrival under redesign and terror

**Use:** Part 2 history and packages — blank slate / perfectibility / redesign of man; discovered constraints (food, incentives); scale effects.  
**Honesty rule:** Prefer primary Mao texts for *intent* and secondary demographic/economic consensus for *grade*. This file mixes both and labels them.

---

## A. Primary: Mao on contradiction, remaking, and politics

### 1. “On Contradiction” (1937) — method

Mao argues contradiction is universal; principal contradiction shifts; analysis must be concrete.

**Writer use:** Not evil as a sentence. Shows a systematic ideology that treats social life as remakable through struggle — pairs with blank-slate / perfectibility packages when applied to agriculture and class.

Locate full text: standard *Selected Works of Mao Tse-tung* (Peking Foreign Languages Press editions are widely archived). Quote only from a named edition when publishing.

### 2. “On the People’s Democratic Dictatorship” (1949)

Theme: lean to one side; dictatorship over reactionaries; remold.

**Writer use:** Explicit dual code — democracy for the people, dictatorship for enemies of the revolution. Contrast Part 1 procedural symmetry within a shared civic pursuit.

### 3. Great Leap Forward orientation (1958 speeches / directives)

Public campaign themes (paraphrase of well-documented campaign lines; verify wording in *Selected Works* / contemporary People’s Daily before block-quoting):

- Catch up with Britain in steel and output on compressed timelines.  
- People’s communes; backyard furnaces; exaggerated harvest claims.  
- Politics in command over technical caution.

**Writer use:** Redesign by will against agronomic and incentive constraints. Links to Part 1 M (wrong model can “report” good numbers) and Part 2 form vs substance (commune form vs food production substance).

## B. Grade: famine and death (secondary consensus — cite carefully)

Scholarly demographic reconstructions of the Great Leap famine (1959–1961) vary in exact totals; they **do not** vary on direction:

- Mass excess mortality in the tens of millions is the mainstream range in serious historical demography (exact figures contested: e.g. works associated with Dikötter, Cao Shuji, Yang Jisheng, Ashton et al.).  
- Causes analyzed in literature: procurement from countryside, false production reports, commune incentives, violence against dissent, weather as partial not full explanation.

**For this book’s logic (not a census paper):**

1. Large-scale attempt to reorganize production and human motivation by political command.  
2. Local officials under pressure falsify success (hermetic + wrong model with “good” numbers).  
3. Reality answers as hunger and death.  
4. That is thrival failure under constraints — not a branding dispute.

**Writer:** In manuscript, pick **one** reputable secondary and stick to it with citation; do not invent a death toll. If no library access, say “scholarly estimates of excess deaths run to many millions” without a fake precise number.

**Primary-ish Chinese documentary collections:** *Zhou Enlai nianpu*, provincial archives used by Dikötter (*Mao’s Great Famine*), Yang Jisheng *Tombstone* (Chinese edition / English abridgment) — use if available; not vendored here.

## C. Cultural Revolution (brief)

**Primary theme (Mao / Central documents 1966+):** bombard headquarters; continuous revolution; struggle against capitalist roaders.

**Grade (consensus direction):** institutional chaos, persecution of officials and intellectuals, interrupted schooling, violence by factions; ended with arrest of Gang of Four (1976) and later reversal under Deng’s reform direction.

**Writer use:** Permanent revolution against stable procedure and competence; formation of youth as political weapon (ties Part 1 education/formation).

## D. Logical sequence (Mao case → book axioms)

1. Humans limited, self-interested, not blank (Part 1 B7).  
2. Agriculture and industry obey discovered constraints (Part 2 A).  
3. Command systems that deny those constraints produce false reports (Part 1 M2: numbers without true model).  
4. Scale: errors that a village experiment might survive kill when nationalized (Part 1 H10).  
5. Life-as-good (B3) grades the result: mass death is not a technical footnote.  
6. Procedure: no same rules for critic and loyalist under terror — rule by man (Part 2 C).
