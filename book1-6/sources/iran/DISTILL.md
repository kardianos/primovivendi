## 1. Khomeini, *Islamic Government* / Governance of the Jurist (1970)

**File:** `khomeini_islamic_government_1970.txt`  
**English materials:** Institute for Compilation and Publication of Imam Khomeini’s Works (translator/annotator per file header). Verify edition if publishing.

### Implementation, not legislation (load-bearing)

> …his task is not legislation, but the implementation of the divine laws that the Prophet (s) has promulgated. It is for this reason that the formation of a government and the establishment of executive organs are necessary. Belief in the necessity for these is part of the general belief in the Imamate…

**Writer use:** This is the package’s constitution of politics — not “Muslims are enemies,” but **public law as application of divine law**, not reciprocal legislation among citizens as sovereign peers.

### Divine precepts perpetual / need government

> 1. Action taken by the Holy Prophet (s) to establish a government;  
> 2. The fact that divine precepts are to be enforced perpetually; they are enacted not only for the time of the Holy Prophet (s); they are meant for all time.  
> 3. The nature and characteristic of Islamic laws and regulations like fiscal, national defense, and legal and penal precepts are such that they are not executable without a government.

**Writer use:** Permanent political enforcement claim — pairs with Part 1 H9 (testable thrival consequences of packages still gradeable).

### Related keywords in full text (search if expanding)

- `faqih` / governance of the jurist  
- opposition of monarchy to “laws of Islam” (polemic against hereditary monarchy in Islamic terms)  
- penal examples (lashings/stonings) in context of successor duties — quote only with surrounding context if used
