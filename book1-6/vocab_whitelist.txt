reality: what exists independent of wish, name, or vote (B1)
truth: correspondence to reality; a claim is true when it matches what is (B2)
correspondence: the match between a claim and what is
survive: continue to exist, as a person, household, or people (C1)
thrive: direction past bare continuation; order, skill, health, building, transmission (C2)
continue and continue well: the affirmed good (B3); use instead of any coinage
limited: finite time, knowledge, and reach; permanent human design input (B7)
self-interested: oriented to own survival and near kin; descriptive wiring, not prescribed egoism (B7)
not naturally good: virtue is trained and maintained, not factory settings (B7)
tiered priority: duties ranked self and household, then family, neighbors, community, polity, world (B4)
scope: a role, membership, or jurisdiction inside which procedure applies (B4)
procedural symmetry: same rules and methods of judgment within an honest scope (B5)
outcome symmetry: rival justice; same end-states, disparity treated as proof of injustice
value symmetry: rival justice; equal return in kind; feud ledger
discovered: arrangement that reappears under independent conditions because the problem remains (substance)
constructed: arrangement that could be otherwise without cancelling the problem (form)
picture: concrete instance that does causal work in the argument; paint with order, logic, vitality (W2)
chain: the if-then steps that force the next step of a claim (A3)
arc: the unit of thought a paragraph or chapter carries
package: a named set of premises, texts, and institutions; never a census of persons
persons are not packages: individuals are judged by commitments and conduct, not surnames
generic person: the fiction rejected in E0; a person with no family, lineage, or situation
cushion: time-bound bridge over a short shock (F0); not a way of life
platform: what took generations to build and can be consumed in far less time
formation: teaching and training that produce capability; never neutral (E6)
wrong: systematically fails across places and trials; no progressive domain (M3)
incomplete: succeeds inside a domain; successor keeps limit-case successes (M3)
enemy: defined by acts and programs against your people or shared process (E9)
opponent: shares enough process to be answered inside law and argument (E9)
mercy: chosen forbearance of full just consequence, after judgment (D9)
permissiveness: skipping judgment while calling the skip compassion (D9)
doomerism: analysis that reliably concludes in the analyst's own exemption from effort (D12)
dispositional love: general goodwill; cheap; scales wide (D13)
action-based love: costly time, money, attention; budgeted by tier (D13)
