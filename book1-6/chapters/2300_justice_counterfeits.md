## Justice counterfeits

### Three things called fair

%% [HIGH] [justice] [plan-gap] P2.C1 triad complete: keep procedure; refuse outcome; refuse value/feud. Steelmans both rivals. **Primary repair of Run-1 1100 justice CRIT**—axioms can stay positive-first if this chapter stays. Do not thin.

Three different things answer to the name fair, and a people that cannot tell them apart will be governed by whichever meaning the loudest claimant is pushing. The first is procedural symmetry: within an honest scope, the same rules and the same methods of judgment apply to every person in the same position. The second is outcome symmetry, lately named equity: justice means the same end-states across persons or groups, and any disparity between them counts as proof that the process was unjust. The third is value symmetry: justice means an equal return in kind, harm answered by equal harm. This book keeps the first. The second and third are counterfeits, and each counterfeit gets accepted because it borrows one true piece of the real thing.

Procedural symmetry is the meaning worth keeping. A scope is honest when it names a real role, membership, or jurisdiction inside which the rules apply: the same criminal procedure for the rich defendant and the poor one, the same licensing standard for every candidate for a trade, the same household rule for each child of the same age class, the same critical standard for the favored hypothesis and the disfavored one. Procedural symmetry does not pretend that every person stands in the same position. A parent and a child are different positions, and a citizen and an armed invader who rejects the shared order by force do not receive the same process. Limited and self-interested persons cooperate only when they can predict what follows from what they do, and prediction dies when rulers change the rule with the person. Same rules, same methods, inside an honest scope: that is what justice means before anything else is added.

Outcome symmetry has a case its holders would recognize. No one earns their parents, and when the children of one group reliably land on top while the children of another group reliably land at the bottom, a decent person suspects the results were arranged before the contest began. Gaps really do come from rigged arrangements: theft enforced as law, exclusions written into charters, fraud dressed up as merit. The demand to examine results is, at its best, a demand to check whether the procedure everyone praises was ever actually run.

The counterfeit enters when same results become the definition of justice and every disparity is treated as automatic proof of crime. The chain then runs on its own logic. Talent, effort, choice, and luck differ between real persons, so end-states differ under any honest procedure. Forcing identical end-states therefore requires unequal procedure: quotas that override the standard, excellence suppressed where it pulls ahead, success punished where it separates. A class of equalizers appears, a class whose office and income are the adjusting itself, and that class never works itself out of a job, because the gaps it governs regenerate after every round of adjustment. The equalizers erase causes along the way: the gap is the only fact admitted to the hearing, and asking why the gap exists becomes the new offense. A people that punishes competence and forbids questions about causes thrives less. None of this forbids remedy. Proven theft, fraud, and coercion are remedied under the same rules that govern everyone. The error is results as the definition, not restoration under law.

Value symmetry also has a case its holders would recognize. A harm done should be answered, and the one who caused the harm should bear the cost, not the victim and not the public. The old rule of an eye for an eye, its defenders say, began as a ceiling rather than a license: one eye and no more, where blood vengeance would have taken the whole family. Its defenders add that punishment should fit the crime, and within limits this book agrees: proportion belongs in sentencing, after judgment, under law.

The failure of value symmetry is that its ledger faces backward and never closes. Picture the book a feud keeps. A man from one household wounds a man from another, and the wounded household writes the debt in the first column of its book. Years later the wounded household pays the debt with a second wounding, and now the first household opens its own book and enters the debt afresh. Each book records the latest injury as the first entry, and neither book contains a page for settlement. The measure of an equal harm is never agreed: one household counts a dead son, the other counts a burned barn, and no scale weighs the two to both households' satisfaction. The sons learn the outstanding balance before they learn the plow, and the fields the feud crosses go unplanted. Equal return cannot build forward cooperation or formation, because its energy is spent balancing what is already done. Proportion after judgment has its limited place. Equal return in kind fails as the master rule of a social order.

### Rule of law and rule by man

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] P2.C2: published process including rulers; jersey selective prosecution = rule by man even with court buildings. Complete.
Rule of law is a published and stable process that includes the rulers. The rule is written before the case, announced to everyone, and applied to the minister who drafted it as surely as to the applicant who petitions under it. Inside such a process, distinctions track real roles. An adult and a child differ in what each can do and owe, and the law marks that difference. A citizen and a non-citizen carry different standing in a polity, and the law marks that too. What law may not mark is favored blood or current fashion. A distinction that follows bloodline or trend is not a role distinction but an exemption, and a rule that carries such an exemption is a favor in the form of a law.

Rule by man is what remains when the will of the powerful, or of the favored coalition, replaces the published process. The test is not whether the institutions still stand. Suppose a prosecutor reads the party registration before reading the evidence: the opposing team's minor violations draw the full weight of the statute, while the same violations on the prosecutor's own team are judged unworthy of a charge. The courts still sit in their buildings, the robes are still worn, the forms are still stamped, and every verdict cites a real section of the code. None of that makes the process law. Selective prosecution by jersey, by the team the accused plays for rather than the act the evidence shows, is rule by man, and the buildings change nothing, because the rule actually operating is the will of the team in office.

### When every gap is luck

%% [LOW] [marble] P2.C3: lottery/generic person fuels outcome equity; particular duties renamed bias. Anti-marble. Complete.
Outcome symmetry draws its force from a particular view of the person: the generic person, a someone with no family, no lineage, and no situation, whose every advantage and disadvantage was dealt by chance. This book has already rejected that fiction; persons arrive inside households, lineages, and duties. On the lottery view, though, every gap between persons is arbitrary luck, and what is arbitrary cannot be just. The conclusions follow without resistance. If all gaps are luck, then no selection is honest: the surgeon chosen over the less capable candidate and the applicant chosen over the less diligent one are only winners of a draw, and the standard that chose them gets renamed bias. Particular duties are renamed next. The duty a parent owes this child before children in general, and the whole tiered priority of self and household, then family, neighbors, community, polity, and world, are renamed favoritism, because the generic person has no particular child and no household to rank first. The renaming is the evidence. A doctrine that makes judgment as such impossible, and duty as such suspect, is not a refinement of justice but a refusal of it.

### The charge

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] P2.C4 charge exact. Chapter plan-complete. No constructivist capture.
When fair is demanded, name which fair is being demanded before anything is granted. If the demand is procedural symmetry, grant it and hold the powerful to it: same rules, same methods, inside an honest scope that names real roles. If the demand is outcome symmetry, refuse forced sameness of results as the definition of justice, and answer with the honest questions: was a rule broken, was the process actually run, is there proven theft, fraud, or coercion to remedy under the same rules that govern everyone. If the demand is value symmetry, refuse the feud ledger as the standing rule of a society; judgment under published process closes accounts that return in kind only reopens. The counterfeits keep the word fair because the word is honored. Keeping the honor and refusing the copies is the work.
