## Packages and purity

### The standing rule

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] P2.F1: persons ≠ packages; premises cut labels. Correct standing rule before adversary substance.
Persons are not packages, and this chapter grades packages only. A package is a named set of premises, texts, and institutions; it is never a census of persons. The reader who uses this book aims at texts, institutions, and demonstrated political commitments, never at surnames, accents, or private piety practiced inside the law. Premises cut labels. A Baptist elder who defines justice as equal end-states has changed sides no matter what the church sign says, and a Muslim engineer who defends stable secondary causes stands closer to these axioms than a secular colleague who denies correspondence. The rule comes first because the rest of the chapter names adversary substance, and naming without this rule turns grading into blood-hunting.

### Purity procedure and reality procedure

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] P2.F2: purity sort vs reality procedure (same bans under facts; virtue cultivated). Procedure altitude; no package war by blood. Complete.
A purity system sorts people into clean and unclean by membership or supposed nature, then loads moral procedure from the sort. The sort decides which law protects whom. A hard creed package binds its own to strict discipline while granting license toward outsiders. A progressive dialect codes one sex's desire as pollution and the other sex's conduct as clean by default. A softer traditional tale awards purity at birth to one kind of person and outsources vice to witches and stepmothers. The contents differ; the procedure is shared: who you are, not what you did, decides your standing.

Reality procedure runs the other way. The same basic bans and duties bind everyone under the facts, honestly scoped: force, fraud, and predation are wrong toward the in-group and the out-group, for men and for women. Virtue under this procedure is a cultivated capacity for continuing well, trained and maintained in both sexes, because people are not naturally good. Virtue is not a halo the sort awards at birth, and it is not a checklist of avoided acts. A person can pass every purity test and still be cruel, idle, or false.

### Package dossiers

%% [CRIT] [constructivist] Social constructivism again external: form true half → overreach treats substance as decision → redesign loop bills population → conflict with discovered/constructed. Aligns 1800 + 2100. Do not import into axioms.
%% [LOW] [plan-gap] H8/P2.F3 list present: constructivism, occasionalism, scripturalism/two-books, Islamic package + strands, name-that-outlives-swap (blacklist-safe rename of jersey inversion), outcome equity, blank slate, marble/luck, anti-natal, reactionary anti-procedure.
Part 1 named these packages briefly. What follows deepens each naming with the consequence that appears at scale or across generations, then marks the conflict with the axioms.

**Social constructivism.** The package starts from a true observation: many arrangements are made by people and could be otherwise. It then overreaches and treats every arrangement as mere decision, including the ones that reappear because the underlying problem remains. At scale it produces redesign after redesign of money, family, and law, with each failure billed to the stubborn population rather than to the plan. The conflict: the package denies the discovered and constructed distinction, so it cannot explain why banned forms keep being reinvented.

**Occasionalism-style blocks on testable order.** The package starts from piety: God alone creates each event, and created things carry no causal power of their own. Where the teaching holds that divine habit is steady, prediction and craft proceed and the quarrel stays metaphysical. Where public epistemology treats will as unbound by stable order, experiment loses authority, false claims escape the shared veto, and a civilization's maps stop correcting. The conflict: correspondence requires that reality answer, whether or not the answer suits the pious.

**Scripturalism against the two books.** The package starts from zeal for the written word: scripture alone speaks for God, and the book of nature is mute or corrupt. Carried into institutions, the package censors astronomy, medicine, and history wherever findings press the literal reading. The two-books path reads creation as a second book by the same author, so honest inquiry into the world honors the author instead of threatening the faith. The conflict: truth is correspondence to reality, and a package that forbids reading reality forbids testing anything.

**Islamic doctrinal-political packages.** Not every Muslim, and not one school: strands inside Islamic civilization defend real secondary causes and a stable created order (the falsafa of Ibn Rushd, Muʿtazilī rationalism), and many believers cite "There shall be no compulsion in [acceptance of] the religion" (Qur'an 2:256, Sahih International style wording) for liberty of conscience. The package this book grades starts from divine law as constitution. Classical political readings of Qur'an 9:29 structure non-Muslim submission "until they give the jizyah willingly while they are humbled," and creed tiers replace citizen tiers. Institutionalized across centuries, that structure yields two-tier standing and managed exit, with equal procedure conditional on creed. The conflict: procedural symmetry inside honest scopes, and tiered priority grounded in household and community rather than submission status. Grade by texts, institutions, and conduct, not by surname.

**The name that outlives the swap.** The package starts from loyalty: members trust the name because the name once carried the premises they love. A church or party keeps the name and swaps the premises, and the swap is cheap because institutions rarely retest their own foundations; the seminary or the central committee changes the creeds while the sign out front stays painted. Across a generation the institution draws authority from the old deposit and spends it on the new program, and tells members who notice that the name obliges their assent. The conflict: premises cut labels, and the package is what the premises are now, not what the building says.

**Outcome equity.** The package starts from a real grievance: some disparities are rigged. It then defines justice as same end-states and treats every disparity as proof of injustice, so no honest difference in effort, gift, or fortune can clear the charge. At scale the standard punishes the high performer, rewrites every entry test, and teaches each cohort that procedure is a disguise for power. The conflict: procedural symmetry is the justice this book keeps, outcome symmetry is the rival, and naming which "fair" is being demanded ends most of these arguments before they start.

**Blank slate and perfectibility.** The package starts from hope: people are plastic, and better formation makes better people. Hardened into doctrine, the package denies expressed endowment and reads every failed program as sabotage by wreckers rather than as information about the material. Across generations the programs escalate, because the clay never confesses fast enough. The conflict: humans are limited and not naturally good; formation works on real endowment, and design aimed at rewriteable clay builds coercion instead of schools.

**Marble-self and the luck lottery.** Two packages share one denial. The marble-self claims the person sculpts himself from nothing, so no household, line, or forming institution earns credit or claim. The luck lottery claims the reverse: endowment and circumstance decide everything, so no one owes gratitude and no one may keep what he has earned. The first inflates formed agency, the second dissolves it, and both erase it. The conflict: persons are formed agents, limited and teachable, owing debts they did not contract and deserving what their labor adds.

**Anti-natal.** The package starts from cost accounting: children mean suffering, carbon, or lost freedom, so refusing birth counts as mercy or virtue. The premise denies the affirmed good at the root, since a life that continues well requires a next life to continue. Across generations the arithmetic is pitiless: a people that stops bearing and raising children does not continue, and its platform passes to peoples who did not stop. The conflict: survive and thrive is the affirmed good, not one lifestyle option among many.

**Reactionary anti-procedure.** The package starts from an accurate diagnosis: captured institutions abuse procedure, and patience loses. It then prescribes the strong hand unbound: no published process, no correction, loyalty tests in place of tests of fact. At scale the package reproduces what it opposed, rule by man under a different flag, and it leaves the platform unrepaired, because repair needs exactly the procedure the strong hand discarded. The conflict: procedural symmetry and correction are not the opponent's weapons; they are the load-bearing members.

### When packages coordinate

%% [HIGH] [constructivist] P2.F4: "truth as instrument" again hostile mechanism not method. Full checklist (common enemy through alliance-then-purge). Not census. Iran warning. After packages—order correct.
%% [MED] [source] Qur'an 2:256 verify tag already present—good. Keep Sahih International naming at publish.
The dossiers raise a practical question: why do rival packages coordinate so reliably against a procedural republic? The answer is tendencies and mechanisms, not shared principles and not a census of persons. Many progressives and many Muslims refuse every item below; the mechanisms explain why coordination recurs, not why any person must join it.

- **Common enemy, thicker than a slogan.** Neutral rule of law, equal citizenship, defensible borders, free inquiry, and the ordinary family block both the red program and the green program, so the same institutions obstruct both at once.
- **A shared tongue without a shared metaphysics.** Oppression, resistance, liberation: each side hears its own doctrine in the same words.
- **Truth as instrument.** Each side treats description that harms the cause as attack and denies reality its veto over the package.
- **Shield and sword.** The progressive partner supplies narrative cover, two-tier scrutiny, and institutional access; the hard creed partner supplies organization, numbers, and readiness to dominate.
- **Dual codes on both sides.** Membership-relative morals make two-tier enforcement feel normal to both partners.
- **The collective subject.** Class, race, or creed community replaces the limited agent under law.
- **Humiliation politics.** The wound stories differ; the usable emotion is one, resentment carrying permission to break ordinary restraints.
- **Speech sacralization, explicit and implicit.** One side names blasphemy, the other enforces it without naming it; both hold some claims unspeakable and some speakers fit for ruin.
- **Anti-merit strains.** Where thriving under open procedure reads as suspect, high performers become shared irritants.
- **Entryism.** Entryists read schools, parties, agencies, and congregations as power bases and use the host's rules until they can rewrite the host.
- **Alliance, then purge.** Coordination holds while the shared target stands; when monopoly of force becomes available, the harder package sorts the partner. Iran is the standing warning.

### The charge

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] P2.F5 charge complete. %% [MED] [vocab] "honest scopes" in test line—house diction. Chapter plan-complete.
Name the package and the purity move. When a speaker sorts clean from unclean by membership and then loads the law from the sort, say so, and name the texts and institutions doing the sorting. When a coalition offers help, take the help for what it is: temporary allies are not shared life under the axioms. The test of any ally's package stays the full one: correspondence, limits, life continuing well, tiers, and one procedure inside honest scopes. Aim there, always, and never at surnames.
