## Social virtues and forms

%% [LOW] [marble] [plan-gap] E0 ¶1 load-bearing line present and undiluted: no generic person; you, family, lineage, situation. Uses continue-and-continue-well; limited / not naturally good. No theorist names; no package war. Plan-faithful positive core.
There is no generic person. There is you, tied to your family, their lineage, and your situation: the body you were born in, the place that raised you, the language you think in, the debts you inherited, and the people who actually fed you, taught you, and buried your dead. None of that is decoration on a blank token. That is the person who must survive and thrive. Human life should continue and continue well, and the chain of people that produced you is how life continues in fact: someone worked, someone saved, someone taught, or you would not be here. Gratitude for what your line and household handed you is not theft. Duty is strongest toward those people and that situation first, and preferring your own children is not a defect of character. Handing on life, skill, and honest premises is how any free and productive order outlasts one generation. Social rules exist so that limited people, bound this way and not naturally good, can keep life continuing well together without requiring angels.

A common mistake begins from a true point: no one chose their birth. You did not earn your parents, your country, or your century, and the holders of the rival view are right to say so. Then the slide begins. Because those facts were unchosen, the holders of the rival view treat them as morally weightless. Because you did not choose your family, they rename love of your own as prejudice. They redescribe the person as a generic person who could have held any slot in any household, with no real parents and no real people. They then redefine justice as whatever that generic someone would choose while knowing nothing of their place. In practice the choosing tends to come out the same way: goods without burdens, claims on what is produced, with no duty to the households and lines that produce it.

Reject the slide from the positive. If there is no generic person, only you, your family, their lineage, and your situation, then a morality written for a generic person is written for nobody real. People also differ, in talent, in formation, in foresight; some of the difference is inborn and no school makes potentials equal. A design that pretends everyone is the same far-sighted cooperator, or that no one would use an open promise of goods without burdens to claim without producing, will be gamed by the first generation that reads its incentives, and the gaming will hollow the platform everyone still stands on. Life continues well only where real people form, produce, and transmit inside their actual chains, not where tokens claim. Same rules inside a shared scope remain necessary among those who share a public order. The fiction of the generic person is not necessary, and it is not true.

Other households stand beside yours, other lines, other claims, and their lives meet yours on shared streets. Those two questions govern the rest of this chapter, and the book answers them with three terms: scope, tiered priority, and procedural symmetry.

### The rule of law: same process

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] E2: same rules including rulers; role distinctions vs fake neutral blood sort; exemption refused. Plan-complete. No jersey-exemption detail (P2.C expands)—OK.
A polity has the rule of law when the same public rules bind everyone in the same legal position, including the people who write the rules. The alternative is will: the clerk decides by who stands before him, the minister by mood, the strongman by appetite. Law puts rulers under rules, and the difference is practical. A judge who must give reasons under a published rule can be checked against that rule, corrected on appeal, and removed for ignoring it; a judge who decides by favor cannot be checked at all, because favor has no reasons. Where will replaces rule, citizens learn the real lesson quickly: outcomes depend on access, not on conduct, and honest dealing becomes a habit for losers. The historical record of government by will is long, and the ruled have usually paid the price.

Same rules do not mean no distinctions. A scope is honest when its boundaries track a real difference the procedure needs: adults and children face different rules because judgment is trained, not born; members and guests face different duties because members carry costs guests have not yet carried. A rule that sorts by those roles can be honest and can be checked. A rule that sorts cousins from strangers under a neutral-sounding label is a fake scope, not law, and procedural symmetry dies there, because the written rule says merit while the applied boundary is blood. When rulers place themselves above the rules they wrote, the court keeps only the appearance of law and the verdict becomes a favor. The rule of law is the refusal of that exemption, applied hardest to the people most tempted by it.

### Honest speech, and fraud as an attack on process

%% [LOW] [constructivist] E3: truth as correspondence; minority claims for error correction; fraud/coordinated lie as process attack not "just more speech." Blocks power-as-truth and fashion-as-truth. Strong.
A society that tests claims runs on descriptions that can be checked. Truth is correspondence to reality, and cooperation is built from reports: the weight on the scale, the balance in the ledger, the measurement on the plan, the witness's account of the night in question. When a speaker reports what he saw as exactly as he can, strangers can build on the report. When a speaker is honestly wrong and says so, the correction feeds the shared process, and the speaker has lost nothing but an error. Because everyone is limited, every settled view may be wrong somewhere, and error correction requires that minority claims be heard. The unfashionable report is sometimes the true one; a process that silences the unfashionable report has removed part of how it corrects itself.

Fraud is a different act from error, and a healthy order treats the two differently. The person in error wants to be right and can be shown the world; the fraudster knows the scale is light and certifies the weight anyway, because the fraud pays. A coordinated lie goes further: many voices repeat a known falsehood until checking becomes expensive and the same voices punish checking itself. Neither is one more contribution to weigh. Both are attacks on the process by which every other claim is weighed; they damage the instrument, not merely the reading. So honest scopes do two things at once without embarrassment: they protect dissent, even sharp and mistaken dissent, and they punish fraud, even profitable and fashionable fraud. A court, a market, and a science fail the same way when lying costs nothing and truth-telling costs everything.

### Property and contract

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] E4: ownership concentrates care; contract as future cooperation; theft/repudiation destroy thrival. Roof picture works. Complete.
Ownership concentrates maintenance, and maintenance is where care meets consequence. Consider a roof over a shared stair. Rain finds the weak seam first. Water sits where it should not sit, and by spring the beam beneath is soft. When one named owner holds the house, the owner climbs the ladder in autumn, because the owner will pay for the beam. When the house belongs to everyone in general, each tenant watches the stain spread and waits for someone else to fetch the ladder, and the rot advances at the price of the whole building. The roof is every maintained thing: a field, a tool, a shop, a fund. Property law answers the maintenance question before the leak begins by naming who pays and who may decide, and that naming is why things owned are usually things kept. Strip ownership from the things a people uses, and the people will find that care without consequence is talk, not practice.

Contract is the same virtue aimed at the future. Two people who will never be friends can still cooperate across seasons if each can rely on what the other signed: the farmer plants for the miller's promise, the miller borrows against the harvest, and both are richer than either could be alone. Reliability of promise turns strangers into a chain of production instead of neighbors who each guard their own pile. Theft and repudiated promises do not merely move goods from one hand to another; where they go without remedy, they teach every producer that keeping faith is for fools, and production shrinks to what one household can watch. Courts that enforce property and contract are not doing owners a favor. Courts are how a people makes cooperation among strangers cheap enough to be common, and cheap cooperation is what continuing well looks like at scale.

### The family: formation and replacement

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] [life-axiom] E5: long childhood dependency; first school; family loyalty as bigotry refused; equal action-love critique. "States tried wholesale institutions, record poor"—soft empirical; OK without fake tolls. Complete.
No institution can be neutral about the family, because the family does the two jobs every people must have done: it forms the next generation, and it replaces the current one. A human child is dependent for years, longer by far than the young of any animal the child will later tend, and those years force long investment. Someone must feed, teach, correct, and protect the child for a decade and more before the child returns any of it. The household is the first school of virtue and skill, the place where a limited, self-interested, not naturally good creature is trained into honesty, work, and self-command by people who are still there when the training gets tedious. Formation at that depth needs permanence, and permanence is what kin supply. States can supplement that formation; where states have tried to rear children wholesale in institutions, the record is poor, and the children paid first.

A polity that treats family loyalty as bigotry is attacking its own replacement and its own formation at the same time. The teaching sounds generous: love everyone equally, prefer no one, let the child belong to all. The results are countable: fewer children, each given less formation, taught that preferring their own is shameful, and a treasury that discovers, a generation late, that every army, orchestra, and pension fund is staffed by somebody's child. A people does not have to hate the stranger to feed its own child first. Tiered priority already says the outer claim is real but weaker; the family is where the near claim is strongest, and a social order that punishes the near claim will not be staffed long enough to honor any outer one.

### Education always forms, so aim it

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] E6: always forms; aim truth/competence/continuation; school capture = axiom capture. Ties D1/D5/D7. Complete.
Formation never stops, and it is never neutral. Every school forms: the timetable teaches what matters, the grading teaches what counts, the teacher's silences teach what may not be asked. A school that announces its neutrality still trains children in someone's habits of attention and someone's list of exemplars; the only choice is whether the formation is admitted and aimed, or denied and unaccountable. Parents who hand a child to a school are handing over hours of formation every day, and the hours do not care what the brochure says.

Since formation is constant, aim it. Teach truth as correspondence to reality: claims checked against the world, the world preferred to the preferred story. Teach competence: real skills, honestly graded, that let a graduate build, heal, grow, calculate, and repair. Teach the capacity to continue life upward: responsibility, ownership, judgment, strength, the personal virtues this book has already named. One generation taught badly is not a pause in the record. A skill untaught for one generation is gone from the next; what the grandparents did with their hands, the grandchildren cannot even describe, and capability once lost is rebought at full price.

Because schools form the next generation's premises, capture of the schools is capture of the next generation's axioms. The capture is quiet: no wall breached, no treasury emptied on the first morning, only new timetables and new exemplars. A polity that would never hand its courts to a hostile program should think at least as hard about who writes its curricula. Parents and polities that intend their people to continue and continue well ask the same three questions of every school: is what it teaches true, is what it trains competent, and what kind of person is it forming on purpose?

### How far claims reach: scopes, tiers, and same rules

%% [HIGH] [justice] [plan-gap] This block repairs the Run-1 CRIT on 1100: outcome symmetry and value symmetry are named, steelmanned briefly, and refused; procedural symmetry kept. Q5 is answered here even if axioms under-answered. Do not cut this paragraph in length trims.
%% [MED] [vocab] [author-ruling] "honest scope" recurs heavily (author rejected the phrase in 1100). Content is right (real roles/membership/jurisdiction); rename to plain English if house diction is fixed: "defined membership," "same position," "jurisdiction."
%% [MED] [tiers] Equal-duty-to-all as performance that does nothing—restores B4 error clause missing from 1100 publish. Good.
Every claim travels along a real chain, and chains have length. You are limited: finite hours, finite knowledge, finite reach. Action-based love is costly time, money, and attention, and a budget that covers everyone alive covers no one. The person who announces equal duty to all humanity has not enlarged his duty; he has excused himself from doing any of it, because the announcement is the whole act. Scope is the honest alternative: a role, a membership, or a jurisdiction inside which procedure and duty actually apply. Inside an honest scope, a promise can name who keeps it and a rule can name who is bound. Outside every scope, obligation dissolves into sentiment.

Within the real chains, priority is tiered and self-anchored. The axiom states the order: duties are strongest near self and household, then family, then neighbors, then community, then polity, then the world. Outer claims are real but weaker. This book does not derive the tiers from anything beneath them and does not try to; they are part of the answer, held as an axiom, and they are what makes duty finite enough to be real for finite creatures. The outer tier is not zero. A stranger in the world tier still has a claim: honest dealing, humane procedure, a bridge over a short shock where a bridge is possible. What the stranger does not have is your child's place at your table, and an ethics that confuses the two claims ends by honoring neither.

Justice inside a scope is procedural symmetry: the same rules and the same methods of judgment for everyone in the same position. Two rival accounts of justice fail from opposite sides, and each deserves its fair hearing first. Outcome symmetry holds that equal end-states are owed and that a persistent gap between groups is itself proof of injustice; its holders can point, truly, to games that were rigged. But where those who judge have genuinely applied the same rules, different choices, different talents, and different fortunes still produce different end-states, and treating every gap as proof of rigging convicts the honest and punishes results as such. Value symmetry is the feud ledger, equal value returned in kind, harm for harm; its holders can say, truly, that bounded revenge once replaced unlimited revenge. But the ledger never closes, because each side counts its own harms at full weight and the other side's at half, and public procedure exists to replace private accounting with a rule both parties were already under before the injury. Procedural symmetry asks the harder, simpler question: did those who judged apply the same rules, honestly scoped, to both? Where they did, the result is just even when unequal. Where they did not, repair the procedure; do not correct the result by decree.

An honest scope, then, is the unit of a just social order. Its boundaries must track real roles, as the rule of law requires; its members must be real people in real chains, as the primer insists; and its procedures must be the same for all inside it, favored and disfavored alike, as honest inquiry demands of every model. The three requirements are one discipline seen from three sides: the same standard for friend and stranger, no refusing the test only when it threatens the preferred story, no exemption for the people who wrote the rules. Everything that follows in this chapter is that discipline applied: to borders, to trade, to defense, to advancement, to reform, and to the plural life of peoples.

### Borders and membership

%% [LOW] [marble] E7: defined we for cushions/defense; not identical claim on every treasury; grade join by thrival of existing people; membership not marble lottery. Granary picture painted. Anti-generic-person. Complete.
A cushion, a court, and an army all answer the same question first: whose? A cushion is a time-bound bridge over a short shock, stocked by named contributors for named members. Picture a village granary. After harvest the villagers fill it sack by sack, and the tally keeper marks who gave and who may draw. Winter comes; nothing grows; the stored grain carries the village to spring. Now declare that anyone hungry anywhere may draw on the granary. The first winter empties it by midwinter, and the second autumn no one fills it, because contributors do not stock a store they cannot keep. The defined we was load-bearing. Dissolving it did not widen the kindness; it ended the provision. Defense spending and jury duty have the same shape: costs borne by members for members, sustained only while the membership is real.

Membership at the scale of a people is the same principle, and membership is not an interchangeable claim that could have belonged to anyone. Peoples and polities are historical products: real chains of descent, language, law, and sacrifice built them, and each new member inherits both the platform and its debts. The morality of the generic person counts every human as holding an identical claim on every treasury, and that accounting feels generous right up to the moment the platform is consumed. A humane polity answers differently: decent procedure for everyone inside the borders, and an honest, graded answer to who may join, judged by whether the existing people will continue and continue well and by the real capacity to absorb. Borders and association rights that guard a people's space are not automatic bigotry. They are how different visions get different spaces to live in, and the end of this chapter returns to that point.

### Trade under scarcity

%% [HIGH] [constructivist] "Trade is discovered, not constructed" is correct plan use of form/substance (P2.A / discovered_vs_constructed): constraint reappears when banned; not "markets are God." This is anti-constructivist-totalism, not house constructivism. Keep the distinction sharp if anyone softens "discovered" into "we invent all social reality."
%% [LOW] [plan-gap] E8 complete: specialization, prices, fraud/coercion still crimes, tools graded by thrival.
Scarcity makes trade necessary and specialization makes it pay. No household makes everything it needs, and the potter who throws pots all year makes better pots than the farmer who throws one a year; both eat better when they exchange. Prices are how a scattered people learns what is scarce without waiting for a ministry to tell it: the price of wheat after a bad harvest carries the news of the harvest to everyone who buys bread, faster and more honestly than any report. Voluntary exchange at honest prices is not a necessary evil. It is how limited people cooperate across distance with others they will never meet.

Trade is discovered, not constructed. Arrangements that reappear under independent conditions reappear because the problem remains, and exchange reappears when rulers ban it, moving through alley and kitchen at worse prices and under worse men.

Discovery is not worship. Fraud is still fraud at a market stall, coercion is still coercion with a contract in hand, and a sale can be formally free and practically forced when one side's desperation sets the terms. Markets are tools for life, graded like every social form in this book by whether the people using them continues and continues well. Keep the tool sharp and the law over it honest; do not make the tool the end.

### Defense: enemies and opponents

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] E9: enemy by acts/programs not surnames; opponent shares process; persons ≠ packages; aim is ordinary life not war romance. Watch picture works. Complete.
A people that refuses to name its enemies will learn the names too late. Sort by acts and programs, never by surnames. An opponent shares enough of the process to be answered inside law and argument: the rival party, the critic, the plaintiff, the competing school of thought. An enemy is defined by acts and programs against your people or against the shared process itself: force used against your people, the deliberate destruction of shared truth and shared law, unbound will that would replace reciprocal citizenship with the power to impose. Persons are not packages; each individual is judged by commitments and conduct, and the neighbor who plays by the shared rules is not an enemy because of his ancestry.

<!-- arc: E9 | point: naming enemies is preparedness, and the aim is ordinary life, not war -->
The work of defense is sorting, and the sorting never ends. Picture a walled town with one gate, and the watch standing in the gate all night. Most who come are traders and cousins, and the watch waves them through. The work is in the exceptions: lantern raised, questions asked, weapons counted, intentions weighed. A watch that waves everyone through has not become kind; the watch has stopped watching, and the town pays for that courtesy in a single bad night. More than one people in the record was prosperous, polite, and unprepared, right up to the day its name became a lesson. Naming an enemy is not war romance, and this book wants no permanent war. The positive aim remains life: continue and continue well. Defense exists so that schools, markets, courts, and households can go on being ordinary, and the best defense is the one whose success is never noticed.

### Merit under honest procedure

%% [LOW] [justice] E10: yeast picture; fix corrupt procedure; envy-as-justice / equal end-states refused with steelman. Reinforces B5. Complete.
Formation is scarce, so a people must decide where its scarce formation goes, and merit under honest procedure is the answer. Master-time spent where capability is highest multiplies what the whole people can do. Picture yeast in dough. A handful of yeast worked through warm dough raises the whole loaf, every crumb, though the yeast is a hundredth of the mass. Formation placed well works the same way: the trained surgeon, engineer, and mason carry many households beyond their own, and the apprentice who can take the teaching and does not wastes it twice, once in the spending and once in the losing. Character is not read off a score. Honest selection weighs proven capability and conduct together, because a brilliant fraud costs a people more than a dull one.

<!-- arc: E10 | point: fix corrupt procedure; refuse equal end-states by force -->
When selection is corrupt, and most peoples know the smell of a rigged competition, the remedy is to repair the procedure: publish the criteria, blind what can be blinded, punish the bribed examiner and the bought appointee. The rival remedy treats envy as justice. Its steelman is real: when the same families win decade after decade, the game may well be rigged, and rage at a rigged game is earned. But equal end-states enforced by power do not unrig the game; they end it. Excellence stops paying, effort stops making sense, the capable leave or hide, and the people inherits equal shares of a shrinking loaf. Judge advancement by procedure. Keep the procedure honest with the same suspicion aimed at favorites. Then let the loaf rise.

### Experiment: expand what works, kill what fails

%% [LOW] [truth] E11: bound trials; reality judges; kill moral-feeling failures; pilot-that-cannot-fail is not a trial. Anti-hermetic. Complete.
No rule survives contact with a whole people unchanged, so social forms should be tried the way a careful farmer tries seed. Picture the test plot. The farmer stakes a small square beside the main field and plants the new seed there and only there, then watches it through a full season: sprout, storm, harvest. If the plot yields, next season a larger plot; if the plot fails, the field has lost a corner, not a crop. Social experiment follows the same order. Bound the trial: one town, one trade, one remit, with the terms of success written down before the trial begins, so that sponsors cannot move the target afterward. Then let reality judge, because reality is real and consequential and does not grade intentions. Expand what works. Kill what fails, and kill it completely, even when the program felt moral and its sponsors weep. The two corruptions are known: expanding what feels good, and killing nothing. A pilot that can never be declared a failure is not a trial; it is a promotion announced as a trial. A people that tests in bounds can afford to be bold, because its mistakes stay small and its successes are free to grow.

### Plural spaces above one public floor

%% [MED] [plan-order] [truth] E12 floor lists five as split B1/B2 ("reality is real; truth is correspondence") + life + tiers + procedure—plan B1–B5. Misses author-merged "knowable and consequential" and human limits (B7) that 1100 elevated. Decide house floor formula once; update here and 2700 matrix together. "Honest scopes" again.
%% [LOW] [marble] Errors correct: forced one-vision remake into generic humans; no shared floor / pure exit. Positive between them. House picture painted. Plan-complete on structure.
Different visions of the ultimate cannot all be lived at full intensity in the same room, so the answer is a house, not a battlefield. Picture the house. One floor, laid true, carries every room above it. The rooms differ: this one keeps icons and fast days, that one keeps books and instruments, a third keeps little but tools and work. The walls between rooms let each household live its thicker program without remaking the neighbor's. The floor beneath all of them is shared, and everyone who walks above depends on the same joists. The floor is the first five axioms, required where lives actually meet: reality is real; truth is correspondence to reality; human life should continue and continue well; duties are tiered from self and household outward; justice is procedural symmetry within honest scopes. Where people share streets, markets, and courts, those five hold for all. Above the floor, households, churches, schools of choice, associations, and whole polities may run thicker programs than the floor demands, and the differences between rooms are not a problem to be solved.

<!-- arc: E12 | point: the positive form stands between forced synthesis and no shared floor -->
Two errors face this house, and each has its truth. The first says that a true vision should fill the whole house at full intensity, that one campus or one state must embody every value, and that the campus or state should remake dissenters into generic humans until the rooms match. Its holders are right that truth is not a hobby. But the remaking never stops at the rooms; it cracks the floor, because a public order busy remaking persons has no attention left for keeping procedure the same. The second error says there should be no shared floor at all, only exit: every room its own house, every dispute settled by leaving. Its holders are right that forced unity is tyranny. But neighbors share wells and roads whether or not they share visions, and a row of houses with no shared process settles its deepest disputes by force. The positive form stands between them: honest membership in a room, exit where exit is real, the same public procedure for everyone who shares a jurisdiction, and no myth that all people must become one interchangeable pool of generic persons before they may live beside each other in peace.

### The charge: platforms that let virtue scale

%% [LOW] [plan-gap] Charge: platforms as tools for life; grade by continue and continue well; children not generic persons. Matches C5/H7. No brand names. Complete.
Every form in this chapter is a platform: what took generations to build, and what can be consumed in far less time. The personal virtues cannot scale alone. One person's honesty is a habit; a court that holds ten thousand strangers to honest speech is a platform. One household's thrift feeds one child; property and contract feed a city of people who will never meet. One parent drilling one child is formation; schools that aim the formation of a generation are how a people keeps its skills. None of these platforms is an end in itself: not law, not markets, not borders, not any regime or office. They are tools for life, and this book grades them by one test: whether the people living under them continues and continues well, this generation and the next. So the charge of this section is plain. Build the platforms where they are missing. Keep the platforms you inherited: the same rule for both parties in court, the honest scale in the market, the aimed school, the watched gate, the membership that means something. Hand them on stronger than you found them. Your children are not generic persons either, and they will need platforms built by people who were not.
