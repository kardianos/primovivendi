## A. Project stance

### A1. What this book is for
- Open with five diagnostic questions that cut beneath labels; then state this book’s answers as axioms [Q, B].
- Build a positive account of living from that small set of axioms.
- After axioms: teach how to handle **testable** truth (models, wrong vs incomplete, procedure of inquiry); section off what cannot be tested [M].
- Spend most of the page count on survival, thriving, and the personal and social virtues those require.
- Use short, hard sections on how people fall off that path and how bad reactions to the fall also fail.
- End by naming external principle-sets, stating what we test vs leave to the divine, reading long-horizon effects, then how hostile packages still ally, then return to the positive aim.
- Do not organize the book as “the frame vs the inverted frame.”
- Do not make “the Conservative Frame” the product name the reader must learn first.
- The product is a way to live and cooperate under axioms, not a brand.

### A2. What this book is not
- Not a catalog of enemies.
- Not a defense of democracy, republic, monarchy, party, or constitution as ultimate ends.
- Not a steelman tournament with every rival school.
- Not an inverted-five mirror of equal length.
- Government forms are tools. They are graded by whether they help limited people live and continue well under truth and fair procedure within honest scopes.

### A3. Method of argument
1. State the axiom or claim in one sentence.
2. Give the chain that forces the next step (if A then B because …).
3. Give a concrete picture (kitchen, field, border, school, workshop).
4. Name the personal virtue and the social form that carry it.
5. Only then name a failure mode, if needed.
6. After a failure mode: refute the failure, refute the crude reaction, restate the independent positive.

## Q. Five questions that divide societies (opener)

**Arc job:** Before stating axioms, put the diagnostic on the table. Readers already answer these questions in action. Labels (church, party) do not guarantee shared answers. The book then states *its* answers [B]. Do not smuggle thrival or any other answer into question 3’s wording.

### Q1. Placement
- Immediately after front matter; before B1–B8.
- Keep tight: questions + why they matter + “you already answer them”; then move to axioms as answers.

### Q2. Why questions before axioms
1. Two people can share a religion or party and split on these five.  
2. Policy fights fail when premises stay hidden.  
3. Cooperation tracks answers to these questions better than jerseys.  
4. Question 3 stays **open** so rivals can answer differently; the book’s answer is B3 (affirmed), not smuggled into the question.

### Q3. The five questions (full text — do not paraphrase in the manuscript opener)

Copy these into the reader-facing opener **as written**:

1. What is truth? Is reality real and independent of wish? Can we know it with usable confidence? Is knowledge mainly discovery that answers to the world, or only a product of power, will, or pure dialectic? What makes a claim true?

2. Are humans limited and self-interested by nature, a blank slate waiting to be written, or naturally good / perfectible / restorable by remaking the whole social (or sacral-political) order?

3. What is the standard of the good: what ought human life and society aim at?

4. What deserves priority when obligations conflict?

5. What does justice require: equal procedures within a scope, equal outcomes for everyone, or equal value returned in kind?

### Q4. Structure of the five
- **Q1–Q2:** world and creature (descriptive premises).  
- **Q3:** is–ought turn; many rival answers exist (thrival, harm reduction, equity, sacred political unity, co-suffering, etc.).  
- **Q4–Q5:** duty and justice under a chosen good, given human limits.

### Q5. Map questions → this book’s answers

| 1 Truth / reality / knowledge | B1 + B2 (+ M for testable method) |
| 2 Human nature | B7 (limited, self-interested, not naturally good; not blank slate / perfectible) |

- Parent who funds own child before distant causes → answered priority.

## B. Primary axioms (answers to the five questions)

**Note:** B1–B5 answer Q’s five questions in order (with B7 supplying more of Q2’s anthropology). State them as answers after the questions block, not as if no rival answers exist.

### B1. Reality is real
- **Definition:** There is a world that exists whether anyone likes it, names it, or votes on it.
- **Answers Q1 (part):** reality is real and independent of wish.
- **Not:** “My lived experience is the world.” Not “the map is the territory.”
- **Chain:**
  1. Bodies need food, shelter, heat.
  2. Those needs are met or not met by what actually happens, not by slogans.
  3. A bridge holds or falls; a harvest comes or fails; a knife cuts or does not.
  4. Therefore public life must treat the world as independent of wish.
- **Entailment:** Wish, story, and power can change what people do; they do not rewrite load-bearing facts at will.
- **Error:** Treating social agreement as the test of what is.

### B2. Truth is what corresponds to reality
- **Definition:** A claim is true when it matches what is.
- **Answers Q1 (part):** knowledge is mainly discovery answering to the world; correspondence makes a claim true (among public rivals: decree, power, pure coherence).
- **Not:** Truth as pure group consensus. Not truth as “what serves the cause.”
- **Chain:**
  1. Reality is real (B1).
  2. We need shared knowledge to cooperate (plant, build, heal, defend).
  3. Shared knowledge requires a shared test: does the claim track the world?
  4. Correspondence is that test.
- **Entailment:** Models are provisional; the world is the judge. When model and world disagree, prefer the world.
- **Error:** Equal scrutiny for favored and disfavored claims abandoned; fashion exempts itself.
- **Error:** “X predicts Y, therefore X is a valid full account of reality.” Prediction can hold while the story is wrong or incomplete [M].
- **Church / state of knowledge:** Stated briefly in opening; full method section after axioms [M1, H9]. If testable in reality, test in reality. If not, this book does not settle it.

### B3. Life is good
- **Definition (axiom of goodness):** Human life should continue and continue well. Survival plus thriving. Affirmed, not deduced from pure description alone.
- **Answers Q3:** this book’s standard of the good (one answer among rivals; do not write it into the question).
- **Not:** Headcount only. Not survival at any cruelty. Not “comfort is the good.” Not harm reduction as the highest good if it ends the beings who can be good.
- **Chain (motivations, not fake pure proof):**
  1. Without living people, no value, justice, beauty, or kindness has a holder.
  2. Consent-only ethics: duties only to existing subjects who can agree or be wronged. Then a dead earth and a living earth rank equal, because no subject is wronged by emptiness. That result is only coherent if life’s existence is not itself a good. Reject that.
  3. Every living person is the end of a chain of parents who continued life.
  4. Therefore affirm: flourishing human life is good.
- **Layers (keep separate in prose):**
  - **Axiom:** life continuing and continuing well is good.
  - **Corollaries:** institutions must be able to persist; do not celebrate a culture’s own extinction; honor the chain; transmit language, skill, law, formation; do not free-ride on parents while despising the work.
  - **Participation:** have children or raise them up by real means; structure life so continuation is honorable and common. Duty and honor under the axiom, not a fake syllogism from “I exist” alone, and not a draft into every profession.
- **Error:** Treating anti-natal “generosity” as higher ethics. Treating existence as optional luxury.

### B4. Scopes and tiers are mandatory
- **Definition:** Duties and care are ranked. Strongest near the self and household, then outward: family, neighbors, community, polity, wider world. Scopes (roles, memberships, jurisdictions) are real; unscoped duty is not performable by limited people.
- **Answers Q4:** what deserves priority when obligations conflict — tiered, self-anchored reach.
- **Not:** Hate of strangers. Not zero duty beyond the door. Not nation as the only real unit by metaphysics; the list is practical reach under limits.
- **Chain:**
  1. Time, knowledge, and strength are finite.
  2. You know your child’s need better than a stranger’s on another continent.
  3. Unlimited declared duty becomes undone duty.
  4. Therefore rank duty. Keep outer claims real but weaker. Do not invert the order as a fashion virtue.
- **Entailment:** Borders, membership, household authority, and professional roles are scopes that make procedure operable.
- **Error:** “Love everyone equally in action” as moral theater while the near are neglected.

### B5. Procedural symmetry
- **Definition:** Within an honest scope, the same rules and methods of judgment apply to each person in the same position. Justice is same process, not same outcome, and not equal return in kind (feud ledger).
- **Answers Q5:** justice requires equal procedures within a scope — not equal outcomes for everyone, not equal value returned in kind.
- **Not:** Treating parent and child as identical roles. Not open borders as “symmetry” with no polity scope. Not outcome equity.
- **Chain:**
  1. Limited, self-interested people must cooperate.
  2. Cooperation needs stable expectations.
  3. Stable expectations need same rules within a defined set of people and roles.
  4. Outcome equality requires unequal procedure and breeds a class that lives by equalizing.
  5. Value-for-value revenge is backward and endless.
  6. Therefore procedural symmetry within scope.
- **Domains:** law; truth-seeking (same critical standard for every model) [M5]; logic; conduct (reversal test); generations (what procedure you wish had been used toward you: life, formation, platform).
- **Boundary (short):** Symmetry holds among those still inside shared life and truth-seeking. Armed enemy, systematic fraud against the process, unbound will replacing reciprocal citizenship: the form of the answer changes. Judgment required. (Detail procedure later if author expands; do not let this eat the positive book.)
- **Error:** One-way rules for favored groups. “Mercy” that never names the wrong (that is permissiveness). One-way scrutiny in science and public argument: tests that kill rival models are waived for the preferred model [M5].

### B6. How the axioms lock (without branding a “frame”)
- Truth without life as good becomes trivia or suicide notes.
- Life as good without truth becomes wish and cargo-cult policy.
- Tiers without procedure become clan favoritism and exemption.
- Procedure without tiers becomes a universal claim no limited person can keep.
- Writer may say “these axioms work together.” Writer need not say “the Frame.”

### B7. Human nature (working premise under the axioms)
- **Answers Q2:** limited and self-interested by nature; not blank slate; not naturally good / perfectible / restorable by remaking the whole social or sacral-political order. Virtue is cultivated.
- People are limited in time, knowledge, and power.
- People are self-interested in the descriptive sense: oriented to their own survival and near kin; not “should be pure egoists.”
- People are not naturally good: virtue is trained, not factory settings.
- **Chain into design:** Build personal habits and social rules for the humans who exist. Do not wait for angels. Channel self-interest; do not abolish it by decree.

### B8. Particular person (plant only; full primer at E0)
- **Plant inside the B7 human-premise paragraph (author ruling 2026-07-18; keep this edge):** part of human limits, not an added answer. There is no generic person. There is you—tied to your family, their lineage, and your situation. The axioms are for that kind of creature, not for an interchangeable token.
- **Full three-paragraph primer:** social open [E0]. Continues in D7, D15, E5–E7.
- Do not name academic theorists here. Do not open package wars here.

## M. Looking at testable truth (own section after axioms)

**Arc job:** After B1–B8 are named, teach *how* to handle claims that can be checked in reality. Motivate the axioms in use: reality judges; truth is correspondence, not vibes; procedure must be symmetric across models; limits mean humility without quitting the test. Set divine / non-testable claims **outside** this section’s authority. Then the book can spend its bulk on living (C–E) without re-arguing method every page.

### M1. Section off what this method does not settle
- **In scope here:** Claims about the created/orderly world that can be confronted with observation, measurement, prediction, failed harvest, broken bridge, birth rates, crime counts, engineering, history’s hard grades.
- **Out of scope here:** What cannot be tested in this reality (ultimate divine will, final metaphysical stories that leave no empirical trail).
- **Rule:** If it can be tested in reality, test it in reality. If it cannot, this book does not decide it; let the divine speak there (for those who hold a divine), or leave it open [H9].
- **We offer nothing for or against the words of the divine as such.** No catechism, no anti-catechism, in this section.
- **Public cooperation:** Shared streets and shared tech still need shared tests for shared empirical claims. Non-testable commitment may guide a person; it may not cancel a failed test of a testable claim.

### M2. Why “X produces Y, so X is a valid model” is not enough
- **Claim to reject as sufficient:** A model that yields useful numbers or successful practice is thereby shown to be the true full account of what is going on.
- **Chain:**
  1. Truth is correspondence to reality (B2), not mere usefulness.
  2. A procedure can output right answers for a wrong or incomplete picture (M3–M4).
  3. Usefulness is a **clue** and a **filter**. It is not a certificate of final ontology.
  4. Therefore: keep what works as practice when needed; keep testing the story; prefer models that succeed more widely and fail more honestly when pushed.
- **Survival link:** Practices can save lives while explanations are garbled (crop rotation “because spirits”). Grade practice and theory separately when they come apart [ties C, D1].
- **Error:** Treating predictive success as proof that no further scrutiny is allowed—especially when the model is politically or sacral-favored.

### M3. Wrong vs incomplete
- **Wrong:**
  - Systematically fails when tested properly, across places and trials.
  - No progressive domain where it keeps earning its keep.
  - Example class: alchemy’s promise of base metal → gold by the stated recipes; fails every controlled test.
- **Incomplete:**
  - Succeeds inside a domain of conditions (speeds, sizes, accuracies, timescales).
  - Fails or needs extension outside that domain.
  - A successor theory usually **keeps** the old successes as a limit case, then covers more.
  - Incomplete is not a moral insult. It is the normal state of limited knowers (B7).
- **Wrong dressed as incomplete:** Special pleading when every failure is redefined as “outside the domain” with no independent rule for the domain.
- **Incomplete treated as wrong:** Throwing away a tool that still builds bridges because a better theory exists for extremes—engineering still uses Newton inside the envelope.

### M4. Two examples: astronomy (wrong story that numerically worked) vs progressive incomplete chain

#### Example A — Historical astronomical methods vs Kepler (useful numbers, wrong or strained model)
- **Picture:** For calendar, navigation, and planetary *positions*, older schemes (Ptolemaic and related practical ephemeris methods) could be **relatively accurate in results**—good enough for their era’s needs—by stacking corrections (epicycles, equants, tables).
- **Model problem:** The physical picture (Earth-fixed center, crystalline spheres, epicycle machinery as ontology) was not the true arrangement of the heavens. Math and model were aimed at saving the appearances, not at the real orbits.
- **Kepler:** Elliptical orbits, area law, period–distance relation—laws that describe what bodies actually do more cleanly, under a sun-focused system.
- **Lesson for M2:** Older methods “worked” numerically in a domain. That did **not** make the epicycle ontology a valid full model of reality. Result-accuracy ≠ truth of the story.
- **Writer caution:** Do not sneer at the older calculators; they were serious. Praise the step: better correspondence under harder tests (Tycho’s data → Kepler).

#### Example B — Kepler → Newton → special relativity (incomplete models that expand)
- **Kepler:** Empirical laws of planetary motion; deep, not yet a unified dynamics of all falling and orbiting bodies.
- **Newton:** Inverse-square gravitation and laws of motion; **builds on** Kepler (derives elliptical orbits as consequences under assumptions). Wider domain: apples and moons, tides, predictions beyond the original tables.
- **Special relativity (and, for gravity, the later relativistic extension):** Newton’s picture is **incomplete** at high speeds / different frames (and full gravity needs more than Newton). Newton does not become alchemy. GPS and engineers still use Newtonian tools inside the envelope; relativity extends where Newton fails.
- **Lesson:** Incomplete models that correspond in a domain allow **progressive** accuracy: each step keeps earned successes and covers more cases. That is truth-seeking under limits—not a single final slogan.

### M5. Tie to procedural symmetry and truth
- **Same critical standard:** The tests that kill a disfavored model must also be run on the favored one (B5 in truth-seeking).
- **No one-way valve:** “Our model predicts something, so the ontology is closed” is hermetic if failures cannot count [absences: hermetic argument].
- **Correspondence over prestige:** Kepler wins on data, not on court fashion; Newton wins on unified success, not on epicycles’ pedigree.
- **Humility without relativism:** All models we hold may be incomplete (M3). That does not make all models equal. Wrong still fails; better correspondence still ranks higher.
- **Public method:** In policy and morals that claim empirical effects (“this will reduce harm,” “this will raise thrival”), demand the same honesty: short-run “works” can hide wrong stories and postponed costs [H10].

### M6. Charge of this section
- Section off the divine and the non-testable.
- For what can be tested: prefer the world to the preferred story.
- Do not confuse useful prediction with full validity of the model.
- Distinguish wrong from incomplete; keep progressive tools; discard what reality refuses.
- Apply the same procedure of scrutiny to every model, including your own.
- Then live: survival and thrival need true-enough maps and honest correction [C onward].

### M7. Writer placement and length
- **Place:** Immediately after opening axioms (B), before survival/thriving bulk (C).
- **Length:** Own arc section—long enough for both examples and the wrong/incomplete distinction; shorter than the virtues bulk.
- **Callback later:** D1 (align with truth), E11 (experiment), H9–H10 (divine split; long-horizon effects of practices that “worked” short-term).

## C. Survival and thriving (load-bearing middle)

### C1. Survival
- **Definition:** Continuing to exist as a person, household, people: food, safety, health enough to act, defense against force and fraud that would end you.
- **Not:** Guaranteed comfort. Not a tribunal-granted “right to exist” without work and defense.
- **Chain:** Dead people do not thrive. Dead peoples do not keep justice or art. Survival is the floor.

### C2. Thriving
- **Definition:** Direction past bare continuation: order, skill, health, prosperity enough to act freely, beauty, formation of the next generation, capacity to repair and enlarge what was inherited.
- **Measure:** Vector, not utopia. Slope between two times: dirtier floor to cleaner floor; weaker skill to stronger skill; chaos to usable order.
- **Not:** Arrival at a finished paradise. Not wealth as the whole good. Not feeling good as the test.

### C3. Survival without thriving
- Drawing down capital built by earlier people: living in ruins you cannot repair, skills you no longer teach, birth rates that end the line while you consume the platform.
- Still “alive this year”; failing across decades.

### C4. Thriving rhetoric without survival
- Talk of justice, art, compassion while neglecting defense, food systems, children, and honest work.
- Ends as performance on a collapsing floor.

### C5. Social forms that serve survival and thriving (positive list, not regime worship)
- Family that raises competent adults.
- Property and ownership that tie care to consequence.
- Honest trade under scarcity.
- Rule of law as same process within the polity.
- Schools that transmit skill and judgment (not pretended neutrality that still forms).
- Borders that keep the set of payers and protected roughly the same set.
- Free enough speech that error can be found.
- Grade each by results for life under the axioms, not by sacred regime name.

## D. Personal virtues (main bulk)

### D1. Align with truth
- **Act:** Prefer the world to the preferred story. Check claims. Update when wrong.
- **Why:** Wrong maps kill harvests, patients, and polities.
- **Daily:** Numbers that add up; news checked; self-assessment that hurts if true.
- **Social twin:** Institutions that reward discovery and punish fraud, not loyalty tests as truth.

### D2. Align with survival
- **Act:** Keep body, household, and tools in working order. Earn the next year. Do not outsource existence to a petition.
- **Why:** B3 and C1.
- **Daily:** Work, savings where possible, health habits, refuse permanent exemption from effort.
- **Social twin:** Policy that does not treat survival of the people as optional.

### D3. Responsibility = creating and maintaining
- **Definition:** Bring something into being and keep it in being under limits.
- **Not:** Blame archaeology. Not mood. Not confession without repair.
- **Chain:**
  1. What is made decays if unused and uncared for (roof, skill, child, firm, trust).
  2. Creating without maintaining cancels the creation.
  3. Maintaining without ever creating is only holding a shrinking stock.
  4. Responsibility is both.
- **Forward test:** Who owns the cleanup? What is the next small act?
- **Scope:** Body, household, work, children, a corner of the common. Unlimited responsibility is sentiment that fixes nothing.
- **Sacrifice:** Valid when it builds or protects something real under your duty. Waste when it is display untethered to any maintained good.

### D4. Ownership
- **Definition:** The link between control, care, and consequence. What is yours to maintain; what fails if you neglect it.
- **Not:** Paper title alone. Not “the community owns everything” with no named maintainer.
- **Chain:** Unowned commons tend toward neglect or plunder. Named ownership concentrates care.
- **Personal:** Treat the child, the tool, the street corner you use as if failure lands on you when it does.
- **Social:** Property rules that let people reap and repair; punish theft and fraud under same process.

### D5. Excellence
- **Definition:** Do the work at hand properly and keep the capacity to do harder work. Skill under maintenance.
- **Why:** Thriving is capability. Capability dies in one untaught generation (trades, literacy, order).
- **Not:** Perfectionism that freezes action. Not equal outcomes as justice.
- **Hard case:** Selection can be corrupt. Fix the procedure; do not pretend talent is uniform.
- **Formation:** Children must be taught; “find your truth” with no standard is still a formation, usually a weak one.

### D6. Beauty
- **Structure:** Order + truth + vitality together.
  - Order: proportion, rhythm, usable arrangement—measurable enough to teach and fail.
  - Truth: does not lie about function or human action; flattery that falsifies fails.
  - Vitality: harder to specify than order or truth; still name sterile vs alive work; do not use “vitality” as a club when you only mean taste.
- **Why under B3:** Work that is ordered, true, and alive is part of thriving—people stay and build where daily environment is not garbage and falsehood dressed as depth.
- **Personal:** Clean room, tended plot, true craft, buildings that shed water and do not pretend to be what they are not.
- **Not:** “Whatever I like is beauty.” Not “deliberate ugliness and incompetence as moral high ground.”

### D7. Generational continuation
- **Act:** Have children when you can responsibly anchor them, or raise up by adoption, teaching, material support of families.
- **Raise:** Transmit skill and moral tools (truth, judgment, tiered duty, fair procedure), not only sentiment.
- **Broken link:** Consume platform; transmit nothing.
- **Calibrate:** Full life, not headcount maximising. Biology forbids some paths; raising-up remains.
- **Social:** Honor parents and childbearing; do not make non-parenthood the moral ideal of a fertile society.

### D8. Strength
- **Definition:** Capacity and will to defend what must live: self, household, people, conditions of kindness.
- **Not:** Cruelty as identity. Not contempt for the weak you are bound to carry.
- **Not:** “Right to exist” as petition to a world tribunal with no work of defense.
- **Chain:** Harvests and cities fall to force when undefended. Soft manners that leave the house open are negligence.
- **Kindness vs softness:** Kindness to your own is virtue. Softness that cannot defend the conditions of kindness is failure.

### D9. Judgment
- **Definition:** Name what meets the standard and what does not.
- **Why:** Procedure and mercy both require a named wrong first.
- **Mercy:** After judgment, chosen forbearance of full consequence. Strength that can spare.
- **Permissiveness:** Skip naming the wrong; call the skip compassion. Leaves the field to whoever will still decide.

### D10. Humility
- **Definition:** “I can be wrong.” Fallibility without collapse into “nothing is knowable.”
- **Pair with excellence:** Humility without work is passivity. Excellence without humility is brittle dogma.

### D11. Outcomes over feelings (public scope)
- **Rule:** In institutions among strangers, judge by results more than by how the policy felt.
- **Family scope:** Feelings are real data; love binds.
- **Error:** Promote everyone to spare pain; produce adults who cannot read.

### D12. Action begins with you
- **Fact:** Your own acts are the lever always in reach.
- **Doomerism:** Analysis that always ends in “so I need not try” is self-permission.
- **Smallest next step:** Ten push-ups beats the perfect plan deferred forever.

### D13. Love (two kinds)
- **Dispositional:** Goodwill and non-malice widely; cheap enough to extend far.
- **Action-based:** Costly time, money, attention; budgeted by tier (B4).
- **Rule:** Do not spend action-love on the far in a way that bankrupts the near.

### D14. Forgiveness (two acts)
- **Personal release:** Let go of the need for revenge so you can live; may require nothing from the offender.
- **Social trust restored:** Must be earned: named wrong, change shown, truth.
- **Not:** Victim owes restoration on demand.

### D15. Allow failure; do not court failure
- **Allow failure:**
  - Competence requires real tests: scraped knee, lost contest, failed exam, failed firm under honest rules.
  - If every failure is treated as injustice or is cushioned into nonexistence, the person never gains skill or judgment (ties D5, D9, D11).
  - Social twin: procedures that let bad ideas and bad firms fail without shooting the unsuccessful as moral garbage (ties E11, F6 cushions).
- **Do not purposefully bring failure on yourself:**
  - Neglect, addiction, permanent exemption, “struggle” as identity, dropping tools for fashion: these are not noble non-marble authenticity.
  - Under B3 and D2–D3: you owe maintenance of your own capacity.
- **Do not purposefully bring failure on your children:**
  - Withholding formation so they stay “equal” to a slogan; sabotaging excellence from envy; refusing discipline so feelings never sting; teaching them they are only victims of lottery.
  - That is not justice. It is breaking the chain (D7) while using the language of fairness.
- **Chain:**
  1. Marble-self theories treat outcomes as arbitrary luck to be flattened.
  2. Flattening removes the information failure carries.
  3. Children raised without allowed failure stay fragile; children raised into engineered failure stay stunted.
  4. Therefore: honest standards, real stakes, and adult refusal to seek ruin.
- **Picture:** Let the child lose the race and try again. Do not break their legs so the race is equal. Do not refuse to teach running because winning would be “privilege.”

## E. Social virtues and forms (main bulk, continued)

### E0. Social primer: there is no generic person — then scopes, tiers, and procedure
**Load-bearing claim (do not dilute):** There is no generic person. There is you—tied to your family, their lineage, and your situation. Social morality starts from that fact.

**Placement (hybrid):** One plant line at B8 after the axioms. Full primer here, opening the social bulk, before E2+ and before expanded scopes/tiers/symmetry.  
**Manuscript shape:** three units (¶1 positive core; ¶2 wrong turn + return from the positive; ¶3 personal narrative → transition). Not a bullet list in the book. No theorist names. No ethnoreligious package war in this primer.

#### Exact language to prefer (not metaphors)
Use the book’s own terms:
- **Reality is real, knowable, and consequential** (not “reality answers”).
- **Truth is what corresponds to reality** (not “the world speaks”).
- **Human life should continue and continue well** / survive and thrive (not bare “life continues”).
- **Humans are limited, self-interested, and not naturally good**; virtue is cultivated.
- **Priority is tiered and self-anchored.**
- **Justice is procedural symmetry within honest scopes.**

#### ¶1 — Positive core (writer target; keep strength and clarity)
Open hard: **There is no generic person.** There is you. You are tied to your family, to their lineage, and to your situation—body, place, language, debts, and the people who actually fed and formed you. That is not a costume over a blank token. That *is* the person who must survive and thrive. Human life should continue and continue well; the chain that produced you is how that happens in fact. Gratitude for what your line and household handed you is not theft. Duty is thickest to those people and that situation first. Preferring your children is not a bug. Handing on life, skill, and honest premises is how any free productive order outlasts one generation. Social rules exist so limited people bound this way can keep thrival going together without requiring angels.

#### ¶2a — Wrong turn (one short arc, not a list)
A common mistake starts from a true point (no one chose their birth) and slides away from *you, your family, your lineage, your situation*. Unchosen facts are treated as morally weightless. Care for your own is treated as prejudice. The self is redescribed as a generic person who could have held any slot, with no real parents and no real people. Justice is redescribed as what that generic person would pick without knowing their place—and often, in practice, as if many would simply pick goods without burdens: claims on what is produced, with no duty to the households and lines that produce it.

#### ¶2b — Reject from the positive (minor arc)
Reject that slide **from the positive**: if there is no generic person—only you, your family, lineage, and situation—then a morality built for a generic person is built for nobody real. People also differ: genetic potential for intelligence is largely set; environment and self-determination shape how far it is reached; all three vary. Capacity to track second- and third-order effects (what keeps prosperity and freedom in place) varies. Design that pretends everyone is the same foresighted cooperator—or that “choose any arrangement” would not yield mass demand for free stuff without responsibility—will be gamed and will hollow the platform. Thrival needs people who form, produce, and transmit *in their actual chains*, not only claim as tokens. Same rules in a shared scope remain necessary among those who share a public order. The fiction that you are a generic person does not.

#### ¶3 — Personal narrative → transition (writer target)
Write in a personal register (your household, your work, teaching *your* child a hard skill, keeping *your* roof—particular, not abstract). Show: reality is consequential; truth is correspondence to what works; you are trying to survive and thrive and hand something on **to yours**. People differ in talent, formation, and foresight. **End with the claim, not a dodge:** there is no generic person—there is you, your family, lineage, and situation. **Then the institutional turn:** given that, **how far do claims reach beyond your own, and what same rules bind us where our lives actually meet?** That is scope, tiered priority, and procedural symmetry. Transition into those sections next.

#### What this primer is not
- Not a soft slogan about “procedure and parents.” The hard claim is: **no generic person.**
- Not a named attack on any academic.
- Not collective blame by blood or creed; later chapters judge texts, institutions, and political programs.
- Not a claim that everyone has equal cognitive potential; variation is part of why generic-person design fails.

#### Charge of E0
**There is no generic person—only you, your family, their lineage, and your situation.** Refuse the slide that dissolves that into a token with cost-free claims. Then build social forms for those real people: scopes, tiers, same rules in honest ranges.

### E1. What “social virtue” means here
- Habits and rules that let many limited people survive and thrive together without requiring angels.
- Same axioms as personal virtue; larger scale.
- Assumes E0: **no generic person**—you, family, lineage, situation; uneven ability and foresight.

### E2. Rule of law
- Same public rules for those in the same legal position inside the polity.
- Rulers under law, not pure will.
- Distinctions must track real role differences (adult/child, citizen/non-citizen), not favored blood under a fake neutral label.

### E3. Honest speech
- Description that can be checked.
- Error correction needs minority claims heard.
- Fraud and coordinated lie are attacks on the process, not “just more speech” in moral weight.

### E4. Property and contract
- Ownership concentrates maintenance (D4).
- Contract is future cooperation made reliable.
- Theft and repudiation without remedy destroy thrival.

### E5. Family as civilizational technology
- Long dependency of human children forces long investment.
- First school of virtue and skill.
- Polity that treats family loyalty as bigotry saws off its own replacement and formation.

### E6. Formation and education
- Always happening; “neutral” school still forms.
- Aim: truth, competence, capacity to continue life upward.
- Capture of schools is capture of the next generation’s axioms.

### E7. Borders and membership
- Safety nets, jury duty, defense spending need a defined “we.”
- Humane procedure inside; not the claim that every human has identical claim on every treasury.
- Grade immigration policy by thrival of the existing people and capacity to absorb, under B3–B5.
- **B8 link:** Membership is not a marble lottery ticket. Peoples and polities are historical products; different spaces for different visions (B8) require borders and association rights that are not automatic bigotry.

### E8. Markets under scarcity
- Specialization forces trade.
- Prices and voluntary exchange reappear when banned because the problem remains.
- Not “markets are God”; they are discovered tools under limits. Fraud and coercion still crimes.

### E9. Defense and enemies
- **Enemy:** By acts and programs: force against your people; destruction of shared truth/law process; unbound will replacing reciprocal citizenship with power to impose it.
- **Opponent:** Shares enough of process to be answered inside law and argument.
- Failure to name enemies is not kindness; it is unpreparedness (history is full of ended peoples who were polite).
- Positive aim remains life, not permanent war romance.

### E10. Merit under honest procedure
- Scarce formation resources should grow real capacity (yeast), with character not assumed from scores alone.
- Corrupt selection: fix procedure.
- Envy as justice: equal outcomes by force; kills excellence and thrival.

### E11. Experiment and correction
- Bound trials; expand what works; kill what fails even if it felt moral.
- Reality judges (B1–B2).

### E12. Plural spaces under shared public axioms
- **Claim:** Different ultimate visions often need different spaces (B8).
- **Public floor:** B1–B5 where people must share streets, markets, and courts.
- **Above the floor:** churches, families, schools of choice, associations, and distinct polities may run thicker programs.
- **Error:** One campus or one state must embody every vision at full intensity; dissenters must be remade into generic humans.
- **Error opposite:** No shared floor at all; pure exit only, until force decides.
- **Positive:** Honest membership, exit where possible, procedure among those who share a jurisdiction, no marble myth that all must be one interchangeable pool.

## F. Falling off the boat: harmlessness entitlement

### F0. Temporary cushion is not the fall
- **Good (under B3):** Losing a job or going hungry for a short time should not mean death if the household, kin, mutual aid, or polity can prevent it at bearable cost.
- **Why good:** Life is the axiom. Short shocks (illness, layoff, bad season) are normal under limits. A society that can bridge those shocks without killing the person is thriving machinery, not decadence.
- **Pictures:** spare pantry; family loan; short unemployment help; emergency room for the crash; neighbor food after a fire.
- **Not the same as:** permanent exemption from work; multi-generation non-participation as a normal life script; “I must never feel scarcity”; open-ended claim on others’ labor with no reciprocity.

### F1. The slide (where we fall off the boat)
1. A society actually gets good at survival: food, medicine, police enough, long peace, and real short-term cushions (F0).
2. People stop feeling survival and mutual aid as hard-won. Weather, not work.
3. The cushion meant for shock is treated as a **way of life**: ongoing entitlement to be kept whole without contribution, formation, or acceptance of ranked duty.
4. From “I should not die from a two-month layoff” they slide to “I ought never be harmed, blocked, ranked, refused, or made uncomfortable for any reason.”
5. Pain, exclusion, fair failure, and duty start to look like injustices as such.
6. Policy and culture reorganize around eliminating felt harm and guaranteeing lifestyle insulation rather than protecting life and thrival under truth.
7. **B8 link:** Lottery language (“I didn’t choose to be born here / talented / whatever”) fuels the claim that all particular burdens are unfair; marble-self meets harmlessness entitlement.

### F2. Why the inference fails
- **Chain:**
  1. Survival was built by restraint, work, defense, and saying no.
  2. Saying no harms someone’s plan (the thief, the free rider, the person who wants your child unformed, the invader).
  3. If “no harm to anyone’s feelings or aims” is the master rule, you cannot stop predation or enforce standards.
  4. Then survival itself erodes, and the cushions of F0 collapse with it.
  5. Therefore “I must never be harmed in any way” is false under B3. Some harms are just consequences, deserved penalties, or the cost of protecting others.
- **Separate F0 from the slide:** Refusing death-from-short-shock is life-affirming. Treating endless insulation as a birthright is free-riding on the same life-axiom.
- **Concrete:** Arrest harms the burglar’s evening. Failing a student harms pride. Borders harm the plan of the person outside. Honest medical “no” harms a false hope. All can be required by life under truth.

### F3. What “harm” must mean under the axioms
- **Core harms to forbid:** murder, rape, enslavement, theft, fraud, unprovoked assault; destruction of the shared process of truth and law; preventable death from short, bridgable catastrophe when capacity exists (F0).
- **Not automatic moral crimes:** hurt feelings, status loss, fair competition lost, duty imposed, true description that shames, temporary scarcity while still fed and housed through work or time-limited help.
- **Trade:** Protecting A’s life and property often frustrates B’s will. That is not proof the protection is wrong.

### F4. Reactionary false fix (refute)
- **Reaction form:** “So cruelty is good. Softness is the enemy. Let the unemployed starve; cushions are always decadence. Mock the weak. Might is the only teacher.”
- **Why it fails:**
  1. B3 is thriving, not torment; F0 cushions are compatible with life as good.
  2. Cruelty as identity wastes people you need and corrupts judgment.
  3. Strength (D8) is capacity to defend life, not sadism.
  4. Reaction that only inverts the slogan stays tied to the slogan; it does not rebuild truth, tiers, or procedure.
  5. “No cushions ever” confuses the slide (entitlement as lifestyle) with the good tool (short bridge over shock).
- **Writer task:** Steel the reaction in one short paragraph of their voice, then break it on B1–B5, D8, and F0, then leave it.

### F5. Independent positive after F
- Survival is earned and maintained, not a vending machine of comfort.
- **Keep F0:** build household, kin, and civic bridges so short job loss or hunger does not equal death; keep them time-bound, reciprocal where possible, and aimed at return to contribution.
- **Refuse the slide:** no moral right to permanent non-participation as the default adult life; no right to never hear “no.”
- Adults accept ranked duty, fair no’s, just penalties, and allowed failure (D15).
- Compassion is real and scoped: carry the infant and the elder; bridge the shock; do not redefine predation or perpetual exemption as a self-esteem need.
- Personal: toughness in standards for yourself; justice toward others under same rules.
- Social: institutions that can say no without apology theater, can show mercy after judgment, and can fund emergency bridges without becoming a lifestyle engine.

### F6. Cushion design tests (writer checklist)
- Is this for a **shock** or for a **career of dependence**?
- Does it preserve life (B3) without killing work and formation (D2, D5, D7)?
- Time limit, work requirement, kin-first (B4), fraud control (B5)?
- If removed after recovery, does the person stand? If not, was it a bridge or a substitute life?

## G. Falling off the boat: standards in making (craft, building, image, music)

**Method discipline (hold to M’s honesty, not M’s proof bar):**  
This section does **not** prove a full history of modern art. It shows that **time effects, perverse incentives, and second- and third-order effects exist** in how standards of making are kept or lost. State a thrival-relevant **risk pattern**. Point to **domains to check**. Lower certainty than section M. Do not pretend taste wars are physics.

### G0. What we claim and what we do not
- **Claim:** Skill and standards must be taught and enforced or they thin within a generation or two (same as trades, literacy, law). Institutions can pay people more for shock, negation, and in-group status than for ordered, true, competent work. Later cohorts raised only on the revolt inherit less of the standard the revolt was against.
- **Do not claim:** A closed proof that every later style is thrival failure. A single villain decade. That “vitality” is as crisp a metric as orbital residuals.
- **Honesty line for manuscript:** We are not proving a museum chronology. We are naming mechanisms and places to look.

### G1. Mechanisms (demonstrate these exist; examples are indicators, not courtroom proof)

#### Time / multi-generation effects
- What one generation still *can do* (draw, join, proportion, build square, play in tune, finish a surface) the next cannot if training stops.
- First breakers often still *had* the training; their children may only have the ideology of the break.
- Parallel: take cushions for granted (F); take craft standards for granted (G).

#### Perverse incentives
- Status, grants, academic fashion, and gallery markets can reward: novelty-as-virtue, theory-as-substitute-for-skill, deliberate incompetence sold as critique, audience contempt as prestige.
- When those pay better than mastery, rational careerists follow the money and the clique—not “the muse.”
- Public money and school credentials can lock the perverse payoff in place.

#### Second- and third-order effects
- **2nd:** Students stop being drilled in hard fundamentals because fundamentals are labeled oppressive or obsolete.  
- **3rd:** A workforce and a public that cannot tell competence from fraud; buildings and images that fail basic order and honesty; “critique” that cannot build a chair that stands.
- Parallel to H10: short-run “works” (attention, prestige) can hide long-run skill death.

### G2. Risk pattern (tendency, not law)
1. A people can still train hands and eyes to ordered, true, finished work (D6).  
2. A first cohort attacks that standard while still able to meet it—negation with residual skill. Some of that work remains competent.  
3. Training and public reward shift toward the attack.  
4. Later cohorts get the attack as curriculum and market signal, not the lost standard.  
5. Incompetence and ugliness get moral cover: authenticity, subversion, anti-bourgeois, “harm” as depth.  
6. **Watch for:** whether the society can still *produce and staff* competence in making, or only consume prestige objects that require no skill to defend in words.

### G3. Domains to look into (not prove here)
Point the reader (or later research) at these; do not litigate each in full:
- Craft and trade schooling: drafting, shop, apprenticeship—cut or gutted?
- Architecture and building trades: can graduates detail a building that sheds water and fits together?
- Music and visual training: hours of drill vs theory-only credentials?
- Museums, art schools, grant bodies: what is rewarded—finished mastery or shock-plus-essay?
- Street and housing stock: daily environment ordered and maintained, or vandalism and neglect moralized?
- Elite speech: is calling garbage “important” a status test?

### G4. Wrong vs incomplete (one breath, from M)
- **Incomplete break:** still trained against a real standard; some value remains; domain not fully dead.  
- **Wrong as thrival practice:** formation that cannot produce competence, only negation and moral language—fails B3/D5 the way untaught trades fail.  
- Do not call every experiment “wrong.” Call **stopped transmission + perverse payoffs** the thrival failure mode.

### G5. What would count against the risk pattern
- Durable, widespread production of ordered, true, competent work **without** serious training in prior standards—or clear recovery of hard training and public reward for mastery after a negation-heavy phase.  
If evidence goes that way, drop or narrow the claim. Do not hermetically protect G.

### G6. Reactionary false fix (refute)
- **Form:** Ban everything after year X; only copy old work; novelty is sin; museums replace workshops.  
- **Fails:** Thriving needs living makers, not only curators. Copying is training, not the whole end. Pure nostalgia does not rebuild apprenticeship. Enemy-of-modern is still not D6 criteria.

### G7. Independent positive
- Drill fundamentals before ideology.  
- Copy excellence to gain skill; then make new work that still meets order and truth (vitality last and least used as a weapon).  
- Pay and credential competence, not only shock.  
- Keep ordinary places clean, finished, and honest in materials.  
- Treat “ugliness as virtue” and “skill as oppression” as propaganda against thrival, not as deep theory.

### G8. Parallel to F (structure only)
- F: take survival cushions for granted → entitlement slide.  
- G: take craft standards for granted → formation and incentive slide.  
- Same shape; different good; same demand for time, incentives, and second-order clarity.

## H. Ending arc: external principles, how we judge them, then alliances, then positive close

### H1. Purpose and order of this section
1. **Name** major principle-sets **external** to this book’s axioms (not only “the other side” as a blob).
2. State the book’s **church / knowledge divide**: what we test in reality vs what we leave to the divine.
3. State **limits** and how to read **2nd- and 3rd-order effects** (generational, population scale, hidden interconnects).
4. **Only then** practical alliances among external packages (and against shared obstacles).
5. Close on what we are for: life, virtues, justice under the axioms.

Do not open the ending with alliance theory. Alliance is downstream of named premises and of the test rule.

### H8. Name external principles (packages and axioms, not peoples)
- **Writer rule:** Say the rival’s starting points in their own terms briefly, then mark where they conflict with B1–B5 or with testable thrival. Persons ≠ packages.
- **Social constructivism:** Reality, knowledge, and often morals are products of power, language, or group position. The world does not get an independent veto. Conflict with B1–B2; often with B5 (procedure becomes power tool).
- **Divine constructivism / pure will packages:** What is true or what happens is fixed by unbound divine (or sacral-political) will in a way that denies stable secondary order or denies testing claims against creation. Occasionalism-style and “truth = what God thinks with no correspondence duty” sit here when they block reality’s veto. Conflict with B1–B2 as public method.
- **Scripturalism (as package):** Scripture (or equivalent) as sole axiom of knowledge; creation not a witness. Distinct from two-books theism that lets nature teach. Public conflict when it forbids reality-tests of testable claims.
- **Islamic doctrinal-political packages (not every Muslim):** Hard packages that can include: unbound implementation of divine law as the constitution of public order (e.g. Khomeini-style rule as application not legislation among citizens); two-tier standing (e.g. jizya structure); speech/concealment rules that break shared inquiry; where occasionalism is held, weakened natural regularity. Compatible strands exist (secondary-cause realists, falsafa, etc.); still name the **package** when the package is the issue [see ../sources/philosophy/islam_secondary_causes_brief.md].
- **Outcome equity as justice:** Unequal results prove unjust rules; procedure must be bent until outcomes match. Conflicts with B5 and often B2 (causes of disparity erased).
- **Blank slate / perfectibility / natural goodness:** Humans rewritten by order alone, or good until society corrupts them. Conflicts with B7; history grades large rewrites hard.
- **Marble-self / luck-egalitarian popular Rawls-use:** Generic human; particular chain weightless [B8].
- **Anti-natal / harm-reduction-as-highest-good:** Life optional or footprint to shrink. Conflicts with B3.
- **Reactionary anti-procedure / anti-success packages:** Right-coded forms that reject same rules, excellence, or individual accountability (e.g. fate-sharing sacral leveling). Fail B3/B5 even when they hate the left.
- **Universal action-duty / tiers as bigotry:** Conflicts with B4.
- **Writer:** Short dossier per package is enough. No equal-length steelman of every school. History and present examples do the work.

### H9. Church and state of knowledge (this book’s divide)
- **Primary teaching location:** Section M (after axioms). Ending restates; does not first introduce.
- **Rule:**
  1. If a question **can be tested in reality**, test it in reality (B1–B2, M).
  2. If a question **cannot be tested in reality**, this book does **not** decide it. Let the divine speak there (for those who hold a divine). Or leave it open.
- **We offer nothing for or against the words of the divine as such.** Not a catechism. Not an anti-catechism.
- **What we do claim authority to judge:**
  - Claims about bridges, harvests, bodies, incentives, fertility, crime, conquest, literacy, famine, institutional fraud: reality answers.
  - Practices and laws that produce visible thrival or ruin under human limits.
  - Models: useful prediction is not full validity; wrong vs incomplete [M2–M4].
- **Two-books theists:** Can hold revelation and still accept reality-tests for testable things; no quarrel from this book on the method.
- **Public order:** Shared streets need shared tests for shared empirical claims. Sacral answers that override a failed empirical test are packages we grade by consequence, not by insulting God.

### H10. Limits and 2nd- / 3rd-order effects
- **Know our limits [B7]:** We do not see all chains. Humility (D10) is required. That is not an excuse to skip tests we can run.
- **How to study effects:** History + present cases; not slogans. Ask what happened when a premise was held for a long time, by many people, under real scarcity and power.
- **Generational effects (multi-generation embrace):**
  - Some results appear only after several generations hold the premise.
  - Original meaning can drain out; the slogan stays while the **system that held it together** (family form, elite restraint, shared faith, craft guild, shame culture) dies.
  - First generation may live on inherited capital (skill, trust, beauty, fertility) while teaching the opposite; later generations inherit the teaching without the capital [rhyme with G1, F1].
- **Population / scale effects:**
  - Some practices work when a **small percent** of people run them (sect, vanguard, subculture, experimental art, temporary radicalism).
  - The same practices **degrade** when a large share of the population adopts them (fertility collapse, speech taboos, outcome-equity capture of schools, dual-code enforcement).
  - Do not grade a premise only by a monastery, a campus clique, or a founding generation of saints.
- **Interconnecting / non-obvious related effects:**
  - Second order: effect of the policy on behavior (incentives).
  - Third order: effect of that new behavior on other systems (trust, formation, borders, who holds power).
  - Chains that are not advertised: e.g. “compassion” → weaker enforcement → more victims; “equal dignity rhetoric” → outcome equity machinery; “anti-borders” → safety-net math breaks; “only revelation” in public science → technology and medicine starve or become hypocrisy.
- **Writer duty:** For each major external package named in H8, where space allows, point to at least one generational, one scale, or one interconnect example—not a monograph.

### H2. Logic of “enemies of my enemy” without shared good (after H8–H10)
- **Chain:**
  1. Two packages can disagree on God, sex, property, and end-state utopia [H8].
  2. Both can still face the same obstacle: a society that runs on correspondence, individual under law, borders, family, merit, open criticism (B1–B5 practiced).
  3. Removing the obstacle helps both short-term.
  4. Coordination cost is low when the shared target is concrete.
  5. After victory, residual conflict returns; the harder or better-placed package purges the partner (alliance then purge).
- **Writer:** Mechanism, not mind-meld. Only after packages and the test rule are clear.

### H3. Mechanisms of practical alliance (checklist)
- **Common enemy / common blockers:** neutral rule of law, borders, free inquiry, bourgeois family norms, high-performing open orders under hostile pressure (class, not a single named state for now).
- **Shared political tongue:** oppression, resistance, illegitimacy of the current order, without shared metaphysics.
- **Truth as instrument:** reality may not veto the cause [ties social and some divine will packages].
- **Complementary roles:** narrative/institutional shield + demographic/organizational sword (roles, not races).
- **Dual codes:** in-group vs out-group morals; purity loading by class/sex/creed.
- **Collective subject** over limited individual under same law.
- **Victim/humiliation politics:** different stories, same permission to break ordinary restraints.
- **Speech sacralization:** explicit blasphemy and implicit identity blasphemy.
- **Anti-merit / anti-thrival strains** where present.
- **Entryism and capture** as method.
- **Prime example:** red–green style coordination (progressive left packages with political-Islamist / hard packages). Tendencies, not a census of persons. See also `../sources/notes/red_green_alliance.md`.

### H4. Why alliances do not refute our axioms
- Bad alliances show power seeks partners; they do not falsify B1–B5.
- Seeing the pattern is survival (D8, E9).
- Answer is not “ally with anyone who hates them.” Hold the axioms and the virtues; judge packages by reality-tests and long-horizon effects [H9–H10].

### H5. Premises cut labels
- Return to the five questions [Q]: same label, different answers → cannot build.
- People who share a jersey split on axioms.
- People who differ on God can still share B1–B5 for public cooperation when they accept reality-tests for testable claims [H9].
- Incompatible pairs: correspondence vs truth-as-power; life-as-good vs life-as-optional; same rules vs outcome equity; tiers vs universal action-duty; reciprocal citizenship vs sacral/party unbound will; particular chain vs marble-self; plural spaces vs forced one-world synthesis.

### H6. What we are not ultimately for
- Not “keep democracy” as the highest aim.
- Not “keep the republic / monarchy / prime minister / party” as the highest aim.
- Those are instruments. Grade them by life and thrival under B1–B5.
- Idolatry of the instrument forgets B3.

### H7. What we are for (positive close)
- Life and thriving across this generation and the next.
- Personal virtues: truth, responsibility (create and maintain), ownership, excellence, beauty, children or raising-up, strength, judgment, humility, scoped love; allow failure without courting it.
- Social virtues: law as same process, honest speech, family, formation, borders that work, defense, merit under honest procedure; bridges for shock not lifestyle engines; plural spaces under a shared testable floor.
- True justice: procedural symmetry in honest scopes.
- Clear sight: enemies by acts and programs; external packages named; testable claims tested; divine words not fake-settled by this book; long-horizon effects watched.
- **Final stress:** Weight of the book is how to live and build. End on construction: clean the floor, keep the skill, raise the child, tell the truth, hold the line, make something worth handing on.

## I. Tone and prose constraints (for final writer)

### I1. Cut
- Stacked abstract nouns with no picture (“structural paradigm of relational flourishing”).
- Restating the same claim three ways in one paragraph.
- “It is important to note that…”
- Equal-length tours of every rival school.

### I2. Prefer
- One claim, one chain, one picture, one duty.
- Short sentences when the step is simple.
- Names of acts: sweep, repair, refuse, teach, plant, guard, measure, admit error.

### I3. Length allocation (target feel)
- Axioms: tight, clear, early.
- Virtues survival/thriving middle: **longest**.
- Fall-off harm + beauty: medium each, parallel skeleton.
- Alliances/conflict: medium, sharp.
- Positive close: short, strong, no new theory.

### I4. Words to use carefully
- “Frame,” “mirror,” “bundle,” “inversion”: avoid as architecture. Patterns of failure can be named in plain speech.
- “Harm”: define before moralizing.
- “Justice”: default to same rules in scope unless explicitly discussing rivals.
