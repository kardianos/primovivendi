## P2.A The world is consequential and has natural constraints

### P2.A1 Core claim
- Reality answers (Part 1 B1). Bad maps cost food, blood, and children.
- Many human arrangements are **forms** (how we label, schedule, coin, and dress a practice).
- Under the forms sit **constraints and problems that reappear** wherever limited people face scarcity, specialization, sex, childhood dependency, and violence.
- Redesign that only renames the form does not abolish the constraint. It often drives the real practice underground and pays the cost of pretense.

### P2.A2 Discovered vs constructed (positive tool)
- **Constructed / form:** Could have been otherwise without changing the underlying problem (which side of the road; which word for bread; paper vs metal token as money form).
- **Discovered / substance:** Reappears under independent conditions because the problem remains (need for stable exchange medium under specialization; long investment in helpless children; need for predictable rules against collision and theft).
- **Test:** If unrelated peoples reinvent the arrangement when the official form is banned, you are looking at substance, not pure costume.
- **Chain:**
  1. Limited people specialize (Part 1 B7).
  2. Specialization forces exchange, trust, and deferred claims.
  3. Decrees can change the *form* of money, marriage law, or schooling.
  4. Decrees cannot cancel scarcity, childhood dependency, or the need for reliable labels.
  5. Policies that treat substance as pure construct fail as black markets, broken formation, and empty tables.
- **Not:** “Everything traditional is sacred.” Test customs by truth and thrival (Part 1 B2–B3). Reform forms; respect constraints.
- **Not:** “Nothing may change.” Forms often should change when they fail thrival under present conditions.

### P2.A3 Pictures (writer stock)
- Money form changes; exchange under scarcity returns.
- Official language reforms weekly; coordination fails.
- Property banned; informal ownership and theft patterns return.
- Family roles redesigned on paper; children still need years of feeding and teaching.

### P2.A4 Link to Part 1
- M: models can “work” in form while wrong on substance—same caution for social redesign.
- B4–B5: tiers and procedure are constraints on duty and justice, not decorations.
- H10: scale and generation—forms that “work” for a clique fail when a whole population runs them.

### P2.A5 Charge
- Ask form or substance before celebrating redesign.
- Keep what thrival requires; change what is only costume or failed costume.
- The world is consequential. Natural constraints do not care about the slogan.

## P2.B Words and tests get rigged

### P2.B1 Hermetic argument
- **Definition:** Every failure is declared “not real X”; every success is still claimed for X. Blame never attaches; credit always does.
- **Why it fails Part 1 B5/M5:** Same procedure must assign cause to success and failure.
- **Fix:** If no failure can count against the ideal, no success may be credited either—or better, drop the one-way valve and keep a real ledger.

### P2.B2 Selective logic
- Clear structure while the argument favors the preferred end; the same standard becomes “reductive” or “violence” when it does not.
- Tell of procedural asymmetry at the level of reasoning itself.

### P2.B3 “Not real X”
- Narrower test: do the system’s own central texts and classical practice, under its own rules of reading, support the conduct?
- If yes, the problem is in premises or tradition, not only “bad apples.”
- If no, recognized authorities should say so consistently; silence is information.
- Same test for sacred and secular packages.

### P2.B4 Freedom of speech as equipment for truth
- Under limits, error is found by collision of claims.
- Central censors are neither wise nor disinterested enough to filter without thrival cost.
- Fraud and coordinated lie against the process remain punishable; that is not the same as banning uncomfortable description.
- Link Part 1 E3, M5.

### P2.B5 Charge
- Name hermetic valves and selective standards when you see them.
- Keep speech open enough that reality can still correct the map.

## P2.C Justice counterfeits

### P2.C1 Three things called fair
| Name | Claim | Problem under Part 1 |
|------|--------|----------------------|
| **Procedural symmetry** | Same rules within honest scope | Load-bearing (B5) |
| **Outcome symmetry / equity** | Same end-states; disparity proves injustice | Requires unequal procedure; erases causes; breeds equalizing class |
| **Value symmetry** | Equal return in kind (eye for eye) | Backward ledger; feud; does not build forward cooperation |

### P2.C2 Rule of law vs rule by man
- **Rule of law:** Stable public rules; rulers under the same process; distinctions track real roles (adult/child, citizen/non-citizen), not fashion exemption.
- **Rule by man:** Will of the powerful or the favored coalition replaces published process.
- Selective prosecution by jersey is rule by man even when courts still have buildings.

### P2.C3 Link to marble-self (Part 1 B8)
- Lottery metaphysics fuels outcome equity: every gap is arbitrary luck.
- Particular duties and honest selection are then called bias.

### P2.C4 Charge
- Name which “fair” is being demanded.
- Prefer same process in honest scopes; refuse feud justice and forced sameness of results as the definition of justice.

## P2.D Misused compassion and false foundations

**What this section is:** How real virtues (compassion, kindness, patience with difference) get mis-aimed so that predators, free riders, and platform-destroyers are protected, and how some modern “foundational values” fail as foundations. Not a metaphor title for its own sake.

### P2.D1 Compassion used to block enforcement / reallocate harm
- **Structure:** Real problem exists → reframe as only background injustice → remedy is always leniency / reduced enforcement → offender becomes patient; next victim pays.
- **Strategic form:** Adversary invokes your tolerance, kindness, due process, or guilt so you cannot defend the platform. They need not believe the virtues; they need you to.
- **Rule for refusal:** If applying the principle exactly as demanded would destroy the society that holds the principle, the demand is a misapplication (suicide-pact reading). Life and thrival constrain how virtues are applied (Part 1 B3, F).
- **Picture:** Soft prosecution; open-ended “understand the predator”; borders dissolved as cruelty—while harm reallocates to the undefended.

### P2.D2 Kindness vs politeness
- **Kindness:** Costly, intimate, patient; belongs where abuse can be policed (family, close community).
- **Politeness:** Cheap, general, procedural; cities of strangers run on it.
- **Error:** Institutionalize unscoped kindness as default to every stranger → free riders and predators harvest it.
- **Rule:** Kindness near; politeness in the open civic field.

### P2.D3 False foundational virtues
- **Tolerance as foundation:** Only the intolerant are unconstrained; they win. Ordinary patience with difference is fine; foundation-status is not. (Marcuse one-way tolerance as explicit political form—name if useful.)
- **Diversity as foundation:** Complementary skills enrich; diversity in whether contracts and truth bind is coming apart. The word alone cannot tell which.
- **Non-judgment as foundation:** Procedure and mercy require naming wrong first (Part 1 D9). Non-judgment is abdication sold as humility.
- **Harm reduction as foundation:** Seatbelts good; as highest good it has no stop and can end in anti-life (Part 1 B3, H8).

### P2.D4 Parasitism (named set)
- **Ideological:** Convert and persuade; do not replace themselves; live on host fertility and platform.
- **Social:** Will-not vs cannot—permanent claim without contribution when capacity exists (ties Part 1 F0 vs F1).
- **Technological:** Consume tech and medicine while rejecting the practices and formation that produce them.

### P2.D5 Doomerism as self-permission
- Analysis that always ends “so effort is pointless” is often an exemption from work and defense (Part 1 D12).
- Full development here: mood, status payoff, and thrival cost.

### P2.D6 Link to Part 1 F
- F0 cushions for shock remain good.
- This section is how cushions and virtues are bent into lifestyle entitlement and platform surrender.

### P2.D7 Charge
- Name the weapon when compassion is used to reallocate harm.
- Keep kindness scoped; keep judgment; keep foundations as truth, life, limits, tiers, procedure—not tolerance-as-god.

## P2.E Historical examples under real constraints

**What this section is:** Concrete cases of societies acting under scarcity, enemies, and bad options—so multi-generation and large-scale effects are visible, not only argued in the abstract.

### P2.E1 Why history here
- Part 1 H10: multi-generation, scale, interconnect effects.
- Abstract alliance and purge stay unreal until a people does them.
- Pick depth over catalog: **one strong positive case** and **one strong rupture/purge case** beat five shallow names.

### P2.E2 Positive spine (Singapore-class / Lee)
- World does not owe a living.
- Limited multi-racial island; no hinterland fantasy.
- Merit and formation under scarcity; order as survival equipment; refuse outcome equity as justice.
- Grade by thrival results under constraints—not saint biography.

### P2.E3 Rupture spine (Russia-class)
- Incremental reform path under constraints (property, incentives) interrupted.
- Warning of brittle social structure under great war (Durnovo-class).
- Explicit rupture program (Lenin-class April logic): break the machine, do not channel it.
- Grade: civil war, famine, forced collectivization, empty stalls—reality’s ledger.

### P2.E4 Alliance-then-purge spine (Iran-class)
- Temporary coalition of incompatible packages against shared target.
- Harder or better-placed package sorts partners after victory.
- Lesson: shared enemy is not shared axioms (Part 1 H2–H3 made concrete).

### P2.E5 Design for non-angels (founding / Madison-class)
- Faction and self-interest assumed; ambition counters ambition.
- Procedure and divided power as thrival tools under B7.
- Optional short foil: not utopia of perfect citizens.

### P2.E6 Right false friend (Dugin-class adversary text)
- Surface: enemy of progressive inversion.
- Substance often fails thrival and procedure: anti-success, co-suffering as justice, collective subject without accountable individuals, multipolar common-enemy politics.
- Test is Part 1 axioms—not jersey color.
- Persons ≠ package; name the package.

### P2.E7 Enemy recognition (optional short exemplum)
- Principled non-recognition of enemies who state extermination intent → destruction (Moriori-class).
- Strength (Part 1 D8) is not optional soft manners.

### P2.E8 Charge
- Read history as consequence under constraints, not as costume drama.
- Copy what thrives under limits; refuse rupture romance and false friends.

## P2.F Packages and purity (adversary substance)

### P2.F1 Standing rule
- **Persons ≠ packages.** Officials and readers aim at texts, institutions, and demonstrated political commitments—not surnames or private piety alone.
- Premises cut labels (Part 1 H5).

### P2.F2 Purity vs reality procedure
- **Purity systems:** Sort clean/unclean by membership or supposed nature; load moral procedure from the sort (dual codes: strict for “ours,” license toward “theirs”; or sex-class purity loading).
- **Reality procedure:** Same basic bans and duties under facts, scoped honestly; virtue as cultivated thrival capacity, not halo or checklist alone.
- See working note `purity_virtue_reality.md` for sexual/exemplum material; keep Part 2 at procedure altitude unless author expands exempla.

## P2.G Practice close

### P2.G1 Precepts matrix (lite)
- Run every serious decision through a fixed short list. Fail any one → stop or revise.
- Suggested filters (align to Part 1 + Part 2):
  1. Reality / correspondence — is the claim testable, and does it pass? (M)  
  2. Wrong vs incomplete — are we treating a useful wrong story as final? (M)  
  3. Form vs substance — redesign costume or deny constraint? (P2.A)  
  4. Life and thrival — continue and continue well? shock cushion vs lifestyle free-ride? (B3, F)  
  5. Tiers — who is owed first? (B4)  
  6. Procedural symmetry — same rules in honest scope? (B5)  
  7. Optional seventh: long-horizon / scale — what if many hold this for decades? (H10)  
- **All-must-pass.** Forced stretch to get a pass is information: desire is driving.

### P2.G2 Scenarios (lite)
- Few decisions only; rising stakes of one life/polity if possible.
- Examples: kitchen budget; school discipline; hiring; speech at work; border/membership; soft prosecution; art/funding ugly-as-virtue; temporary coalition with incompatible package.
- Each: run matrix; name package if present; choose and own maintenance.

### P2.G3 Begin with yourself
- Society is individuals under procedures (Part 1).
- One concrete act of create-and-maintain before the next indictment.
- Allow failure in training; do not court failure for self or children (D15).

### P2.G4 Closing charge of Part 2 / book
- The world is consequential and has natural constraints.
- Keep maps that track it; keep procedure honest; keep life and thrival as the aim.
- Refuse counterfeit justice and weaponized compassion.
- Learn from peoples who thrived under limits and from ruptures that emptied the stalls.
- Name packages; do not hunt blood.
- Then do the work: repair, teach, raise, defend, tell the truth.
