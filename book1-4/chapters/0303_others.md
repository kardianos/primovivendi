## Others (Relating to Others)

Human beings relate to one another through the limits that force specialization and through the duties that follow from actual relationships and capacities.

### Consequences of Complexity and Specialization

A farmer must spend their days in the field. A tailor must spend their days indoors. It is difficult to do both well.

Even when one person can do many things, time is finite. People specialize.

Reality is more complex and more time-consuming than any single person can master. People must work together. Society is the byproduct of human limits meeting a demanding world.

### Dispositional Love and Action-Based Love

Dispositional love is a general good will toward others. It costs little, scales widely, and serves as the right default in most situations.

Action-based love requires time, attention, and sacrifice. It costs much, scales poorly, and must be spent according to tiered priority.

Confusing the two is the root of much ruin. People asked to act with love toward everyone end up acting with love toward no one in particular and with no depth.

### Self-Anchored Tiered Priority

Priority begins with the self and spouse, then family, then neighbors and community, then the state and country, then the world and beyond.

A person may not drop a tier. The habit of treating distant strangers as more important than parents is an artifact of the inverted frame, not a virtue.

Enemies are real. Some will physically assault a person. Others will work patiently against a person's interests, against their community, or to dismantle the society they live in to remake it in their preferred image. Both count as enemies.

The inverted frame denies tiers. It tells people to care for the furthest stranger first. It pathologizes kin selection. It rebrands enemies as victims. When compassion is invoked to demand that a person betray an inner tier for an outer one, the pattern is the same.

### Procedural Symmetry Scoped to Tiers

Within a tier or role, the same procedures apply to everyone in that scope.

Across tiers, asymmetry is correct. Citizens receive citizen treatment. Non-citizens do not. Children receive child treatment. Parents do not.

This scoping is not a weakening of symmetry. It is what makes symmetry operable. Without it, procedural symmetry collapses into universalist absurdity in which a person owes a stranger overseas the same concern as their own child.

Value symmetry demands that equal acts be returned in kind. Hand for hand, eye for eye. Some cultures still practice variants of this.

Outcome symmetry demands the same end-state for each person regardless of effort, talent, choices, or reality. Disparity becomes prima facie proof of injustice.

Outcome symmetry breeds corruption. Humans vary. Central rules cannot capture local realities. People respond to incentives. Punish success or reward failure and the result is failure or cheating.

Outcome symmetry entrenches classism. Someone must pay the bill. A new ruling class always emerges. Corruption becomes the membership test of the new class. Mobility ends.

### Examples of Scoped Symmetry

In the family tier, symmetry means equal parental treatment of each child. There are no favorites. Asymmetry across tiers means a person owes their children more than their neighbor's children. This is correct tiered priority, not unequal treatment. Parents have authority that children do not, but that authority is bounded by a symmetric obligation to each child.

In the citizen tier, symmetry means the same criminal law and the same procedural rights for all citizens. Asymmetry across tiers means citizens receive safety nets, voting rights, and legal standing that non-citizens do not. Borders perform this function. Government has power over citizens, but that power is bounded by procedural rules that protect each citizen equally. The president does not receive a different criminal code.

In role-scoped settings, symmetry means all judges owe all defendants the same due process and all teachers owe all students the same standard. Asymmetry across roles means a judge has power a defendant does not and a teacher has authority a student does not. The power is legitimate because the role is defined, the procedures are symmetric within it, and the role itself serves a tier.

Procedural symmetry does not extend across borders the way it does within them. International law is not real law in the way domestic law is. Military might is the ultimate arbiter. This is the recognition that the scope of procedural symmetry is the polity, not the planet.

The parent and child relationship is the canonical case. Tiered priority means children come before strangers. Power over children means parents make decisions children cannot. This power is legitimate because the child is within the parent's tiered responsibility and lacks the capacity for full self-anchoring. Procedural symmetry within the family means each child is owed equal concern. It does not mean identical treatment, since children differ, but it does mean symmetric procedural treatment with no arbitrary favoritism. Procedural symmetry within the parental role means all parents owe all their children the same standard of care. A parent does not get to arbitrarily neglect one child any more than a judge gets to arbitrarily deny one defendant due process.

The parent's power is justified by tiered responsibility, bounded by procedural symmetry, temporary because capacity transfers as the child grows, and accountable because the community or nation tier can intervene if the symmetric obligation of care is broken.

The pattern for all legitimate power is this: power is legitimate when it serves tiered responsibility and is bounded by procedural symmetry within its scope.

### Power Is Real and Necessary

An HOA president has real power. A parent has real power over a child. A teacher presides over students. A police officer carries the authority to arrest.

The claim that no one should pick up the bat of power is foolish. Power is necessary. The question is whether it is bounded.

Power is good when it remains bounded by the same law as everyone else and serves the tier within which it is exercised.

The inverted frame holds power illegitimate unless it enforces equity, in which case it is unlimited. This is power-worship dressed as power-skepticism.

### Division of Power

A statesman, an HOA board member, or a CEO is a specialization. They handle details. If they do it poorly, they can be replaced. This is the reality of human limits applied to authority.

People do not expect the powerful to be perfect. They expect the system to limit damage and to remove those who fail when required. The lesson of bad parents is not that parental authority is evil. It is that those particular parents were bad.

At times power is divided between authority and the public. At times it is divided generationally as children grow up. All of it is procedural symmetry meeting the limited nature of humans.

### Rule of Law

Truth and procedural symmetry mean the law applies to all within its scope.

Rule of law does not mean rule by bureaucrats. Bureaucratic rule under shifting guidance is the opposite of rule of law.

The law exists only as far as it is enforced. Unequal enforcement is de facto unequal law.

Humans are limited. The law must be knowable. It cannot be too large or too complicated.

Leniency toward criminals is violence to the innocent. This is not punitive maximalism. It is the recognition that unequal enforcement reallocates harm from the predator to the victim, who has done nothing wrong.

Removing the small percentage of those who are violent measurably changes the nature of a country.

### Freedom of Speech

Aligning with truth requires that society discuss ideas. Without open discussion, errors cannot be corrected and flourishing is capped.

Humans are limited. No central authority is wise enough to filter speech without doing more damage than the speech itself could cause.

The inverted frame treats speech as harm and silence as safety. It is the inversion of every condition under which truth has ever been found.

### Borders and Common Defense

Borders allow safety nets paid for by citizens and available to citizens. Procedural symmetry and tiered priority operate together in this arrangement.

Borders are protection against enemies.

Borders allow different laws and customs to coexist without forced universal acceptance. Borders and laws correspond.

There is no such thing as international law in the strong sense. The only real international law is military might.

### Unity and Exclusion

Unity implies exclusion. To be unified in something means there are states a person or a society does not have.

Unity is good when its content is good. Unity is the sum of vectors. Pointed well, society can do big things. Pointed poorly, society can do big damage.

Exclusion is not a weapon. It is the recognition of fundamental incompatibility. Some things cannot be inside.

### Survival Ultimately Requires Force

Enemies are real. This is true at the individual level and at the national level.

Rule of law, which is procedural symmetry, drastically reduces the need for blunt force and clarifies expectations for everyone. Force is still there. It is held in reserve.

### Ownership and Markets

Ownership is real and necessary. Responsibility cannot exist outside ownership.

Markets and currency are not a philosophy. They are emergent from specialization. Treating markets as ideology rather than as emergent technology is a category error common to both their critics and their worshippers.

Failure must be allowed for ownership to be real.

### Forgiveness

Personal release is the choice to let go of hurt or wrong so a person can continue to move forward. It is a virtue against bitterness.

Social reconciliation requires accountability, demonstrated change, and truth from the one who did the wrong. This is not a feeling. It is a status earned through real action.

Love is holding on. Forgiveness is letting go. Both are useful for long-term personal and social relationships.

The inverted frame conflates personal release with social reconciliation. It demands the latter without the necessary conditions and then weaponizes the demand against the wronged party.

### Education

Each generation must be taught the skills to replicate and improve the technology, both material and social, that they inherit.

Society itself is a form of technology. People must not lie about its structure or basis when they teach it.

Truth, beauty, and excellence must be taught explicitly. Humans are limited and self-interested. None of these is the default.
