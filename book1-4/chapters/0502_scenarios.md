## Matrix in Action: Scenarios

The Precepts Matrix becomes concrete when applied to real decisions. Each of the following scenarios shows the six filters run in order, followed by the balanced decision and the counter-example that results when one or more filters are ignored.

### Scenario 1: Cleaning and Organizing Your Home

Survival (Flourishing Propagation): The action increases the long-term platform for family health, joy, and raising the next generation well.

Reality Check: Dirt, clutter, and chaos have real negative health, mental, and social consequences.

Human Limits Check: You cannot magically outsource everything or live in chaos without cost. The work begins with your own effort.

Tiered Responsibility: Self and immediate family come first. This is the oxygen mask principle. Tidy your space before demanding help from others.

What Must Be Good: The result creates beauty, order, ownership, and a platform for joy, including dancing, friends, games, and hospitality.

Procedural Symmetry: You do not demand that others clean your house while you neglect your own. The rule applies equally.

Balanced Decision: Take ownership. Clean regularly. This builds a stable base for flourishing life and future generations.

Counter-Example: Ignoring reality plus responsibility leads to living in filth because cleaning is oppressive. The result is health decline, depression, and inability to host or raise children. Survival quality collapses.

### Scenario 2: Deciding Whether to Have More Children

Survival (Flourishing Propagation): The decision directly propagates your lineage, culture, and values in a high-quality way that is worth continuing.

Reality Check: Biological clock, financial realities, parenting demands, and child outcomes are concrete facts.

Human Limits Check: You cannot love children into existence without personal capacity. Limits on time, energy, and money are real.

Tiered Responsibility: Secure self and marriage first, then children, before wider community or global demands.

What Must Be Good: Children are the ultimate good. They bring love, meaning, joy, continuity, and the only way survival has purpose.

Procedural Symmetry: You accept the same rules and expectations you place on others regarding family formation and support.

Balanced Decision: Have children only when you can responsibly anchor them. Prioritize quality plus responsibility over raw quantity.

Counter-Example: Raw survival only, ignoring limits plus what must be good, leads to having many children in chaos. The result is poverty, resentment, and poor outcomes for the next generation. Or the right to exist victim framing leads to demanding that society subsidize endless children without personal ownership, collapsing tiers and symmetry.

### Scenario 3: Forgiving Someone Who Betrayed You

Survival (Flourishing Propagation): Long-term relationship survival and social cohesion matter for your group's ability to flourish.

Reality Check: Honestly assess what actually happened, the evidence, and the realistic likelihood of genuine change.

Human Limits Check: You are also selfish and flawed. Holding endless grudges is personally exhausting and self-destructive.

Tiered Responsibility: Family and community tier takes clear precedence over abstract forgive everyone or distant strangers.

What Must Be Good: The decision combines love, holding on to the relationship, plus forgiveness, letting go of the debt, to restore trust, joy, and platforms for more life.

Procedural Symmetry: Would you want the exact same mercy, process, and accountability if the roles were reversed?

Balanced Decision: Forgive where reality and accountability allow. Restore where possible, but maintain firm boundaries. Rebuild the relationship only if it strengthens flourishing survival.

Counter-Example: Ignoring reality plus symmetry leads to unconditional love and light forgiveness, followed by repeated betrayal and erosion of self. Or ignoring tiers leads to forgiving distant activists while nursing endless grudges against close family, producing social isolation and a weakened core survival circle.

### Scenario 4: Major Career Opportunity Requiring Heavy Travel or Family Sacrifice

Survival (Flourishing Propagation): The opportunity must ultimately support long-term family stability and propagation, not hollow status.

Reality Check: Real financial upside exists, but so do concrete costs to marriage, children, and health.

Human Limits Check: You have limited time and energy. Burnout, divorce, or distant parenting are predictable outcomes.

Tiered Responsibility: Family tier comes before career ambition or community praise. Secure the core first.

What Must Be Good: Balance excellence in work with ownership of family life, beauty of presence, and joy.

Procedural Symmetry: Apply the same standards to others. Do not demand that your spouse sacrifice everything while refusing any sacrifice yourself.

Balanced Decision: Take the opportunity only if it strengthens the core tiers without destroying family platforms. Otherwise decline or negotiate terms that preserve the bundle.

Counter-Example: Ignoring tiers plus limits leads to chasing status and money at the expense of children. The result is a broken family, regret, and eventual demographic dead-end. Pure I deserve this ambition without the survival filter leads to personal success that fails to propagate.

### Scenario 5: Encountering the Political Claim That This Country or Land Is Stolen

Survival (Flourishing Propagation): Group survival and continuity require defending what your people have built and improved.

Reality Check: Historical reality is layered conquest everywhere. No land is pristine. The current civilization delivers real goods.

Human Limits Check: No society can survive on perpetual guilt, reparations without end, or unlimited claims on the living.

Tiered Responsibility: Nation and community tier must come before abstract global justice or outsider demands.

What Must Be Good: Strength, responsibility, beauty, and ordered liberty of the existing civilization are goods worth propagating.

Procedural Symmetry: Rules must apply symmetrically. If all lands are stolen, then no polity has any legitimate claim, including the one making the accusation.

Balanced Decision: Reject the inherently petitionary right to exist framing that begs some higher authority to adjudicate. Survival is asserted through power, responsibility, and law, which is ultimately made of swords. Defend borders, culture, and inheritance as tiered duty.

Counter-Example: The petitionary right to exist appeal, ignoring survival plus reality plus tiers, leads to endless guilt, demographic surrender, open borders, and loss of the polity that enables all other goods. The system collapses because raw survival without the bundle is not worth propagating.
