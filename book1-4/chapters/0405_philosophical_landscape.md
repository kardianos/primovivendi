## The Philosophical Landscape: Where the Bundle Stands

The five principles are common ground available to theists and atheists alike. This is one of the frame's most distinctive features. Atheism says no more about a person's premises than theism does. The five principles cut across both. Faith and atheism are treated here as domains in which the bundle applies, not as separate topics with their own chapter.

### To Theists

Some theists hold the bundle in full. Some do not. The label Christian, Muslim, or Jew alone does not tell you which. The premise test does.

Within Christianity, two-books theology, in which nature and scripture both reveal, sits compatibly with the bundle. Scripturalism, the position associated with Crampton that a proposition is true because God thinks it to be true, is incompatible because it severs truth from correspondence with reality. Some recent Jesuit, United Methodist, and progressive-church positions have absorbed the inverted bundle and are incompatible regardless of denominational continuity.

Within Islam, the bundle is fundamentally tied to the Quran and Muhammad and to the logic that follows. Ash'arite occasionalism, the doctrine that God is the direct cause of every event, severs causality from regularity and is incompatible. The expected criticisms must be addressed directly.

The claim is not that the bundle replaces faith. The claim is that without the bundle, faith cannot operate in society without devolving into either parasitism or tyranny.

### To Atheists

Some atheists replace God with the state or with an ideology. Whatever serves the state or the ideology becomes good by definition. This is the inverted bundle in atheist clothing.

Some atheists care only about themselves. Some care more about distant strangers than about their own neighbors. Some believe that humans should go away.

Some atheists do seek truth, hold tiered priority instinctively, and act with procedural symmetry. These are the atheists with whom this frame is common ground.

The recommendation is to ground yourself and your family in explicit principles that lead to human life and flourishing. Without them, you or your children will bend to whatever inverted bundle is dominant in your social environment. Adopt the five principles as a bulwark.

The payoff is this. With the bundle, an atheist can work and reason with theists who also seek truth. The line is not theism versus atheism. The line is the bundle versus its inversion.

### To Readers Caught Between

The premise test, whether there is truth and whether it can be known, and the four questions that follow, does not require a person to have answered the God question. A person can begin from the bundle and let the deeper questions resolve in their own time.

Do not let people who share your label but invert your premises convince you that they are your allies. They are not.

### Adjacent Traditions and Philosophers

The bundle can be placed in its intellectual neighborhood by examining which traditions and thinkers are compatible with it and which are not.

Compatible traditions include Scottish Enlightenment Realism and Classical American Liberalism, represented by thinkers such as Thomas Reid, James Beattie, James Oswald, John Locke, James Wilson, Thomas Jefferson, and James Madison. Edmund Burke and Russell Kirk are compatible in substance; the form of government remains an open conversation within the bundle. Two-books Christianity, in which nature and scripture both reveal, is structurally compatible.

Several positions are incompatible at the root because they invert one or more of the five principles. Rousseau taught human perfectibility and society as the corruptor. Hegel presented history as the unfolding of an Absolute that overrides particular truths. Marx and Marxism treat outcome symmetry as the engine of justice and recast reality as material determinism carrying normative load. Critical Theory and its descendants, including Woke, DEI, CRT, and Queer Theory, treat reality as social construction, equity as goodness, universal priority as the rule, and identity-tier asymmetries dressed as procedural symmetry. American Progressivism in its current form delivers the inverted bundle through institutional capture. Postmodernism, as developed by Foucault and Derrida, treats truth, knowledge, and reality as socially constructed. Within Christianity, Scripturalism in the form associated with Crampton and recent Jesuit currents that have absorbed Critical Theory premises are incompatible, as are United Methodist and other progressive churches that have done the same. Within Islam, Ash'arite occasionalism in its strict form, where God is the direct cause of every event and causal regularity is denied, is incompatible.

Some positions are incompatible despite surface alignment. They diverge on at least one, and often several, of the principles. Thinkers such as Stephen Wolfe, Curtis Yarvin, Mencius Moldbug, Charles Haywood, and Aaron MacIntyre fall in this category. They reject procedural symmetry in favor of engineered outcomes or value hierarchies, and in several cases they redefine truth itself away from correspondence. The label right or conservative is not the test. The bundle is the test.

The cross-cutting claim is this. The bundle is the test, not the label. A truth-seeking atheist and a truth-seeking theist have more in common with each other than either has with someone who shares their label but inverts the bundle.
