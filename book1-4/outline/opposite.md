### The Five Inverted Principles (the opposite bundle)

1. **Reality is subjective, socially constructed, and malleable.**  
   Truth is not what corresponds to reality; truth is what serves the preferred narrative, power dynamics, or emotional outcome. Reason detached from empirical reality is not “hallucination”—it is liberation. Science, history, biology, and statistics are tools of oppression unless they produce the desired equity.

2. **Humans are inherently good and perfectible.**  
   Self-interest and limitation are not fundamental features of human nature; they are the corrupted products of bad systems, bad upbringing, or “oppressive” structures. With the right education, therapy, policies, and social engineering, humans can be made altruistic, unlimited in potential, and free of sin/irrationality. The noble savage (or noble victim) is the default; society is the problem.

3. **Goodness = equity, harm reduction, and deconstruction of “oppressive” structures.**  
   Human life continuing and thriving is *not* the baseline of goodness. Survival and flourishing can be subordinated (or actively opposed) if they produce unequal outcomes, “harm,” or insufficient diversity/inclusion/equity. Non-existence or reduced human footprint can be morally preferable to “oppressive” thriving. Feelings, victim status, and symbolic gestures matter more than measurable long-term outcomes.

4. **Universal priority / no legitimate tiers.**  
   All humans (or all living things) have equal moral claim regardless of relationship, citizenship, competence, or threat level. Tiered priority is selfishness or bigotry. Family, community, and nation are arbitrary or immoral constructs. Open borders, global redistribution, and “care for the furthest stranger first” are moral imperatives. Kin selection, Dunbar’s number, and enemies are denied or pathologized.

5. **Outcome symmetry (equity) and/or value symmetry.**  
   Procedural symmetry within scoped tiers is rejected as fake or insufficient. Justice demands equal *outcomes* (or equal *value*) regardless of effort, talent, choices, or reality. Any disparity is prima facie proof of injustice that must be corrected by force if necessary. Power is legitimate only when used to enforce equity; otherwise power itself is oppressive.
