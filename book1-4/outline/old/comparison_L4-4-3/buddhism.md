# Provenance regarding Buddhism
Primary sources include the Pali Canon (*Dhammapada*, *Samyutta Nikaya*, *Majjhima Nikaya*). Buddhism focuses on cessation of suffering (Dukkha) through realization of No-Self (Anatta) and Impermanence (Anicca).

## 5-Principle Compatibility Summary

| Principle | Rating | Reason |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| P1 (Reality) | **Incompatible** | "Form is like foam, feeling a bubble, perception a mirage"; goal is to see THROUGH reality, not build on it |
| P2 (Human Nature) | **Compatible** | "From craving springs grief"; mind is restless, hard to control; "by oneself is evil done"; constrained vision |
| P3 (Tiered Responsibility) | **Mixed** | "I am owner of my actions (Karma)"—individual focus; BUT "wander alone like rhinoceros" encourages detachment from social tiers |
| P4 (Goodness) | **Incompatible** | "Cessation of becoming is Nibbana"; ultimate good = ending the cycle of life/rebirth, not its continuation |
| P5 (Procedural Symmetry) | **Mixed** | Egalitarian in spirit (no caste), BUT Karma implies "Just World" where suffering is deserved from past lives |
| Humility Test | **Passes** | Kalama Sutta: "Do not go upon tradition... when you yourselves know"; "If you meet Buddha on road, kill him" (Zen) |

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential (Incompatible: Illusion)
"All conditioned things are impermanent... All conditioned things are suffering... All things are not-self." (*Dhammapada*, Verses 277-279).
"Form is like a glob of foam; feeling, a bubble; perception, a mirage." (*Samyutta Nikaya* 22.95).
"The world is swept away; it does not endure." (*Majjhima Nikaya* 82).
"This world is blinded, few here see clearly." (*Dhammapada* 174).
*(Note: The goal is to see 'through' reality, not to build upon it as a solid foundation).*

## Humans are Limited and Selfish
"From craving springs grief, from craving springs fear." (*Dhammapada* 216).
"Restless is the mind, hard to guard, hard to control." (*Dhammapada* 33).
"By oneself is evil done; by oneself is one defiled." (*Dhammapada* 165).
*(Note: Strong alignment with the 'Constrained Vision' of human psychology).*

## Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility (Incompatible: Karma)
"I am the owner of my actions (kamma), heir to my actions." (*Anguttara Nikaya* 5.57).
"If a man speaks or acts with an evil thought, pain follows him." (*Dhammapada* 1).
"Wander alone like a rhinoceros." (*Sutta Nipata* 1.3 - Ideal of Renunciation).
*(Note: Karma focuses on cosmic individual responsibility, often encouraging strict detachment from social tiers).*

## Goodness is to Live and Live Well (Incompatible: Cessation)
"The cessation of becoming is Nibbana." (*Samyutta Nikaya* 12.68).
"This is peace, this is exquisite — the resolution of all fabrications... the cooling (Nibbana)." (*Majjhima Nikaya*).
"Better it is to live one day seeing the rise and fall of things, than to live a hundred years without seeing the rise and fall of things." (*Dhammapada* 113).
*(Note: The ultimate Good is the cessation of the cycle of life/rebirth, not its continuation).*

## Procedural Symmetry (Incompatible: Value Symmetry)
"Whatever action he does, either good or evil, of that he will be the heir." (*Anguttara Nikaya* 5.57).
*(Note: While egalitarian in spirit ('No caste'), the doctrine of Karma asserts a 'Just World' fallacy where suffering is always deserved from past lives, complicating legal symmetry).*

## Humility (The Criticism Test)
"Do not go upon what has been acquired by repeated hearing; nor upon tradition... nor upon a bias towards a notion that has been pondered over." (*Kalama Sutta*).
"When you yourselves know: 'These things are bad; these things are blamable'... abandon them." (*Kalama Sutta*).
"If you meet the Buddha on the road, kill him." (Linji Yixuan - Zen Koan).

## 5-Principle Analysis

**P1 (Reality)**: **Incompatible.** "Form is like foam; feeling, a bubble; perception, a mirage." "All conditioned things are not-self." The goal of Buddhism is to see THROUGH the apparent reality to its empty nature. Reality is not denied as non-existent, but it is denied as a foundation to BUILD on. P1 requires reality as the foundation for truth and action. Buddhism sees that foundation as ultimately illusory. You cannot ground rights, property, or institutions on what is fundamentally mirage.

**P2 (Human Nature)**: Compatible. "From craving springs grief, from craving springs fear." "The mind is restless, hard to guard, hard to control." "By oneself is evil done." Buddhism has a deeply constrained vision of human psychology: we are driven by craving, aversion, and ignorance. This aligns well with P2.

**P3 (Tiered Responsibility)**: **Mixed.** "I am the owner of my actions (Karma)"—strong individual responsibility. BUT: "Wander alone like a rhinoceros" (ideal of renunciation). Buddhism encourages detachment from family and social relationships as impediments to enlightenment. The monastic ideal explicitly abandons the self → family → community gradient. While not denying worldly duties, Buddhism subordinates them to the solitary path.

**P4 (Goodness)**: **Incompatible—directly so.** P4: "Life should continue and continue well." Buddhism: "The cessation of becoming is Nibbana." "This is peace—the resolution of all fabrications, the cooling." The ULTIMATE good in Buddhism is to END the cycle of rebirth, not to continue it. These are directly opposed positions. A society cannot be GROUNDED on Buddhism because Buddhism's goal is to escape the conditions that make society necessary.

**Note**: Individual Buddhists can live within a P4-based society and contribute to flourishing. But Buddhism cannot GROUND that society—its teleology points toward cessation, not continuation.

**P5 (Procedural Symmetry)**: **Mixed.** Buddhism rejects caste—egalitarian in spirit. "Whatever action he does, good or evil, of that he will be the heir." HOWEVER, the Karma doctrine implies that current suffering is deserved from past lives. This creates a "Just World" fallacy: the poor are poor because of past-life actions. This can undermine compassion for the suffering ("they deserve it") and create friction with legal symmetry (are punishments just if Karma already punishes?).

**Humility Test**: Passes. The Kalama Sutta: "Do not go upon what has been acquired by repeated hearing; nor upon tradition... when you yourselves know: 'These things are bad'... abandon them." "If you meet the Buddha on the road, kill him"—question even the teacher. Buddhism is philosophically open to critique. It does not threaten death for questioning.
