# Provenance regarding Islam
Primary sources include *The Quran*, *Sahih Al-Bukhari* (Hadith), and Al-Ghazali's *The Incoherence of the Philosophers*. Islam emphasizes Submission to the Will of Allah and the Sacred Law (Sharia).

## Reality is Real, Knowable, and Consequential (Mixed: Occasionalism)
"Allah created the heavens and the earth in truth." (Quran 39:5).
*Al-Ghazali on Causality:* "The connection between what is habitually believed to be a cause and what is habitually believed to be an effect is not necessary... The combustion of cotton, for example, at the time of its contact with fire, may be admitted [as occurring], but that it is done *by* the fire is rejected." (*The Incoherence of the Philosophers*, Discussion 17).
"There is no agent except Allah." (Ash'arite maxim).
"Allah is the Creator of all things." (Quran 39:62).
*(Note: Occasionalism denies secondary causation, creating friction with scientific realism).*

## 5-Principle Analysis

**P1 (Reality)**: **Mixed.** Islam affirms that Allah created real heavens and earth. HOWEVER, Ash'arite Occasionalism (dominant theological school) denies secondary causation: "Fire does not burn; Allah burns at the moment of contact." If causes don't cause, then scientific investigation of reality's mechanisms is undermined. Reality exists but operates in ways that cannot be studied causally. This creates friction with P1's "knowable" requirement.

**P2 (Human Nature)**: **Mixed.** Islam acknowledges human weakness: "Mankind was created anxious." HOWEVER, the Fitra doctrine holds that children are born on innate purity—"it is his parents who make him a Jew or Christian." This contrasts with P2's claim that humans are not naturally good. If corruption is parental/societal rather than innate, then the constrained vision is weakened.

**P3 (Tiered Responsibility)**: **Incompatible.** "The believers are but brothers." "Do not take the Jews and Christians as allies." "Let not the believers take the disbelievers as allies." Responsibility is BIFURCATED: one set of duties for the Ummah (Muslim community), another for the Kafir (disbeliever). This is not tiered by proximity (self → family → community) but by RELIGIOUS IDENTITY. A Muslim in Indonesia may have stronger duties to a Muslim in Egypt than to their non-Muslim neighbor.

**P4 (Goodness)**: **Mixed.** "Fight them until... all religion is for Allah." "Whoever desires other than Islam as religion—never will it be accepted." The "Good" is defined as the establishment of Islam, not universal human flourishing. A non-Muslim's flourishing, if it conflicts with Islam's establishment, is not good by this definition. This creates friction with P4's human-centered flourishing.

**P5 (Procedural Symmetry)**: **Incompatible—structurally so.** Sharia explicitly encodes asymmetric procedures by identity:
- Jizya: Tax on non-Muslims that Muslims do not pay. Same action (living in Islamic territory), different treatment by identity.
- Testimony: Woman's testimony = half of man's. Same speech act, different evidentiary weight by sex.
- Murder: "A Muslim is not killed for killing an infidel." Same crime (killing), different penalty by religious identity of victim and perpetrator.
This is the definition of P5 violation: procedure varies by WHO you are, not WHAT you did.

**Humility Test**: **Fails.** "Whoever changes his religion, kill him." Apostasy is capital. "If anyone insults the Prophet, he must be killed." Blasphemy is capital. These are not medieval relics; they are living law in multiple Muslim-majority states. A system with capital punishment for critique cannot pass the Humility Test. Questioning Islam is structurally prohibited.
