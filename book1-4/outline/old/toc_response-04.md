**What Works Less Well / Areas for Improvement**
- **Redundancy**: "All or Nothing" appears in both Introduction and Foundations. "Enemies are real," "power is real," and tiered responsibility echo across Individual, Society, and Deeper Grounding. This dilutes impact.
- **Logical flow**: Deeper Grounding contains foundational material (systems, division of power, discovered technology, parasitism) that should live with the five principles rather than after applications. The Matrix feels tacked on at the end instead of being the practical engine of the book.
- **Under-developed or thin spots**: Human Nature is only a few bullets; it needs explicit linkage to every other principle. "Have Children" is powerful but risks reading as purely natalist without the "flourishing propagation, not raw fertility" qualifier being louder. "Unity & Exclusion" is good but could be tighter. The Conclusion is too short and lacks a strong forward-looking call.
- **Scope creep / tone risk**: A few lines ("Don’t try to make people philosophers," "leniency toward criminals is violence to the innocent") are punchy but could be softened or framed as observed consequences rather than slogans to avoid alienating readers who otherwise agree with the premises.
- **Numbering inconsistency**: The Matrix introduces a sixth filter ("What must be good?") that is really a synthesis of Goodness + Beauty + Excellence. Readers will notice; the re-write should map it explicitly back to the five principles.
- **Missing connective tissue**: The outline jumps from principles to applications without a clear "how the bundle produces emergent order" bridge. Also missing: a short "How to read this book" or "Who this is for" paragraph.

2. Make **Foundations** the longest, most rigorous chapter: define each principle, immediately follow with the Scoped Symmetry clarification (under Procedural Symmetry), and fold in the relevant Deeper Grounding material (systems, division of power, discovered technology, parasitism constraint) as "Why these principles are robust."

5. Consolidate everything that clarifies "what this is not" into one muscular Distinctions chapter (including the full comparisons section).
6. End with a tighter, more inspirational Conclusion that returns to the vector imagery and issues a direct challenge.

This structure eliminates repetition, improves narrative momentum, and makes the Matrix the practical heart of the book rather than an afterthought.

**RE-WRITTEN OUTLINE**  
**Book Title Suggestion**: *Anchored: Five Principles for Truth, Responsibility, and Human Flourishing* (or *The Bundle: Reality, Limits, Priority, Goodness, and Symmetry*)

### Chapter 1: Introduction – Premises Before Conclusions
- To be productive in society, we must discuss the premise, not conclusions.
- Once we understand each other’s premises—including where we believe we should be going—we can talk about layers on top of that.
- Core premises that fundamentally divide societies:
  - Is there truth and can we know it?
  - Are humans selfish/limited or good/changeable?
  - Should human life continue, should human life thrive?
  - What should be prioritized?
  - What is justice in society? Outcome symmetry? Procedural symmetry? Value symmetry?
- These premises appear in religion, politics, and culture.
- Here we introduce five principles that work together and provide a specific, consistent set of premises.
  - Everyone either explicitly or implicitly operates with some set of premises.
  - We recommend premises that benefit yourself, benefit others, and align with reality.
- To retain meaning, all five principles must be active simultaneously and interpreted consistently with one another.
- A principle must never be applied in isolation.
- These principles function as a **filter**, not a blueprint.
  - This is more political than purely philosophical.
  - It does not replace or subsume religion or atheism.
  - It offers a positive vision for defining systems that can work together and, by doing so, clarifies what cannot work with these principles.
  - It is not a system of virtue, honor, or morality; it is a practical guide for how to live.
  - The possible parameter space inside these principles is broad yet still bounded.
- **The Bundle**: All five principles must be held together. Removing or distorting any one collapses the entire structure (detailed in Chapter 2).

### Chapter 2: The Five Principles – The Foundation
**Opening**: Reality is real, knowable, and consequential. These five principles are not arbitrary; each is required for the others to function. Together they form a self-reinforcing bundle.

**Principle 1: Reality & Truth**  
Reality is real, knowable, and consequential. Truth is what corresponds to reality.  
- Reason is not enough by itself. Reason that is not grounded is hallucination or imagination. Reason can guide ideas; reality has the final say.  
- Goodness that includes survival is completely linked to reality being real.

**Principle 2: Human Nature – Limited and Self-Interested**  
Humans are limited and self-interested.  
- This nature is **productive when properly ordered**.  
- Do not try to make people angels.  
- Do not try to make people philosophers.  
- Understand that people will make irrational decisions.  
- Because humans are limited, power must be scoped; no one can exercise symmetric concern across all of humanity.  
- Systems enable faulty humans to achieve things beyond their individual capacity (see "The Importance of Systems" below).

**Principle 3: Tiered Self-Anchored Priority (Tiered Priority)**  
Prioritize yourself and family, then neighbors, then community, then nation, then world.  
- Do not drop any tiers.  
- Enemies are real and have negative value.  
- Kin selection and Dunbar’s Number describe real boundaries of human concern and cooperation.  
- This is not selfishness; it is the only way limited beings can actually discharge responsibility.

**Principle 4: Goodness – Surviving and Flourishing**  
Goodness must include humans living, and living well.  
- Only what survives and thrives can be good in the future.  
- We must allow failure in order for goodness to survive and thrive.  
- Life, not nihilism: propagation of a full life (joy, meaning, excellence), not merely raw numbers.  
- Outcomes are more important than feelings. Feeling good about something is not a reason to do it; proof of good outcomes is.

**Principle 5: Procedural Symmetry**  
Equal procedures applied to all **within a defined scope**.  
- This is what seeking truth looks like at scale.  
- Procedural Symmetry is **scoped** — a tier, a role, a jurisdiction. The same rules apply to everyone inside that scope.  
- It is **not** outcome symmetry (equity). It is **not** value symmetry.  
- **Scoped Symmetry in practice** (expanded clarification):  
  - Family tier: All your children receive equal parental treatment (no favorites). You owe your children more than your neighbor’s children — this is correct tiered priority, not unequal treatment.  
  - Citizen tier: All citizens subject to the same criminal law and procedural rights. Citizens receive citizen treatment (safety nets, voting); non-citizens do not. Borders do their job.  
  - Role-scoped: Judges owe all defendants the same due process. Teachers owe all students the same grading standards. Power asymmetries (judge > defendant, parent > child) are legitimate when they serve tiered responsibility **and** are bounded by procedural symmetry within the role.  
  - Between nations: Symmetry does not apply the same way across borders. International law is not real law; military might is the ultimate arbiter. This is recognition of scope, not failure of symmetry.  
- **Parent–child relationship** (clearest case): Parent has tiered priority (children before strangers), legitimate power over children, and owes each child symmetric procedural treatment (equal concern, no arbitrary favoritism). Power is temporary, accountable, and justified by responsibility. This pattern holds for all legitimate power: it serves tiered responsibility and is bounded by procedural symmetry within its scope.  
- Principle 4 without 5 = tribalism. Principle 5 without 4 = universalist collapse. Together they produce legitimate, bounded, fair order.

**The All-or-Nothing Bundle**  
All five principles must operate together. Removing or distorting one distorts them all.  
- Examples of distortion: outcome symmetry alone produces corruption and new ruling classes; universal procedural symmetry without tiers produces neglect of one’s own; truth without limits produces impossible demands on human nature.  
- The bundle is a filter, not a rigid blueprint. It defines a broad but bounded parameter space for healthy individuals and societies.

**Why These Principles Are Robust (Deeper Grounding folded in)**  
- **The Importance of Systems**: Humans are limited, selfish, often irrational. Systems enable us to achieve more than we could individually. Science (as a method, not an institution) separates irrational ideas through demonstration over time and place. Government (properly bounded) prevents individual selfishness from governing. Writing in a diary is a system; markets and currency are emergent discovered technologies.  
- **Discovered vs. Constructed**: Money, language, and the need for stable communication are discovered necessities. The specific form (shells, paper, ledger) is constructed.  
- **Division of Power**: Reality is complex; humans are small. Specialization is required. Parents, teachers, judges, CEOs, and statesmen exercise legitimate authority within scoped roles. Power is real and necessary; the lesson of abuse is not "power is evil" but "the system was insufficient or the person corrupt." We must be able to limit damage and remove people from power.  
- **Constrain Parasitism**: Any framework must value what builds society. Ideological parasites (ideologies that produce no children and must convert or indoctrinate to survive), social parasites (genuinely lazy/unproductive who live off others), and technological parasites (who reject rigor yet use the technology others created) are fundamentally incompatible with a culture that wishes to continue.  
- The principles are intentionally broad: use them to judge past and present societies. Better and worse ways of living exist, but alignment with the bundle allows improvement and continuation.

### Chapter 3: The Precepts Matrix – Your Daily Operating System
**How to use it**: When facing any decision, run the situation through all six filters **in order**. All must align. If any fails or feels forced, pause, adjust, or reject. This forces the entire bundle to work together.

**Quick mental version (memorize)**:  
1. Survival filter? (Will this propagate *flourishing* life?)  
2. Reality? (Facts, not wishes)  
3. Limits? (Oxygen mask — self first)  
4. Tiered responsibility? (Core before outer rings)  
5. What must be good? (Excellence, beauty, joy, ownership, children?)  
6. Symmetry? (Would I accept this if roles reversed?)

**Full scenarios with counter-examples** (all retained verbatim from original, with minor tightening for flow):  
- Scenario 1: Cleaning and organizing your home  
- Scenario 2: Deciding whether to have (more) children  
- Scenario 3: Forgiving someone who betrayed you  
- Scenario 4: Major career opportunity requiring heavy travel or family sacrifice  
- Scenario 5: Encountering the political claim “This country/land is stolen”

Run the matrix often. When all six align, you are living the bundle. When they do not, you are choosing a path that ultimately fails to survive.

### Chapter 4: The Individual – Personal Application
Action begins with you. Integrate these directly into your life.

- **Align with Truth**: Reality & Survival.  
- **Seek Excellence**: Survival and living well demand quality.  
- **Admire and Create Beauty**: Platforms for personal and social joy and propagation. Beauty sits at the conjunction of order, truth, and vitality. Order provides intelligible structure. Truth ensures correspondence to reality (even fiction can be beautiful when it distills). Vitality is the life in it. Missing any one destroys beauty.  
- **Take Responsibility**: Responsibility as ownership. Ownership as creating and maintaining. Society specializes. Sacrifice according to tiered limits. Make judgments.  
- **Love and Forgiveness**:  
  - Dispositional Love and Action-based Love.  
  - Forgiveness as Personal Release (avoid becoming bitter).  
  - Forgiveness as Social Reconciliation (requires accountability, truth, demonstrated change).  
  - Love is holding on; Forgiveness is letting go. Both are useful for long-term relationships.  
- **Have Children**: Will to live generationally. Generational Symmetry derived from Goodness (surviving) and Procedural Symmetry. Not having children is failure to survive generationally — an end to everyone who came before you. Joy, Love, Meaning (flourishing propagation, not raw fertility). Do not have one micromanaged child; have more. Facilitate and push for thriving, but allow failure. Only by allowing failure can a child truly succeed.

### Chapter 5: Society – Structuring Institutions and Norms
- **Rule of Law**: Truth + Procedural Symmetry. All subject to the same law. Does **not** mean rule by bureaucrats. The law exists only as far as it is enforced. Unequal enforcement is de-facto unequal law. Humans are limited: law must be knowable — not too large, not too complicated. Leniency toward criminals is violence to the innocent. It is clear that removing the small percent who are violent can change the nature and course of a country.  
- **Freedom of Speech**: To align with truth, society must discuss ideas. Without discussion, society cannot correct errors or limit flourishing. Humans are limited.  
- **Borders and Common Defense**: Borders allow safety nets for citizens, limit abuse (procedural symmetry + tiered priority). Borders protect against enemies. Allow different laws and customs without universal acceptance. No such thing as international law; the only real international law is military might.  
- **Power Is Real and Necessary**: Those in power must wield real power to enforce law, defend borders, and maintain order. Power is good when bounded by the same law as everyone else.  
- **Unity & Exclusion**: Unity implies exclusion. Unity can be good if the ideas and practices are good. Unity enables bigger things than an individual can achieve. Exclusion is not a weapon but recognition of truly fundamental differences when required. Survival ultimately requires force. Enemies are real at both individual and nation level. Rule of law (procedural symmetry) drastically reduces the need for blunt force.  
- **Ownership is Real and Necessary**: Responsibility cannot exist outside ownership. Markets and currency are not a philosophy; they are emergent from specialization. Failure must be allowed for true ownership.  
- **Education**: Teach new generations the skills to replicate and improve technology. Society itself is a form of technology — do not lie about its structure or basis. Teach truth, beauty, and excellence. Humans are limited and selfish.

### Chapter 6: Distinctions – What This Framework Is and Is Not
**Core Distinctions**  
- Procedural Symmetry, not Outcome Symmetry. In critical theory, outcome symmetry is called equity. Outcome symmetry breeds corruption (humans vary in talent/effort/honesty; central rules cannot capture local realities; people respond to incentives). It entrenches classism (someone must pay; 80/20 rule persists; new ruling class emerges; corruption becomes class membership test; mobility suppressed, class permanent).  
- Procedural Symmetry, not Value Symmetry.  
- Strength, Not Weakness. There is no "right to exist."  
- Responsibility, Not Sacrifice. Responsibility can include sacrifice (especially inner tiers), but sacrifice by itself is waste and not a virtue. Responsibility, not unbounded action love.  
- Outcomes are more important than feelings.  
- Discovered vs. Constructed (already covered in Foundations).  
- The "Compassionate Delusion" (name early and often): Crime and social problems seen as education/childhood/social-ill problems. Lenient judges, open-borders advocates, "defund the police" types. Primary tool of outcome symmetry. Creates victims of innocents.  
- Not virtues: Tolerance (logically insane as fundamental virtue — least tolerant thrive; game theory confirms). Diversity (different people/opinions can be healthy but is not a virtue).  
- Kindness vs. Politeness: Kindness within family (form of love). Politeness in society. Not kindness as societal norm or policy (subsidizes irresponsibility/criminality; rewards predation; selects against excellence, responsibility, survival). In large/open systems the least-kind strategy eventually dominates.  
- Strategic Adversaries weaponize your own morals against you. They invoke children/the weak because they know you value them, even while offering them up to be hurt. Reject any such call. Procedural Symmetry operates in tiers; enemies will demand interior-tier compassion be extended to them. It is your duty to refuse. Words as weapons must be recognized.  
- Life, Not Nihilism: Propagation of a full life, not just numbers.

Also incompatible: Stephen Wolfe, Curtis Yarvin/Mencius Moldbug, Charles Haywood, Aaron MacIntyre (diverge on at least one principle, usually several; reject procedural symmetry for engineered outcomes or value hierarchies; redefine truth).  
- Atheism: Atheism says less about beliefs than theism. Some atheists replace God with state/ideology. Some care only about themselves; some prioritize distant strangers over neighbors; some believe humans should disappear. Some seek truth. Advice to theists: do not paint all atheists the same. Advice to atheists: ground yourself and your family in explicit principles that lead to human life and flourishing, or you/children will bend to those around you. These five principles can serve as a bulwark and allow cooperation with truth-seeking theists while drawing a clear line against those who work against truth and reality.
