Exactly. The core premise that we argue over are simple and divide everything:

* Is there truth and can we know it?
* Are humans selfish/limited or good/changeable?
* Should human life continue, should human life thrive?
* What should be prioritized?
* What is justice in society? Outcome symmetry? Procedural symmetry? Value Symmetry?

These questions stretch over religion, politics, and society.

The problem with US constitution first amendment clause of religion, is religion had different views, but on these fundamental questions, they were largely aligned and compatible with the US constitution. However in the wider world, not all religions or belief systems will answer these questions in the same way, and more importantly, answer them in a way incompatible with the US constitution. So on one hand, they claim freedom because they invoke some deity. On the other hand, they undermine the very thing that allows them.

On this note, tolerance is not a virtue, but goodness and truth is.
