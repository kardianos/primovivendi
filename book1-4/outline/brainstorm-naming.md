Brainstorm: Book Title & Name for the 5 Principles

---

## PART 1: WHAT THE NAME NEEDS TO DO

The name must accomplish several things simultaneously:
1. Signal that this is a complete, interlocking system (not a menu)
2. Capture the two poles: hard realism (reality, limits, survival) and ordered process (symmetry, tiers, systems)
3. Be memorable and repeatable ("the five ___")
4. Not sound like an existing school of thought it isn't (not "libertarianism," not "conservatism," not "Stoicism")
5. Work as both a book title and a principle-set name

---

## PART 2: CANDIDATES FOR THE COLLECTIVE NAME

### Already in use in the materials:
- "Procedural Realism" — from differences.md. Strong. Captures both poles. Risk: sounds academic/narrow. A casual reader might think it's about court procedures.
- "The Bundle" — used colloquially in resp chains. Too informal for a book title. Useful as shorthand.
- "Five Indivisible Principles of Ordered Liberty and Human Flourishing" — current subtitle. Accurate but mouthful. Not memorable.

### Strongest candidates:

**Procedural Realism**
- Pros: Already established in your materials. Two-word. Captures the core: procedure (symmetry, tiers, systems) + realism (reality, limits, survival). A reader can immediately grasp "this is about grounded, honest process."
- Cons: "Procedural" sounds legalistic to some ears. May undersell the survival/thriving/family/children dimension.
- Verdict: Best option currently available. Could be the framework name while the book title is different.

**Correspondence Order**
- "Correspondence" ties to truth (correspondence theory of truth). "Order" ties to the structured nature: tiers, symmetry, hierarchy, systems.
- Pros: Philosophically precise. Novel. Not confused with anything else.
- Cons: Abstract. Won't resonate with non-philosophers.

**Survival Realism**
- Pros: Bold. Immediate. Ties to the root axiom (what survives propagates).
- Cons: Might sound like prepper culture. Loses the procedural/symmetry dimension.

**Ordered Realism**
- Pros: Clean. "Ordered" captures tiers, symmetry, hierarchy, systems. "Realism" captures truth, limits, survival.
- Cons: "Ordered" is passive. Doesn't convey the active, survival-oriented nature.

**Grounded Order**
- Pros: "Grounded" = reality-anchored. "Order" = structured, not chaotic.
- Cons: Sounds like a landscaping company.

**The Interlock**
- Pros: Captures the key property: all five must hold together. Memorable. "The Five-Point Interlock" has a mechanical/engineering feel that matches the systems-thinking approach.
- Cons: Doesn't describe WHAT it is, only HOW it works.

### Dark horses worth considering:

**Hard Symmetry**
- "Hard" = realist, non-utopian, grounded in human limits and survival.
- "Symmetry" = the core political/social mechanism.
- Pros: Punchy. Memorable. Two words. Novel. "Hard" signals: this isn't soft, this isn't wishful.
- Cons: Could sound aggressive or mathematical. "Hard" might confuse — hard as in difficult? hard as in hard science?

**Bounded Flourishing**
- "Bounded" = limited humans, tiered responsibility, procedural constraints.
- "Flourishing" = surviving well, beauty, excellence, children.
- Pros: Captures the tension that defines the framework. Flourishing that is bounded (not utopian, not unbounded).
- Cons: "Flourishing" is a word already claimed by positive psychology and Aristotelian virtue ethics. Might create wrong associations.

**Rooted Symmetry**
- "Rooted" = in reality, in survival, in human nature, in self-anchored tiers.
- "Symmetry" = the procedural core.
- Pros: Evocative. "Rooted" suggests depth, groundedness, organic growth from real soil.
- Cons: Slightly soft-sounding. "Rooted" could be read as merely traditionalist.

---

## PART 3: RECOMMENDATION

For the **principle-set name**: keep **Procedural Realism**. It's already in your materials, it's precise, and it accurately describes the system. Use it consistently.

For a **book title**, the name needs to work differently — it needs to intrigue, provoke, or orient. Some options:

- *Procedural Realism: Five Principles for Ordered Liberty*
- *The Hard Symmetry: Five Principles That Stand or Fall Together*
- *Five Principles: A Framework for Those Who Would Rather Live Than Argue*
- *The Interlock: Reality, Survival, and the Five Principles That Cannot Be Separated*

I'd lean toward **Procedural Realism** as the framework name (keep using it) and something punchier for the actual book title. The book title should make someone pick it up. "Procedural Realism" is what they call it after they've read it.

## PART 4: INDIVIDUAL PRINCIPLE NAMES — MINOR TWEAKS

The current names are mostly strong. One observation:

Principle 1: "Reality & Truth" — good.
Principle 2: "Human Nature: limited, self-interested" — good, descriptive.
Principle 3: "Goodness — Surviving and Flourishing" — good.
Principle 4: "Tiered Self-Anchored Priority" (toc.md) / "Tiered Self-Anchored Responsibility" (differences.md, pt-01.md) — the name is unstable across documents. Needs to settle. This is addressed in the companion file.
Principle 5: "Procedural Symmetry" — excellent. The best name of the five. Memorable, precise, novel.

The interlock/all-or-nothing property could be named: **The Interlock** or **The Bundle Condition** or **Indivisibility**. "The Interlock" is the strongest — it's mechanical, precise, and conveys that removing one piece collapses the structure.
